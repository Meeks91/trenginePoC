In the fast-paced world of fitness, TikTok is where the coolest workout trends emerge and spread rapidly. As a [personal trainer](https://www.trainerize.com/independent-trainers/), staying updated with these trends can give you a competitive edge. Keeping up with these trends not only can help to spice up your clients’ routines, but they can also give you a leg-up in attracting new ones! 
Let’s dive into four TikTok fitness trends you should explore and see how they can boost your business.
## **Top TikTok Fitness Trends in 2024**
### **#1: Dead hangs**
One of the most popular fitness trends on Tiktok is the dead hang, a simple yet effective exercise that involves hanging from a horizontal bar with a relaxed body. This exercise, with over 138 million views on Tiktok is used for its benefits in decompressing the spine and shoulders.

**Benefits for clients:**
  * **Enhanced posture:** Dead hangs counteract the effects of prolonged screen time and poor posture by elongating the spine and opening up the shoulder joints.
  * **Improved shoulder health:** The exercise gently stretches and decompresses the muscles around the shoulders, alleviating tension and improving flexibility.


**How personal trainers can use this trend**
Introduce dead hangs as part of your clients’ warm-up or cool-down routines. Start with shorter durations of 20-30 seconds and gradually increase as your clients build strength. Highlight the posture and shoulder health benefits to clients who spend long hours at desks.
### **#2: Reformer pilates**
Reformer Pilates has gained significant attention on TikTok. This variation of classic Pilates uses a reformer machine to provide a comprehensive full-body workout, engaging muscles through controlled resistance.

**Benefits for clients:**
  * **Lean muscle development:** Reformer Pilates helps build lean muscle through resistance training without heavy weights.
  * **Low-impact:** Ideal for clients of all fitness levels, it minimizes injury risks while delivering effective results.


**How personal trainers can use this trend**
Consider offering Reformer Pilates sessions or collaborating with a certified instructor. Promote these sessions to clients looking for low-impact, full-body workouts. Emphasize the strength and toning benefits, especially for those cautious of high-impact exercises.
### **#3: 12-3-30 Workout**
The 12-3-30 workout, created by TikTok influencer Lauren Giraldo, involves walking on a treadmill at a 12% incline and 3 miles per hour for 30 minutes. This simple yet effective routine has gained widespread popularity due to its accessibility and effectiveness.
**Benefits for clients:**
  * **Joint-friendly:** As a low-intensity exercise, it’s gentle on the joints, making it suitable for clients with joint issues or those recovering from injuries.
  * **Bone density:** Helps strengthen muscles and bones without excessive strain, reducing the risk of osteoporosis.


**How personal trainers can use this trend**
Incorporate the 12-3-30 workout into your cardio training programs. It’s perfect for clients who prefer a low-impact, easy-to-follow routine. Highlight its joint-friendly nature and accessibility, making it appealing to a broad client base.
### **#4: Shy girl workouts**
This trend has gained growing traction on TikTok in recent months, with over 200 million views under the hashtag #ShyGirlWorkout. Shy girl workouts cater to individuals who feel self-conscious in crowded gym spaces. These routines require minimal equipment and can be performed discreetly, either at home or in a quiet corner of the gym.

  * **Increased confidence:** Helps clients overcome gymtimidation, building confidence and consistency in their fitness routines.
  * **Accessibility:** Minimal equipment needed makes these workouts easy to perform anywhere.


**How personal trainers can use this trend**
Develop personalized shy girl workout plans for clients who feel anxious in public gyms. Include a mix of dumbbell exercises, bodyweight movements, and mobility drills. Provide tips on finding quiet spots in the gym and using headphones to stay focused.

#### **Educate your clients**
  * Keep your clients informed about the benefits and proper techniques of these exercises through blogs, newsletters, or [social media](https://www.trainerize.com/blog/fitness-social-media/) posts.
