By [Fadeke Adegbuyi](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/users/fadegbuyi)

Artificial intelligence is an increasingly important topic of discussion, as its applications continue to expand across various industries—from [healthcare](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-in-healthcare) and [e-commerce](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-ecommerce) to [education](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-in-education) and [game development](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-game-development). Whether you’re a business owner looking to leverage [AI business tools](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/article/ai-tools-in-business) to improve your operations or an individual curious about how this technology is shaping our future, AI podcasts can help you stay up to date. From enthusiastic advocates to critical voices, these podcasts offer various perspectives, ensuring you receive a well-rounded understanding of the AI landscape.
If you’re not paying attention, you risk missing out on crucial AI and ML developments that could affect you, professionally or personally. [Google’s Project Mariner](https://deepmind.google/models/project-mariner/) AI agent can now autonomously browse the web and complete up to 10 tasks simultaneously, from purchasing tickets to managing online shopping, fundamentally changing how users interact with websites. [Microsoft’s MatterGen AI system](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/blog/mattergen-a-new-paradigm-of-materials-design-with-generative-ai/) uses a diffusion model to directly generate novel materials with specified properties like bulk modulus or magnetic density, enabling a paradigm shift from screening existing materials to creating entirely new ones for applications like batteries and solar cells. By listening to AI podcasts, you can stay informed about these developments and gain valuable insights into the opportunities and challenges they present for you and your business. Read on for a curated list of AI podcasts that provide you with the knowledge you need to navigate the world of artificial intelligence.
Key takeaways:
  * AI-focused podcasts are an accessible way to learn about artificial intelligence developments, featuring interviews with experts, discussions of breakthrough research, and explorations of AI’s impact on society and industry.
  * The top 15 AI podcasts cover a range of content—from technical deep dives that demystify algorithms to broader conversations about ethics, careers in AI, and future trends—catering to both beginners and seasoned professionals.
  * Regularly listening to these podcasts can boost your understanding by providing nuanced insights and real-world perspectives that textbooks or online courses might not offer, fitting AI learning into your daily routine (like commutes or workouts) in an engaging format.


##  [15 AI podcasts you should listen to](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#15-ai-podcasts-you-should-listen-to)
From in-depth discussions on the latest breakthroughs to thought-provoking conversations about the ethical implications of AI, these fifteen podcasts cover a wide range of topics that will keep you informed.
###  [1. How I AI](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#1-how-i-ai)
In How I AI, host [Claire Vo](https://x.com/clairevo?lang=en), a three-time chief product officer, engineer, and founder, delivers the practical AI education you’ve been searching for. Unlike podcasts focused on philosophical debates about AI’s future, this show is designed for immediate action—each episode features live demonstrations of specific AI workflows that guests use to transform their daily work. You’ll get live screen sharing and step-by-step processes you can copy and implement immediately. Whether you’re building products, leading teams, or starting a company, Claire’s carefully curated guests demonstrate exactly how they’ve integrated AI into their craft with real-world examples and workflows you can start using today.
Recent guests include [Hilary Gridley](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xDMkkOC-EhI) (Head of Core Product at Whoop) demonstrating custom GPTs for management scaling, Joel Unger showing how AI transforms design workflows at Atlassian with 10x faster prototyping in Cursor, Cody De Arkland creating 3D multiplayer games in 15 minutes with no game dev experience, and Sahil Lavingia (Gumroad CEO) achieving 40x productivity improvements using v0, Cursor, and Devin.
Where to listen:
  * [Listen on Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/4aRP2XSavdtrLG5FZoonOK)
  * [Listen on Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/how-i-ai/id1809663079)
  * [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@howiaipodcast)


###  [2. The AI Daily Brief](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#2-the-ai-daily-brief)
The AI Daily Brief (formerly The AI Breakdown) serves as your essential daily briefing on the most significant developments in artificial intelligence. Host [Nathaniel Whittemore](https://x.com/nlw?lang=en) cuts through the noise to deliver analytical depth on the AI revolution unfolding around us, examining everything from creative breakthroughs with tools like Midjourney and ChatGPT to the fundamental questions of AGI development, alignment, and existential risk. What sets this podcast apart is its commitment to exploring AI’s implications across multiple dimensions—technological, philosophical, ethical, and practical. Rather than simply reporting AI news, Whittemore provides the analytical framework to understand why developments matter for the future of work, creativity, and human civilization itself.
Recent episodes tackle the industry’s most pressing topics: “The Push to Productize AI” analyzes the race to commercialize AI capabilities, “Here Comes the Internet of Agents” explores the shift toward autonomous AI systems, and “The Five Vectors of AI Competition” breaks down how major tech companies are positioning themselves in the AI arms race. Whittemore also brings unique perspectives through conversations with cultural icons like legendary music producer Rick Rubin and covers major industry moves such as legendary iPhone designer Jony Ive joining OpenAI.
Where to listen:
  * [Listen on Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/7gKwwMLFLc6RmjmRpbMtEO)
  * [Listen on Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-ai-daily-brief-formerly-the-ai-breakdown/id1680633614)


###  [3. High Agency](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#3-high-agency)
For developers and entrepreneurs building with [LLMs](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/large-language-models) and [generative AI](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/generative-ai), High Agency offers the insider knowledge you need to succeed in production. Host [Raza Habib](https://x.com/RazRazcle) connects you directly with leaders at companies on the AI frontier who have already shipped successful AI products, sharing their hard-won stories, lessons, and playbooks to help you build faster and with greater confidence. Recognizing that AI moves incredibly fast and expertise is still emerging, High Agency creates a community for builders who learn by doing. Each weekly episode delivers actionable intelligence from practitioners who’ve solved the problems you’re likely to face, whether you’re integrating AI into existing workflows, building AI-native applications, or scaling AI systems for enterprise use.
The podcast tackles the real-world challenges of AI development through conversations covering code review automation, multimodal AI applications, rapid AI agent development, clinical trial optimization in healthcare, AI-powered recruiting systems, and next-generation search technologies. Guests reveal how they’ve navigated everything from technical architecture decisions to scaling challenges, offering practical insights on transforming AI research into profitable products.
Where to listen:

###  [4. No Priors](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#4-no-priors)

The podcast features engaging discussions with leading AI engineers, researchers, and founders, addressing critical questions about the future of AI and its impact on various aspects of life and business.
By tuning in, you’ll gain valuable insights into the latest developments in AI research, the potential disruptions in different markets, and the ways in which AI may transform commerce, culture, and society. With their extensive experience in the tech industry and startup world, Gil and Guo provide a unique and informed perspective on the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.

###  [5. AI & I](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#5-ai-i)
In _AI & I_, host [Dan Shipper](https://x.com/danshipper), co-founder and CEO of Every, a daily newsletter focused on technology, productivity, and AI, explores how the smartest people in the world are using AI to improve their thinking, creativity, and relationships. Each week, Shipper interviews various guests, including founders, filmmakers, writers, and investors, to learn how they incorporate AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Midjourney into their work and daily lives. He’s interviewed venture partner Sarah Tavel on the next wave of social AI and its implications for discovery and sharing, WIRED co-founder Kevin Kelly on predicting the future through decades of technological evolution, and Microsoft CTO Kevin Scott on the future of programming and AI agents in software engineering.
Through screen-sharing historical chats and live experimentation with AI during the show, listeners gain insights into the practical applications of these tools and how they transform how we perceive and interact with the world around us. Tune in to discover innovative ways to leverage AI in your own projects and gain a deeper understanding of its impact on our personal and professional lives.

##  [AI podcasts FAQ](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-podcasts#ai-podcasts-faq)
**Which AI podcasts are best for learning practical implementation strategies?**
“How I AI” focuses on practical implementation with live demonstrations and step-by-step workflows you can copy immediately, while “High Agency” focuses specifically on helping developers and entrepreneurs build successful AI products in production. Both shows feature real practitioners sharing actionable insights rather than theoretical discussions.
**What are the main benefits of listening to AI podcasts regularly?**
AI podcasts help you stay current with developments across artificial intelligence and machine learning while providing practical insights you can apply immediately to your work or business. They offer access to expert knowledge from leading researchers, entrepreneurs, and practitioners who share real-world experiences, case studies, and implementation strategies in a digestible way.
**Can AI podcasts help me advance my career or business?**
Yes, AI podcasts provide valuable knowledge that can give you a competitive edge, whether you’re learning to integrate AI tools into your workflow, understanding market opportunities, or staying ahead of industry disruptions. Many episodes feature actionable advice, technical tutorials, and strategic insights that listeners can directly apply to improve their productivity, decision-making, and professional development.
