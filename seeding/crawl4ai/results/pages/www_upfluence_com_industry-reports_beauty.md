# Introduction to the Beauty industry
The beauty industry is in a period of fast growth, predicted to [grow from $420B in 2018 to $716.3B by 2025](https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/cosmetics-marketing-industry-trends?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic). The US comes second in terms of cosmetic industry market share with [24%](https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/cosmetics-marketing-industry-trends?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic), against the Asia Pacific Region with [48%](https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/cosmetics-marketing-industry-trends?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic). In general, US shoppers still prefer to buy their cosmetics in-store at pharmacies or the likes of Walmart and Target. However, newer beauty brands are seeing high sales in eCommerce, with [86%](https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/cosmetics-marketing-industry-trends?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic) of their sales happening online, versus only [4%](https://commonthreadco.com/blogs/coachs-corner/cosmetics-marketing-industry-trends?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic) of online sales for the top 20 beauty manufacturers. Beauty subscription services are also becoming popular, with [28% of male and 22% of female](https://eu.usatoday.com/story/money/2019/03/04/age-amazon-services-like-birchbox-grow-popularity/3018289002/?utm_source=organic&utm_campaign=direct_beauty_brands&utm_medium=organic) American consumers currently receiving a subscription box service.
