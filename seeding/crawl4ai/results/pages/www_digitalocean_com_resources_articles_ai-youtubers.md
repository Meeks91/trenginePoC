Artificial intelligence and machine learning education aren’t limited to textbooks or university labs. YouTube has become the go-to classroom for millions eager to learn these skills in real time with new developments. From coding tutorials and model walkthroughs to AI ethics discussions and research paper breakdowns, today’s creators are helping make complex topics more accessible to everyone.
Whether you’re a beginner exploring [neural networks](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/conceptual-articles/neural-network-guide-step-by-step) or a developer [fine-tuning](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/fine-tuning) LLMs, our picks for the top educational AI YouTubers teaching machine learning in 2025 share clear explanations, hands-on projects, and up-to-date insights in the AI industry.
💡**Key takeaways:**
  * YouTube has become a leading platform for AI and ML education, where YouTube channels like Two Minute Papers, [DeepLearning.AI](http://DeepLearning.AI), AI Explained, Tina Huang, The AI Advantage, How I AI, DigitalOcean, Skill Leap AI, Wes Roth, Dwarkesh Patel, Siraj Raval, Matt Wolfe, AI Foundations, and Alex Finn simplify complex AI topics through hands-on tutorials, research explainers, and practical projects.
  * Each AI YouTube channel offers a unique learning approach, from research breakdowns and coding walkthroughs to automation tutorials and real-world applications, which makes AI accessible for students, developers, and business professionals alike.
  * Extending beyond AI tutorials, these YouTubers have built active communities through newsletters, forums, and interactive groups where learners can exchange ideas, share projects, and grow together in their AI journey.

##  [AI YouTubers FAQ](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-youtubers#ai-youtubers-faq)
**Who are the best AI YouTubers to follow in 2025?** Some of the best AI YouTube channels include _Two Minute Papers,[DeepLearning.AI](http://DeepLearning.AI), AI Explained, Tina Huang, The AI Advantage, How I AI, DigitalOcean, Skill Leap AI, Wes Roth, Dwarkesh Patel, Siraj Raval, Matt Wolfe, AI Foundations, and Alex Finn._ They cover everything from research analysis and tutorials to coding, AI tools, and real-world applications.
**Can you learn machine learning exclusively from YouTube?** YouTube is an excellent starting point for getting an education on machine learning. It offers free access to tutorials, coding walkthroughs, and project-based lessons from experienced educators. However, mastering ML requires applying concepts through hands-on projects, reading research papers, and exploring structured courses or textbooks for deeper mathematical understanding. YouTube can be your launchpad, but consistent practice, staying updated, and applying what you have learned are key to mastering the field.
**Which AI YouTuber explains research papers best?** Two Minute Papers excels at turning complex research into easy-to-grasp summaries and offers detailed technical breakdowns for advanced learners.
**What’s the best YouTube channel for AI beginners?** [DeepLearning.AI](http://DeepLearning.AI) and The AI Advantage are excellent for beginners, thanks to their step-by-step tutorials, structured content, and focus on real-world applications.
**Which YouTuber is best for ML coding tutorials in Python?**
Siraj Raval is known for energetic, project-based tutorials that simplify AI and ML coding in [Python](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/tutorials/python-tutorial).
**Do AI YouTubers cover LLMs and generative AI?** Yes. Many channels focus on the LLMs and generative AI tools, including AI Explained, Matt Wolfe, and Alex Finn, who showcase updates, use cases, and tutorials.
**How often do AI YouTubers post new content?** Most top AI YouTube creators post weekly or bi-weekly, balancing tutorials, AI news, and hands-on projects to keep content timely and engaging.
**Which AI YouTubers should developers follow in 2025?** Developers can follow AI Foundations, Skill Leap AI, DigitalOcean, The AI Advantage, and Alex Finn for practical workflows, automation tutorials, and guidance on building real AI-powered applications.
