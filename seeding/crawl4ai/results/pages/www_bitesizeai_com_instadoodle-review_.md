# InstaDoodle Review: A Mixed Bag of Doodle Animation
_The post contains advertisements through affiliate links._
In the ever-evolving world of digital content creation, new tools pop up regularly, promising to revolutionize how we make engaging visuals. 
InstaDoodle, a recent entrant in the doodle animation space, caught my eye through a Facebook ad. 
Intrigued by its promise of easy-to-create animated doodles, I decided to give it a spin. Here’s my honest review of InstaDoodle, covering the good, the bad, and the not-so-pretty.
Table of contents
InstaDoodle shines in its simplicity. The interface is intuitive, allowing even novices to create decent-looking doodle animations without a steep learning curve. 
For $37 (via a special offer), you get lifetime access to the basic version, which seems reasonable for what it offers.
The software provides a variety of characters and props, and you can even upload your own images or use AI to generate custom elements. 
The animation effect, particularly for images, is quite satisfying, giving that hand-drawn feel that’s so popular in explainer videos. It’s not perfect, but good enough given how easy they are to make.
While functional, InstaDoodle has its quirks. The AI image generation, while interesting, is slow and not always practical. 
Some generations are decent, others are non-usable. It is hit and miss, which makes the credit system quite expensive in the end.
I found it faster to create images in other tools and import them. Text animations can look awkward at slower speeds, and managing multiple objects on a slide can be fiddly.
What really raised my eyebrows were the aggressive upsells. Before you even try the product, they push a $67 “pro” version. 
It feels a bit like buying a car and being asked to upgrade the engine before you’ve even sat in the driver’s seat.
More concerning is the lack of company information on their website. In an age where transparency is key, this omission feels like a red flag. It makes you wonder about the long-term viability of their “lifetime access” promise.
InstaDoodle is a mixed bag. It’s a functional tool for creating basic doodle animations, and at $37 for lifetime access, it could be worth it for those who need this type of content regularly. 
However, the aggressive marketing tactics and lack of company transparency are off-putting.
If you’re in the market for a doodle animation tool, InstaDoodle might be worth considering, but approach with caution. 
Make sure it truly fits your needs before committing, and don’t feel pressured by the upsells. As with any “lifetime” deal from a new company, there’s always a risk, so factor that into your decision.
For casual users or those just dipping their toes into doodle animations, InstaDoodle could be a good starting point. For professionals or those needing more advanced features, you might want to explore other options in the market.
Remember, the best tool is the one that fits your specific needs and workflow. InstaDoodle might be that tool for some, but it’s certainly not one-size-fits-all.
