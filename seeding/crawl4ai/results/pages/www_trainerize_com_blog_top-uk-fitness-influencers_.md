## **Top UK Fitness Influencers to Follow**
From Love Island stars who’ve become fitness moguls, to content creators who make burpees bearable, the UK’s most engaging fitness influencers prove there’s more than one way to inspire a nation to move.
### **Lucy Davis | @lucydavis_fit**
**Instagram:** 779K **TikTok:** 203.4K **YouTube:** 281K
First up, this former pro swimmer is taking the UK by storm with her fitness content! As a fitness model and personal trainer, Davis champions a refreshingly balanced approach to health. 
She’s not afraid to get real about her past struggles with sport-related eating disorders. This authenticity shines through in her advocacy work. Lucy was among the first to celebrate Swim England’s [ban on weighing children](https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-england-66091661). 
Today, her content spans practical workout tips, intuitive eating guides, and honest conversations about body image. It’s this mix of expertise and vulnerability that makes her stand out in the digital fitness space!
### **Meggan Kirkland | @meggangrubb**
**Instagram:** 1.3M **TikTok:** 464.2K **YouTube:** 548K
Next, British fitness entrepreneur Meggan Kirkland (née Grubb) is redefining what it means to build an empire in the wellness space. 
She’s the founder of two thriving businesses: [Beyond](https://www.beyondapp.co.uk/), a health and well-being app with over 2 million (!!) completed workouts. She’s also the founder of [Bluee. ](https://www.movewithbluee.com/en-us)It’s an ethically-produced and adaptable activewear line inspired by transformation from caterpillar to cocoon to butterfly. 
Kirkland believes that true strength true strength encompasses body, mind, and soul. Through her content and products, she’s built a supportive community where women can embrace their unique fitness journeys.

### **Joe Wicks | @thebodycoach**
**Instagram** : 4.8M **YouTube** : 2.84M
Joe Wicks, better known as “The Body Coach,” transformed from a park bootcamp trainer into one of Britain’s most influential fitness figures. His journey began handing out flyers at train stations. Now, he’s helped over 600,000 people transform their lives through his accessible approach to fitness and nutrition.
During the COVID-19 pandemic, his “PE with Joe” YouTube sessions became a global phenomenon. Ultimately [earning him both an MBE and a Guinness World Record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Wicks_\(coach\)) for the most viewers on a fitness livestream. His #Leanin15 recipe videos and bestselling cookbooks have redefined quick, healthy cooking for busy people.
Through his [Body Coach app](https://subscribe.thebodycoach.com/) and social platforms, Wicks continues his mission to help people feel “fitter, healthier, and happier”—no matter where they are on their fitness journey.
### **Courtney Black | @courtneydblack**
**Instagram:** 819K **YouTube:** 61.8K 
Next, personal trainer and fitness coach Courtney Black has transformed her struggles into strength. After battling an eating disorder during her dance career—where she was pressured to maintain an unsustainably low weight—she found a healthier path through balanced fitness and nutrition.
Today, she champions the message that [“there’s more to life than having a flat stomach](https://www.womenshealthmag.com/uk/fitness/a42472738/courtney-black-my-body-my-story/).” Her approach resonates, and over 300,000 “warriors” have transformed their lives through her [fitness app](https://www.courtneyblack.co.uk/), which offers everything from home workouts to personalized meal plans.
Through her content and coaching, Black combines practical fitness advice with mental wellness support, helping people feel stronger, healthier, and happier—without the toxic diet culture she once experienced. We love to see it!

### **Michael Griffiths | @mac_griffiths_**
**Instagram:** 960K **YouTube** : 4.56K 
Former firefighter turned personal trainer Michael Griffiths knows what it’s like to struggle with fitness. He was “the fat kid in school” before starting his fitness journey. After overcoming disordered eating, he discovered the power of balanced nutrition and sustainable training, shaping his down-to-earth coaching approach.
Following his memorable stint on [Love Island UK’s fifth season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Love_Island_\(2015_TV_series\)_series_5), Griffiths turned his fitness passion into [Hustle As One](https://www.hustleasone.com/personalised-program), a game-changing training program. His philosophy? You don’t need to live in the gym to see results. Instead, he offers personalized workout plans and nutrition guidance that actually fit into hectic schedules. 
Through his content and coaching, Griffiths tackles both physical and mental transformation, helping clients smash through their barriers while keeping it real. 
### **Natalie Long | @nat_runs_**
**Instagram:** 318K**TikTok:** 1.1M**YouTube:** 265K
From ticking off her first road marathon with just her mum’s old Casio watch and a single energy gel, to conquering 50-mile ultras, Nat Long provides _serious_ ultrarunning inspiration. Her journey started with the Brighton Marathon in 2019, where she learned firsthand that marathon running isn’t always Instagram-perfect—but that’s what makes it real.
Known for her engaging “run until you see” videos that have blown up on TikTok and Instagram, Nat shares the ups and downs of distance running with refreshing honesty. Through her content and community building, she shows that ultra running isn’t just for elite athletes—it’s for anyone willing to lace up and give it a go! 
Recently a new mum, she continues to inspire others by showing how running fits into real life, sharing everything from training tips to staying active while pregnant.

**Instagram** : 165K **TikTok** : 28.1K **YouTube** : 2.17K 
After battling a vicious eating disorder, Niamh Spence has transformed her pain into purpose. As founder of [The Glow Up Project](https://theglowupproject.co.uk/), she’s on a mission to show women that true confidence comes from within. To her, the real “glow up” isn’t about changing your appearance—it’s an inside job. 
She’s a former host of the BBC podcast “[Fit and Fearless](https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p05sssdk/episodes/downloads).” Today, she’s actively building a community that champions self-love through workshops, public, and social content. 
### **Adam Maxted | @adammaxted**

**Instagram** : 201K **TikTok** : 10.2K **YouTube** : 1.28K 
From reality TV contestant to professional wrestler, [Adam Maxted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Maxted) has built a unique path in fitness. Standing 6’3″ and weighing 235 pounds, the Belfast-born athlete proves that physical transformation and mental strength go hand in hand.
As a professional wrestler turned mindset coach, he combines his experience in the ring with his passion for personal development. Since his Love Island appearance in 2016, Maxted has established himself as a championship wrestler and sought-after personal trainer.
Through social media and personal coaching, he shares his philosophy that success—whether in the ring or the gym—starts with mastering your mindset.
### **Laura Zialor | @laurazialor**
**Instagram** : 17.1K **TikTok** : 4.21K 
British high jumper Laura Zialor embodies resilience in athletics. After representing Great Britain at the World Championships and Commonwealth Games, the Buckinghamshire-born athlete faced [a career-threatening Achilles injury](https://www.bbc.com/sport/athletics/66072065) in 2023.
Rather than let it derail her dreams, she dove into advocacy. When funding challenges arose during her recovery, she successfully crowdfunded her surgery while highlighting the need for better support for athletes. 
Now an online coach specializing in fitness, rehab, and performance—especially after an injury—she shares her journey of coming back stronger.
