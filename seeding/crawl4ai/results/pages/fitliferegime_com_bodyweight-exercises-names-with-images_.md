## **Bodyweight Chest Exercises With Image**
While weight training builds immense chest strength, you should not underestimate callisthenics training.
[Bodyweight exercises](https://fitliferegime.com/bodyweight-chest-exercises-beginner-to-advance/) can help you strengthen your muscles and improve your stability. 
Push-ups may be king, but dozens of challenging bodyweight moves target the chest from all angles.
Here is a list of 15 of the best chest exercises, with images that illustrate the precise body positioning for each move.
### **1. Wall Push Up**
[Wall push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/wall-push-ups/) is a variation of the traditional exercise designed for beginners or those with limited upper body strength.
This exercise engages the chest, shoulders, and triceps, helping to build strength in these areas.
The closer you are to the wall, the easier this push-up will be, but remember to keep your body alignment in mind when you do it.
### **2. Knee Push Up**
The [knee push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/how-to-do-knee-push-ups-and-its-variation-with-proper-form/), a modified push-up, is a bodyweight chest exercise that works for muscle groups throughout your upper body.
Beginners should add these bodyweight exercises to their at-home [chest workout routine](https://fitliferegime.com/10-best-chest-exercises-and-workout/).
If you’re having trouble performing a full push-up, practice an easier push-up variation, the knee push-up.
### **3. Incline Push-Ups**
The incline push-up is a variation of the classic push-up that involves elevating your hands on a bench, chair, or other sturdy surface.
The inclined position emphasises the[ lower portion of your chest muscles](https://fitliferegime.com/lower-chest-bodyweight-workout-that-you-can-do-at-home/). You can do it anywhere, and it’s free. You don’t need expensive equipment. 
You only need a stable surface like a table, desk, or wall.
### **4. Standard Push-Ups**
The press-up or push-up exercise is standard for strengthening the upper extremities. It is a closed kinetic chain exercise that requires no tools and uses the body weight as resistance.
The [standard push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/standard-push-up-vs-incline-push-up-vs-decline-push-up/) is undoubtedly one of the best bodyweight chest exercises. 
They work the [triceps, pectoral, and shoulders](https://fitliferegime.com/best-chest-shoulder-triceps-workout-for-muscle-mass/). With proper form, they can strengthen the lower back and core muscles.
### **5. Close Grip Push-Ups**
[Narrow grip push-ups](https://fitliferegime.com/close-grip-push-up-how-to-do-muscle-worked-benefits/) are the best bodyweight workout for building your entire upper body, including [shoulders, chest, and triceps](https://fitliferegime.com/best-chest-shoulder-triceps-workout-for-muscle-mass/). **It’s one of the best inner chest exercises you can do at home.**
This is evidenced by a [study](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16095413/) that found greater electromyography (EMG) activity in the triceps brachii and inner chest when doing close push-ups compared to when executing [regular push-ups.](https://fitliferegime.com/standard-push-up-vs-incline-push-up-vs-decline-push-up/)
Close-grip push-ups can be challenging, so it’s important to start with a modified version (knee push-ups) if needed.
### **6. Wide Push Up**
If you’ve mastered regular pushups and want to target your muscles differently, [Wide pushups](https://fitliferegime.com/wide-grip-push-ups/) are a good option.
Wide pushups target your chest and shoulder muscles more than standard pushups because you position your hands farther apart.
For a simpler version, you can do a wide-hand knee push-up, also called a modified type push-up.
### **7. Decline Push-Up**
The [decline pushup](https://fitliferegime.com/how-to-do-decline-push-up-its-variation-with-good-technique/) is a variation of the basic pushup. It is done with your feet on an elevated surface, which puts your body at a downward angle and works your shoulders and chest.
It puts special emphasis on the muscles in your [upper chest](https://fitliferegime.com/10-best-upper-chest-exercises-and-workout/).
It is a more difficult version of a regular push-up in which you raise your feet higher than your hands.
### **8. Parallel Bar Dip (Chest Dip)**
The parallel bar dip is a compound exercise primarily targeting the[ chest muscles (pectoralis major), triceps, and shoulders.](https://fitliferegime.com/best-chest-shoulder-triceps-workout-for-muscle-mass/)
Parallel bar dips allow for a greater range of motion than many chest exercises. To [train the lower chest](https://fitliferegime.com/10-best-lower-chest-exercises-and-workout/) effectively, you should lean forward rather than upright.
The range of motion of the dip **allows for a deeper stretch on the lower[chest and triceps](https://fitliferegime.com/12-best-chest-and-triceps-workout-to-build-muscles/)**.
### **9. Spider-Man Push-Up**
A [Spider-Man push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/spiderman-push-ups/) is an advanced variation of the [traditional push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/standard-push-up-vs-incline-push-up-vs-decline-push-up/) exercise that targets the core, shoulders, and back muscles.
The twisting motion of the Spider-Man push-up also targets the**[oblique muscles](https://fitliferegime.com/oblique-crunches/) and abs,** helping to strengthen and tone the muscles.
This exercise is primarily**chest and[arm strengthening](https://fitliferegime.com/arm-workouts-without-weights-that-you-can-do-at-home/),** but it also **increases**[**hip flexibility** ](https://www.healthline.com/health/tight-hips)and works your core.
### **10. Shoulder Tap Push Up**
The shoulder tap push-up is a variation of the traditional push-up that strengthens the chest, shoulders, triceps, and core muscles.
It is a **full-body strength move** that focuses on your arms and chest while also strengthening your core.
  * It is performed by starting in a plank position, 
  * lowering the chest towards the ground, pushing back up to the plank position, 
  * lifting one hand off the ground and tapping the opposite shoulder. 
  * Repeat with the other hand.


### **11. Hindu Push-Up**
Indian wrestlers call it ‘Dand’. This exercise is different from regular pushups and is thought to give a more complete workout. 
Just like the push-up, the [Hindu push-up](https://fitliferegime.com/hindu-pushups-how-to-benefits-and-muscle-worked/) can be performed anywhere and is a [great option for a home workout.](https://fitliferegime.com/best-ways-to-workout-at-home-without-equipments/)
Each repetition involves**going from a downward dog position to a cobra pose and** swooping by holding the head and body close to the ground.
**Bruce Lee also incorporated Hindu pushups in his training regime.**
### **12. Alternate Punching**
Alternate Punching is a dynamic exercise that enhances cardiovascular health, boosts upper body strength, and improves coordination. 
This exercise engages the chest, shoulders, arms, and core and improves coordination, endurance, and agility.
It’s popular in boxing, martial arts training, and functional fitness routines.
### **13. Diamond Push Up**
[Diamond push-ups, also known as triangle push-ups](https://fitliferegime.com/diamond-push-ups-how-to-do-muscle-worked-benefits/), are a more **advanced type of the classic push-up**. 
You can practice diamond push-ups by bringing your hands too close together to form a diamond or triangle shape below your chest.
Several benefits include increased triceps and chest activity, improved core strength and stability, and enhanced shoulder strength.
### **14. Single-Leg Push Up**
The single-leg push-up is a challenging variation of the classic push-up that takes it to a whole new level. 
It’s not just about upper body strength; it also requires core stability, balance, and coordination.
Once you can do single-leg push-ups on a flat surface, try for a more significant challenge.
  * by adding weight, like a weighted vest or backpack.
  * Try single-leg push-ups with a medicine ball for an even more significant core challenge.


### **15. Clapping Push-Up**
Regular push-ups are common exercises, but their more powerful cousins are very intense and powerful.
Clapping Push-ups are an advanced variation of push-up exercise that **works your chest, triceps, abs, and shoulders**.
The clap push-up is a difficult exercise that **combines strength and technique**. It’s good for both athletes and lifters.
### **16. T Push Up**
A T push-up is a variation of the traditional [**push-up**](https://fitliferegime.com/best-push-ups-for-chest/)**that adds a rotational movement. This****compound exercise** **primarily targets the[chest, shoulders, and triceps](https://fitliferegime.com/best-chest-shoulder-triceps-workout-for-muscle-mass/)**.
The exercise is performed similarly to a traditional push-up. However, with an additional movement, one arm is lifted and rotated out to the side, forming a “T” shape with the body.
## **Name Of Bodyweight Back Exercises**
[Bodyweight training](https://fitliferegime.com/bodyweight-workout/) helps build a strong, muscular back through challenging exercises that use your weight as resistance. 
When doing calisthenics, it’s important to do it correctly to build the connection between your mind and muscles that helps your muscles grow.
This glossary will overview popular bodyweight back moves, such as pull-ups, Superman holds, back extensions, and inverted rows.
Let’s [build a stronger back with no equipment](https://fitliferegime.com/15-best-bodyweight-back-workout-beginner-to-advanced/) required!
### **1. Pull-Up**
[The pull-up ](https://fitliferegime.com/different-types-of-pull-ups-and-chin-ups-variations/)is an upper-body strength movement that targets your [wings](https://fitliferegime.com/wings-exercise-at-home-gym/), chest, shoulders, and arms.
They are often a challenge for beginners and even experienced athletes to perform. 
The pull-up increases your back’s strength, thickness, and width, [specifically your lats.](https://fitliferegime.com/lats-exercises-to-build-back/) The lats influence the back width and form the “V” in the upper back.
### **2. Chin Ups**
Do you think chin-ups are just a [back workout](https://fitliferegime.com/7-back-workout-at-home-with-dumbbell/)? Think again. Chin-ups are a **powerful exercise that works many muscles**. It gives you a more vigorous upper-body workout than you’re expecting.
Initially, chin-ups and pull-ups may appear similar, but there are **actually some key differences between them**.
  * Chin-ups are performed with an **underhand or supinated grip** , with palms facing toward you.
  * Many find chin-ups to be slightly easier than pull-ups.


### **3. Neutral-Grip Pull-Ups**
In neutral-grip pull-ups, the **hands are positioned with a parallel grip**(palms facing each other) on the bar or a parallel bar.
This grip variation **puts less stress on the shoulder** , and also works on the biceps and forearms.
A neutral grip pull/chin-up variation can be useful for those with shoulder injuries or pain, as it is less stressful on the shoulder joint.
### **4. Inverted Row**
The inverted row is a bodyweight exercise where you push yourself up towards a bar or other sturdy overhead support.
Due to the reduced bodyweight resistance, the inverted row is easier to perform than pull-ups. It’s a great starting point for building upper body strength for those who find pull-ups too challenging.
It effectively targets various muscle groups, including your back muscles (lats, traps, rhomboids), biceps, shoulders, core, and hamstrings and [glutes](https://fitliferegime.com/bresistance-band-glute-exercises/). 
### **5. Superman**
Superman is one of the best back exercises you can do without equipment. It **strengthens your upper and[lower back muscles](https://fitliferegime.com/lower-back-strengthening-exercises-home/)**.
If done regularly, the Superman exercise may **help alleviate back pain** related to weak back muscles.
Additionally, it strengthens back muscles. It also works on your [glutes](https://fitliferegime.com/foam-roller-exercises-for-leg-including-hips-glutes/) and your [hamstring muscles](https://fitliferegime.com/15-best-hamstring-exercises-for-mass-and-strength/).
### **6. Good Morning**
The good morning exercise is a hinge movement where you bend at the hips while keeping your back straight.
It effectively targets your glutes, hamstrings, and lower back.
  * The erector spinae [muscles of the lower back](https://fitliferegime.com/lower-back-strengthening-exercises-home/) work isometrically to **keep the spine in an extended position**
  * The hamstrings and gluteus maximus work isotonically to perform hip extension.


### **7. Hip Bridge**
The hip bridge is a good starter move for butt, hamstring, and lower back muscles and the best back exercises at home to [**manage chronic low back pain**.](https://fitliferegime.com/foam-roller-exercises-for-back-to-improve-pain-flexibility/)
But when you do glute bridges regularly, you work your glutes and**lower back muscles**.
These muscles are meant to hold your body upright, so they will get stronger when you do this.
### **8. Flat Bench Hyperextension**
The flat bench hyperextension is a strength training exercise that primarily [targets the lower back muscles,](https://fitliferegime.com/lower-back-dumbbell-exercises/) but also engages the glutes and hamstrings.
Stronger lower back muscles can help reduce the risk of injury, especially during other physical activities and sports.
### **9. Bird Dog**
The Bird Dog exercise is a fantastic way to strengthen your core, improve your balance, and increase your stability. It targets your core, lower back, shoulders, and hips.
It is a low-impact exercise, making it a good option for people with joint pain or injuries.
### **10. Cat Cow**
The cat-cow exercise is a **spinal mobilization exercise** that helps a person “loosen” their spine and **improves movement of the****lower back.**
It is a gentle flow between two poses, which warms the body and improves spine flexibility. 
This exercise stretches the back and neck, softly stimulates and strengthens the abdominal organs, and opens up the chest.
### **11. High Reverse Plank**
The reverse plank exercise is a great bodyweight shoulder exercise for strengthening your core and the key muscles of your **posterior chain, namely, your erector spinae, gluteus maximus, and hamstrings.**
Beginners should start with learning the regular plank.
