### 6. Start With Test Campaigns to Minimize Risk
If you’re new to TikTok influencer marketing or working with a creator for the first time, start with a test campaign. Test campaigns are small-scale collaborations that allow you to evaluate a creator’s performance without committing to a large budget.Here’s how to run a successful test campaign:
  * ****Keep It Simple**** : Ask the creator to produce 1–2 short videos (30–60 seconds) highlighting your product or service. Focus on UGC-style content—authentic, unpolished videos that feel like the creator’s usual content.
  * ****Set Clear Expectations**** : Provide brand guidelines (e.g., key messages, hashtags, prohibited content) but give the creator creative freedom. TikTok users can spot scripted content from a mile away—let the creator’s personality shine.
  * ****Track Key Metrics**** : Measure engagement rate, watch time, completion rate, and any conversions (e.g., clicks to your website, sales). Use this data to determine if the creator is a good fit for long-term partnerships.
  * ****Test Multiple Creators**** : Partner with 3–5 nano or micro influencers for your test campaign. This allows you to compare performance and identify which creators drive the best results.


Test campaigns are also a great way to build relationships with creators. ### 7. Leverage UGC to Boost Authenticity & Reach
User-generated content (UGC) is gold on TikTok. It’s authentic, relatable, and trusted by consumers—79% of people say UGC influences their purchasing decisions more than traditional ads.   * ****Ask for Rights to Repurpose Content**** : Include a clause in your collaboration agreement that gives you the right to reuse the creator’s content in paid ads, on your website, Instagram, Facebook, or other platforms. This extends the life of the content and increases your ROI.

  * ****Feature UGC on Your Brand Account**** : Repost the best UGC from influencers and their followers on your brand’s TikTok account. This not only rewards the creator but also shows your audience that real people love your product.

### 9. Track Performance & Measure ROI
Finding and partnering with influencers is just the beginning—you need to track performance to determine if your campaign is successful and identify areas for improvement. TikTok’s built-in analytics (for business accounts) and influencer marketplaces like Socialbook offer tools to measure key metrics. Measurable ROI is a top priority for brands in 2026, as highlighted in [our latest influencer marketing trends report](https://socialbook.io/blog/).Here are the most important metrics to track for TikTok influencer campaigns:
#### 1. Engagement Metrics
  * ****Engagement Rate**** : As discussed earlier, this is the percentage of the creator’s followers who interact with the post (likes, comments, shares, saves).
  * ****Watch Time & Completion Rate****: These metrics indicate how engaging the content is. A high completion rate means viewers are watching the entire video—TikTok’s algorithm loves this and will push the content to more users.
  * ****Shares & Saves****: Shares and saves are strong indicators of content quality—users only share or save videos they find valuable or entertaining.


#### 2. Reach & Awareness Metrics
  * ****Total Reach**** : The number of unique users who saw the content.
  * ****Impressions**** : The total number of times the content was viewed (including repeat views).
  * ****Brand Mentions**** : The number of times your brand is mentioned in the video or comments.
  * ****Hashtag Performance**** : If you used a branded hashtag, track how many times it was used and how much reach it generated.


#### 3. Conversion Metrics
  * ****Click-Through Rate (CTR)**** : The percentage of viewers who clicked on the link in the creator’s bio or video (use a unique tracking link to measure this).
  * ****Landing Page Visits**** : The number of users who visited your website from the creator’s content.
  * ****Conversions**** : The number of users who completed a desired action (e.g., made a purchase, signed up for a newsletter, downloaded an app). Use UTM parameters or affiliate links to attribute conversions to specific creators.
  * ****ROI**** : Calculate your return on investment using this formula: (Revenue Generated – Campaign Cost) / Campaign Cost × 100. A positive ROI means your campaign was profitable.
