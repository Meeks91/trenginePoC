## Step 2: Find niche for your new yoga studio
There are several types of yoga. Each has its own characteristics, and depending on your vision for your studio and the mission you have in creating it, you should decide which yoga styles you want to introduce into your studio.
**What type of yoga will you specialize in?** Here are brief characteristics of each type of yoga:
  * **Hatha yoga:** This is one of the most basic types of yoga, focusing on the physical aspects of the practice. It includes poses (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation.
  * **Ashtanga yoga:** It is a dynamic, rigorous style of yoga that consists of sets of asana (pose) sequences linked by fluid movements and synchronized breathing.
  * **Vinyasa yoga:** It is a dynamic and flowing yoga practice in which movements are synchronized with the breath. It usually focuses on smooth transitions between poses.
  * **Joga Iyengara:** This style of yoga places great emphasis on precision and technique. It uses props, such as blocks or straps, to enable practitioners to perform asanas correctly.
  * **Yin yoga:** This is a gentle yoga practice that focuses on holding postures for long periods, often in sitting or lying positions. This form of yoga is designed to deeply relax muscles and joints.
  * **Kundalini yoga:** It focuses on activating vital energy (kundalini) through dynamic asana sequences, breathing techniques, mantras, and meditation.
  * **Bikram yoga:** Performed in a heated room (about 40 degrees Celsius), it consists of 26 poses designed to improve strength, flexibility, and health.
  * **Power yoga** : It is an intense, dynamic form of yoga that combines strength, endurance, and fluidity of movement. It often focuses on strengthening the body and improving physical fitness.
  * **Air yoga** : This is a type of yoga performed using hammocks suspended from the ceiling. It allows you to perform various asanas while suspended, which can help improve balance and flexibility.


Each of these types of yoga has its unique features and benefits, allowing practitioners to choose the style that suits their needs and preferences. Remember, as long as your studio’s floor space allows you, you can introduce all of these styles. However, if your studio is too small – no worries! You can introduce them at different times on different days of the week.

## Step 5: Obtain necessary licenses and permits to become part of yoga alliance
So you’ve decided to share your love of yoga by opening your own studio – congratulations! Before you welcome your first downward-facing dog, however, you’ll need to take a few necessary steps to ensure your business runs smoothly and legally. This includes facing the sometimes daunting world of licenses, permits and insurance.
### Check local business license requirements to get thriving yoga studio
Every region has its own regulations for operating a business. The first step is to research the specific requirements in your area. This might involve contacting your local government office or chamber of commerce.
  * **Local regulations:** Check what kind of license you need to become a part of the yoga market. Local government websites or business offices often provide detailed information on requirements.
  * **Business registration:** Make sure you register your yoga studio as a business entity, such as an LLC or Corporation, which can provide legal protection and tax benefits. 
  * **Yoga Alliance Membership:** Although not a legal requirement, registering with the Yoga Alliance can increase credibility and provide additional resources. Check their requirements for your studio and teachers.


### Secure any necessary licenses and permits to operate
Once you’ve tackled business registration, delve into any necessary permits for operating your yoga studio. This may be a large initial investment, but it will contribute to having a successful business. 
These could include:
  * **General Business License:** Almost all localities will require a general business license.
  * **Special permits** : Depending on your location, you may need special permits related to health and safety, especially if you plan to make structural changes to your studio space or operate in a residential neighborhood.
  * **Yoga studio requirements** : Follow any additional regulations that may be specific to yoga studios, such as accessibility standards or health department guidelines for wellness facilities.
  * **Zoning permits:** Ensure your chosen location is zoned for commercial use, particularly activities involving fitness or health instruction.
  * **Health department permits:** Some areas require permits from the health department to operate a yoga studio, especially if you plan to offer amenities like showers or changing rooms.
