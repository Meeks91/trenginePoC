## The food influencer marketing landscape
The UK food scene on social media is naturally vibrant and diverse, reflecting the nation’s passion for everything from heritage dishes to global innovation. Food influencers in the UK typically emphasise not just recipes, but also the story behind the food, sustainability, and accessible cooking, resonating with audiences across all age groups.
[Credibility](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/authenticity-in-influencer-marketing/) is paramount. The era of overly polished, unattainable gastronomy is over. This shift has paved the way for honest product reviews, realistic cooking journeys, and a more holistic approach to food. Home-grown trends like sustainable eating, ‘grow your own,’ and ethical sourcing are significant. There is also a strong focus on affordable, plant-based, and healthy meals. The shift toward value-based food consumerism often intersects with Gen Z spending habits; see how [UK student influencer marketing](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/student-influencers-uk/) is reshaping peer-to-peer advocacy for modern food brands. Creators aren’t just posting recipes; they are building communities around shared culinary experiences, dietary choices, and the simple joy of food.
Before starting your outreach, research [trends in the food industry](https://www.innovamarketinsights.com/trends/consumer-food-trends-in-the-uk-2025/). Some exciting things to look out for right now include:
  * Conscious consumerism as a core value in sourcing of food and reduction of waste.
  * Non-alcoholic drink pairings that elevate dining experiences.
  * Affordability for both restaurants and diners.

## How to choose UK food influencers
Any UK food influencer your brand partners with should check these important boxes:
  * **Audience demographics:** Review their audience data (or request a [media kit](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/influencer-media-kit/)) to ensure alignment with your target market.
  * **Performance metrics:** Examine engagement and other key [influencer marketing metrics](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/influencer-marketing-metrics/). Quality over quantity is essential here. Micro and nano influencers won’t have millions of followers, but they often command a much higher engagement rate than mega influencers.
  * **Previous brand partnerships:** Look through their history to gauge alignment with your brand values. Partner with authentic, transparent creators who will not risk their audience’s goodwill—or non-compliance with the Advertising Standards Authority (ASA).


ASA guidelines govern both influencers and the brands they work with. The [ASA website](https://www.asa.org.uk/) has specific platform guidelines and content types that you should review before embarking on a new campaign. The rule is simple: Every piece of content posted on your brand’s behalf must be clearly marked as advertising.
