## What is a social media wall
A social media wall aggregates content from various social media platforms like Instagram, Facebook, BlueSky and X and displays them as a single feed in one place. It is typically embedded in websites to build social proof and displayed on screens during events to boost attendee engagement.
You can curate images, videos, and posts from a single social channel or customize it by mixing content from different sources. If you’re mixing visuals from multiple sources, applying a consistent look with [filter apps](https://fixthephoto.com/best-filter-apps.html) can help the wall feel cohesive without changing what people actually posted. This content is aggregated through hashtags, mentions, or specific handles and gets automatically updated unless you prefer moderating post-by-post.
## What are the different types of social media walls (+ examples)
Here are the most common types of social media walls you can create to grab your website visitor's attention , increase website engagement, and bring in more sales:
### Hashtag social media walls 
Hashtag social media walls use specific hashtags to gather and display social media content in dynamic layouts. These types of walls are best for creating buzz during marketing campaigns and events. You can promote your hashtags through social media and encourage people to use them when posting content related to your brand or event.
[Wake Forest University](https://homecoming.wfu.edu/) encourages students and faculty to use the hashtag #WFUHC to promote their homecoming event. They display these posts on their homecoming page to give prospective students an idea of the university culture and campus life.
### UGC social walls
User-generated content (UGC) social walls are created to showcase how customers use your products. In fact, showcasing [UGC content](https://flockler.com/blog/ugc-video), such as product reviews, before-and-after content, and product tutorials, on your website boosts your conversion rates by[ 29%.](https://flockler.com/blog/ugc-statistics)
Here's how a Swedish makeup brand[ IsaDora](https://www.isadora.com/en/) uses an[ Instagram wall](https://flockler.com/blog/instagram-wall-for-website-examples) on their website to display user-generated content. The feed gives a peek into how customers use IsaDora products and offers potential buyers the confidence to buy from the brand.
### Social proof wall
In a social proof or[ review wall](https://flockler.com/blog/3-ways-to-use-flockler-reviews), product reviews from various sources are aggregated and displayed as a feed on websites and digital displays during events and in retail shops. Social proof walls display a mix of reviews, ratings and testimonials in visual and textual formats. 
One of the key differences between a UGC wall and a social proof wall is the impact it creates on your target audience. A social proof wall establishes your credibility, while a UGC wall gives potential customers a reason to trust you through real customer experiences.
Here's a social media wall example from[ GoPro](https://flockler.com/case-studies/gopro) featuring a mix of text-based and visual reviews and user-generated content for social proof.
### Employee branding walls
Employee branding walls showcase employee-generated content, behind-the-scenes content and reviews from LinkedIn and Twitter in dynamic layouts on company websites or intranets. The aim of these walls is to make employees feel heard and seen and boost their morale. You can also use these walls to promote your brand as an employee-friendly brand with a great work culture.
[APRA](https://www.apra-optinet.pl/), a German-based manufacturer of housing cabinets, does this perfectly. They let their employees take over their Instagram and post everyday BTS content. This gives their target audience and potential employees a glimpse of APRA's life. They also have custom social media walls with employee-generated content and company announcements embedded on their website.
### Event social walls
Event social walls feature live updates or social media posts from attendees to create a sense of community in event-goers and keep them updated on important information like venue changes or speaker details. These walls, when placed in strategic locations at the event venue, like in the lobby, stage areas and jumbotrons and screens, also help create an event buzz and increase attendee engagement.
### Shoppable social media walls
[Shoppable social media walls](https://flockler.com/solutions/instagram-feed-webshop), as the name suggests, let you shop for a brand's products directly from a social media feed. The embedded posts in the feed give product details and a buying link, making it easy for buyers to check out from the feed. 
[Sohome](https://flockler.com/case-studies/sohome), a luxury home decor brand, pairs user-generated content (UGC) with the shoppable feature to increase sales. Their social media wall, a curated collection of posts on how customers style Sohome products when combined with product tagging, builds social proof and nudges buyers to make a sale.
## 4 best places to add social media walls
You can add social media walls to your websites, e-commerce stores, live and virtual events, intranets and even retail shops. In this section, we also delve deep into the best type of social media wall content for each use-case to achieve optimum results. Read on to know more.
### 1. Social media walls on websites
Social media walls that match the look and feel of a website instantly make the website more visually appealing. A stunning social media wall urges website visitors to engage with your content and spend more time on your website. 
You can add user-generated content like product reviews or entries from hashtag campaigns to social media walls and display them on websites to build authority and social proof. You can also add employee-generated or spotlight content to boost employee morale and let visitors take a peek into the inside world.
**Pro Tip:** Create a custom feed by mixing different content types from multiple social media channels. Include videos, posts, and images from multiple channels on a single social media wall to hook your audience's interest.
### 2. Social media walls in e-commerce stores
Adding live social media walls to e-commerce stores helps build social proof and encourages potential customers to make a purchase from you. To do this, create social media walls using[ user-generated content](https://flockler.com/blog/ugc-video), influencer marketing content and customer reviews. This content gives buyers an idea of how people are using your products, increasing their confidence to buy from you.
You can further reduce friction and increase the chances of making a sale by adding shoppable Instagram feeds to a social media wall.[ Shoppable feeds](https://flockler.com/solutions/shoppable-ugc-galleries) are Instagram posts linked to specific product pages that allow buyers to make purchases directly from where they're embedded. For instance, when buyers click on an influencer collab post added to your wall, they'll see product details and a call to action to buy now.
### 3. Social media walls for live and virtual events
You can use [social media walls to engage attendees before, during and after an event](https://flockler.com/blog/social-media-walls-for-events). To create buzz around your event and increase registrations, create and embed a social media wall with speaker sneak peeks and event promotions from influencers on your website.
To boost event audience participation and engagement during the event, display social media walls with posts, stories, mentions and event hashtags. Using [digital signage software](https://www.aiscreen.io/), you can easily sync these walls across multiple venues or screens to create a consistent visual experience. In case of hybrid events, you can add social media walls on display screens and jumbotrons to bridge the gap between in-person and virtual attendees and build a rapport among them.
After the live event, you can pull event-related content using hashtags and embed it on your websites. This way, you can inform website visitors about the latest news and garner more likes, comments and shares for your event's social media content.
### 4. Social media walls in retail shops
A social media wall on retail shops' screens is an effective and non-salesy way to convince indecisive potential customers. In fact, these walls make the job of salespeople easier, as they serve as a testament to your product's worth.
Some of the social media content you can use to curate walls for retail shops includes before-and-after videos, unboxing videos, and product reviews. You can also display promotional or exclusive social media content to increase your follower base and engagement.
## What are the benefits of social media walls
Here are the top 3 [benefits of using social media walls](https://flockler.com/blog/what-are-the-key-benefits-of-a-social-media-wall) on websites and screens:
### 1. To boost the organic reach of social media posts
Social media walls increase the organic visibility of your social media posts. Here's how: When you embed social media walls on websites, visitors are more likely to check out your social media handles, follow them and even engage with your posts. The more it happens, the higher the engagement and the greater the chances of these posts appearing on people's feeds.
### 2. To cross-promote social media marketing campaigns
Running multiple campaigns on different social platforms is effective, but you still leave money on the table if you don't cross-promote them on websites. By adding a social media wall with influencer campaign posts, you can[ build social proof](https://flockler.com/blog/what-is-social-proof), drive website visitors to influencers' posts and nudge them to avail campaign-specific offers to purchase from your brand. 
Brands that collaborate with expert teams such as [InBeat media advertising](https://inbeat.agency/blog/top-advertising-agencies) agency often see stronger campaign performance, as such agencies specialize in leveraging influencer content and social walls to amplify engagement and conversions across multiple channels.
### 3. To boost website engagement
If your website is text-heavy, an aesthetically curated social media wall is a great way to catch your potential buyer's eye. They'll be more likely to spend more time on the website checking out the social media posts on the wall.
