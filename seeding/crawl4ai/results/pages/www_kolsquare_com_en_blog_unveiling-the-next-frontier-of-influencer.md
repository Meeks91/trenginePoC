## What is a Professional YouTuber?
A YouTuber is simply an individual who uploads videos on YouTube. There are no fees for creating a YouTube channel, so many start posting as hobbyists. While most YouTubers have other professions, they can earn money through brand collaborations and advertisements shown during, before, and after their videos. If their content accumulates views and subscribers, it offers an additional source of income and may become their full-time profession with a potential for significant earnings. 
## The Evolution of the Youtuber
While the precise definition of a YouTuber has mostly stayed the same since YouTube began, the potential and opportunities for creators differ vastly. As a result, the potential for brand collaboration with these influencers has also evolved. 
Let’s explore how YouTube has adapted and expanded and, consequently, transformed the way YouTubers act on the platform. 
### Humble Beginnings and Humble Creators
Since its launch in 2005, YouTube has undergone many changes. Originally, it served as an entertainment platform for sharing entertaining videos with low production quality. The public perceived YouTubers as novel and amateurish, and, making little profit, YouTube couldn’t offer content creators a way to earn money directly from their channels. 
In 2007, soon after Google purchased the platform, YouTube unveiled its tagline, “Broadcast Yourself,” and presented itself as a space for anyone wishing to share their thoughts, feelings, and personal experiences through video. Creators embraced this message by continuing to upload general yet relatable and genuine content. 
### Influencer Earning Potential with the Partner Program
The influencer landscape started transforming at the end of 2007 when YouTube announced its Partner Program, allowing creators to monetize their videos. In the following years, YouTube began to make a profit and strengthened its position as a top video-uploading platform. As a result, many YouTubers with successful channels evolved into high-earning celebrities. 
### The Pitfalls of Popular Youtubers with Authenticity without Professionalism 
As YouTube continued establishing itself as a serious platform, it removed its “Broadcast Yourself” tagline from the home page. It also altered its algorithm to prioritize videos with long watch durations over high numbers of clicks. 
As it transitioned to promoting high-quality content, YouTube encountered an incredible challenge. While famous YouTube channels added to the platform’s popularity, they also posed a risk due to the unfiltered nature of their content. This risk was compounded by the growth of social activism after the #MeToo and Black Lives Matter movements. 
Some celebrated creators with millions of viewers, notably PewDiePie, caused controversies that threatened YouTube and the brands that advertised on it. In response, many brands canceled their advertisements with YouTube, causing a phenomenon known as the “Adpocolypse.”
Realizing the potential dangers, YouTube revised its monetization policies to reduce the risks from scandal-prone channels and prioritized content moderation to maintain its respectability and domination of the online video marketplace. 
In response, many YouTubers recognized their unsafe position and moved towards professional, specialized content. 
### COVID-19 to the Present Day: The YouTube-Certified Expert
During the COVID-19 pandemic, many people with abundant spare time flocked to YouTube for information, entertainment, and skill-building, further encouraging an influx of specialist creators.
Today, YouTube’s social impact is undeniable. It boasts more than[ 2.4 billion monthly users](https://www.statista.com/statistics/272014/global-social-networks-ranked-by-number-of-users/) and is the second-largest search engine. 
Yet, this success has heightened competition among creators, further pushing them towards specialization to stand out amid the crowded landscape of over 14 billion uploaded videos.
## How Do You Become an Expert on YouTube? Generalization vs. Niche Dominance
YouTubers must now consistently create high-quality videos with eye-catching thumbnails to stand out in a saturated market. While generalists still exist and can command massive audiences, it is easier to succeed by creating niche content. This allows Youtubers to position themselves as experts in their field, whether it is tech reviews, crafts, DIY, fashion, or sports. 
These key opinion leaders (KOLs) maintain the authenticity of the platform’s origins to form close bonds with their audiences. They offer product recommendations, in-depth analysis, and sage advice. Over time, viewers trust that their content is valuable, and the YouTuber develops a strong track record with their audience. 
For brands, the appeal of collaborating with niche YouTubers is clear. They can tap into a community of like-minded individuals who enjoy sharing and learning about their niche through a trusted voice. This provides an opportunity for enhanced brand visibility and engagement.
## Cultivating Trust through Authenticity
For brands to thrive, consumers must be aware of and trust their products. Especially as consumers increasingly seek to buy products that align with their[ values and needs](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/marketing-strategies/video/creator-content-in-2024/?_gl=1*ot238t*_up*MQ..*_ga*NDQ0ODE1MzA2LjE3MTEzNjM5MTg.*_ga_BXYLHC2HPB*MTcxMTM2MzkxNy4xLjEuMTcxMTM2MzkxNy4wLjAuMA..).
Expert YouTubers can help in this regard. They cultivate a sense of trust built upon the authenticity YouTube was initially known for and proven expertise. Unlike mainstream influencers who gain subscribers solely through their personality and entertainment, Niche experts become trustworthy sources of information. 
As endorsements from a YouTube channel lead to positive experiences, their credibility grows. By providing unbiased and honest reviews, they build trustworthiness. These creators acquire a dedicated following with a keen interest in the niche and a strong likelihood of making purchases based on their suggestions.
Openness to finding products through social media is commonplace, and[ 68% of people have watched](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/consumer-insights/consumer-trends/youtube-shopping-decision-statistics/) YouTube content to help make a purchasing decision. Consumers particularly value the recommendations of influencers with whom they feel a personal connection.

## The Most Common YouTuber Collaborations for Brands
Brand and YouTuber collaborations are mutually beneficial relationships in which the influencer gains content ideas and provides their audience with reviews of relevant products. At the same time, the brands build awareness and trust. 
While creator content was, at one point, limited to simple deals, it can now take many forms. Here’s a streamlined look at key methods of collaboration with a YouTube channel:
  * Sponsorship: Here, brands pay influencers to showcase their products on specific videos.
  * Gifting: This is a subtle approach in which brands send free products to influencers for review. Often, the only cost involved is sending a product to a creator for review. It is ideal for generating organic content, but clarity about expectations is crucial.
  * Collaboration: Brands can jointly create content with influencers that showcases both the YouTuber’s expertise and the brand’s message. This method demands a high degree of creative alignment and mutual respect.
  * Partnerships: These are long-term connections between a brand and an influencer built upon shared goals. They often involve creating multiple content pieces across various platforms.


For each method, it’s essential to maintain open communication, establish clear goals, and ensure that the collaboration maintains the audience’s trust in the YouTuber.
