The story of influencers and the creator economy is intertwined with the culture of gamers and the gaming community. When social media exploded, gaming didn’t just join the party, it became the main event. YouTube gave early gaming influencers the stage to build an entire genre from the ground up, and what started as gameplay videos quickly evolved into one of the most powerful forces in digital culture.
Today, gaming is more than trying to reach the next level or beating the final boss. It’s a global entertainment powerhouse. A cultural engine. And arguably, one of the biggest drivers of online culture today. The scale is impossible to ignore; the [global gaming market is projected to reach over $503 billion annually by 2025](https://www.statista.com/statistics/292056/video-game-market-value-worldwide/), up from $396 billion in 2023.
Behind that growth, gaming influencers have become key players. The biggest names and titles in the industry now routinely put their faith in creators to bring new releases to life, build communities, and shape conversations with audiences at scale.
Here are some of the top gaming creators who aren’t just playing the game — they’re shaping it.
## What is a gaming influencer?
A gaming influencer is a content creator who specialises in sharing gameplay, commentary, and insights into gaming culture across platforms like YouTube, Twitch, and [TikTok](https://disruptmarketing.co/tiktok/). These creators are at the heart of the gaming community - entertainers, storytellers, and trendsetters who turn their passion for gaming into thriving digital communities.
Gaming influencers cover a wide range of content and niches, including:
  * Live streaming gameplay, from casual playthroughs to competitive esports. 
  * Creating reviews, tutorials, and reaction content around the latest games and tech. 
  * Showcasing gaming culture through challenges, collaborations and event & e-sport attendance. 
  * Engaging with their audience through interactive content, Q&As, and community events. 


While gaming started as a hobby, it’s now a dominant force in entertainment, and gaming influencers are leading the charge. With highly engaged audiences and strong community trust, they offer brands a direct route into one of the most passionate and loyal fan bases online. For brands in gaming, tech, lifestyle, sport or even food and beverage, collaborating with gaming creators is a powerful way to connect authentically and drive real impact.
### Angry Ginge (Morgan)
[**Angry Ginge**](https://www.instagram.com/angryginge13), real name Morgan Burtwistle, has cemented himself as one of the UK’s most recognisable gaming influencers. Rising through the ranks with his hilarious GTA Online content and larger-than-life persona, he quickly became a fan favourite for his grumpy but loveable character. His crossover into broader entertainment, including regular collaborations with the Sidemen and appearances at the iconic Sidemen Charity Matches, has helped him expand his reach far beyond gaming alone, earning him partnerships with some of the biggest names in gaming and e-sports, including Xbox and Red Bull.
Today, Angry Ginge blends gaming content, vlogging, and real-world football, where he now manages his own amateur team. With a growing presence across YouTube, TikTok, and Twitch, he represents the modern evolution of a top gaming influencer — versatile, culturally relevant, and deeply connected to his audience.

**What makes a “top gaming influencer” and how do I identify one?**
A top gaming influencer typically combines large follower counts and high engagement, credible content (gameplay, reviews, streams), and relevance to your brand. Look for creators who bring authenticity in the genre you target and whose community responds actively.
**How do I choose a gaming influencer for a campaign with a brand objective?**
Consider these factors when selecting a gaming influencer:Identify the influencer’s gaming niche (mobile, console, PC, specific genres).Check which platforms they create on (Twitch, YouTube, [TikTok](https://disruptmarketing.co/tiktok/), etc.).Review audience demographics (age, region, interests, relevance to your target market).Evaluate engagement quality, not just views but comments, chat activity and community interaction.Look at past brand partnerships and how well those campaigns performed.Ensure all of the above align with your brand’s goals, product and overall campaign strategy.
**What trends are shaping gaming influencer marketing that brands should be aware of?**
Key trends include:Rapid growth of mobile and cross-platform gaming audiences.Rising importance of micro-influencers and niche gaming creators.Increased integration of live gaming formats with brand activations and collaborations.Stronger emphasis on community-building rather than one-off sponsored posts.
**Can gaming influencer marketing work for non-gaming brands, and how?**
Yes, non-gaming brands can tap into gaming audiences by aligning with relevant creators (tech, lifestyle, accessories) and using gaming contexts (e.g., gear, livestream events, crossover content). The key is genuine fit between brand and creator.
