## When Modash is the best choice
Let me save you some time.
**If you agree with one or more of the following statements, you don’t need a Modash alternative at all** – because Modash is already the ideal solution:
### You’re a Shopify DTC brand with a small influencer team
If you…
✅ Sell direct to consumers on Shopify 
✅ Have a focused influencer team of ~1-5 people
✅ Treat influencer partnerships as a core acquisition channel, not a side experiment
…then Modash is perfect for you. You need a good Shopify integration for [influencer gifting](https://www.modash.io/blog/influencer-gifting), discount code generation, and sales attribution. Modash does all that stuff without forcing you to stitch together multiple tools.
### You’re scaling your influencer program 
You started out with ~10–50 influencers, but now you’re [scaling your influencer program](https://www.modash.io/blog/scale-influencer-marketing) to 100-300+ active partnerships – and your trusty spreadsheets are starting to crack under the weight. At this stage, things like…
✅ Discovery volume
✅ Repeatable workflows
✅ Operational efficiency 
…become more important than one-off campaigns or manual [influencer outreach](https://www.modash.io/blog/influencer-outreach). All areas in which Modash excels.
### You prioritize speed and cost-effectiveness over enterprise complexity
For many brands, speed and cost-effectiveness matter more than complex enterprise functionality. If you fall into this camp, then you’re looking to launch campaigns in a single day and iterate quickly. And you definitely want to steer clear of two to eight-week implementation periods, heavy onboarding, and wallet-busting enterprise pricing.
In other words: if you want serious capability without enterprise-level costs, _Modash just makes sense_.

## When a Modash alternative might make sense
Sure, I think Modash is the best thing since chili crunch, but that doesn’t mean it’s right for _everyone_.
Specifically, Modash likely isn’t a great fit if…
**📱 You require platform coverage beyond the “Big Three”:** Modash supports the three main influencer-friendly social platforms – Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. If your strategy depends heavily on Pinterest, Twitch, Facebook, blogs, or _something else_ , you’ll need a tool with broader platform coverage, like Upfluence or HypeAuditor.
‍**🤳 You're only buying content, not distribution:** If you need [UGC creation](https://www.modash.io/blog/tiktok-ugc) without influencer reach (_i.e. no posts to their audience_), simpler tools like Billo are purpose-built with this in mind.
‍**💼 You need true enterprise complexity:** If you're a large organization with complex approval hierarchies, custom integrations for business intelligence, and other advanced needs, tools like Traackr and CreatorIQ might be a better fit.
## The best Modash alternatives by use case
### GRIN: Best for AI copilot-assisted influencer marketing
  * **Influencer discovery:** Modash makes it easier to find the brand-fit influencers you need to reach your ideal audience. Our database is almost twice the size of GRIN’s, covering every influencer on the planet with 1,000+ followers on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. Plus our visual AI search tool lets you find creators based on _what’s inside the content_(e.g. you can search for “ _woman applying lipstick_ ”), not just what’s in the caption.


  * **AI agent assistance:** GRIN says its AI agent, Gia, can run an affiliate program on autopilot – including discovery, outreach, tracking, and optimization. There are no AI agents built natively inside Modash, but we _do_ use AI in our search tool to help you find more relevant influencers.


  * **Influencer management:** Unlike GRIN, you don’t need to ask influencers to sign up for Modash in order to track their content. Modash also features a more intuitive influencer CRM – for example, creator profiles can be viewed from anywhere in the platform.


  * **Landing pages:** GRIN gives you the tools to build basic landing pages for generating inbound influencer applications. You can’t currently do this in Modash.


  * **Influencer payments:** GRIN only supports payments via PayPal, potentially leading to extra fees for your influencer partners. Whereas with Modash Payments, your influencer partners get paid direct to their bank accounts.

### Upfluence: Best for enterprise brands looking for agency support
  * **Influencer database:** Upfluence has a much more limited influencer pool than Modash, with just 9+ million profiles in its “regular” database (plus over 12 million in its opt-in influencer marketplace). So you’ll likely need to purchase an additional tool alongside Upfluence to find brand-fit influencers.


  * **Platform coverage:** Both Upfluence and Modash cover the big three of Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. However, Upfluence also supports Twitch, X/Twitter, Pinterest, and blogs.


  * **Agency support:** Upfluence offers white-glove influencer marketing agency services – ideal for brands with lots of money that don’t want to run their own influencer program. Modash doesn’t do this.


  * **Ecommerce integrations:** Upfluence integrates with Adobe Commerce, BigCommerce, Shopify, and WooCommerce, whereas [Modash only has a Shopify integration](https://www.modash.io/shopify) (_it’s pretty slick though, allowing you to create promo codes, manage affiliate links, simplify influencer product orders, and more_).


  * **Influencer comms:** With Upfluence, all communication with influencers has to go through the platform, giving you _yet another inbox to check_. Whereas Modash works with your existing Gmail or Outlook account.


  * **Purchase risk:** Upfluence only offers annual plans. So if you find that it’s not working as expected, you’re locked into a year-long commitment. Modash, on the other hand, offers monthly plans – allowing you to downgrade, upgrade, or cancel at any time.

### CreatorIQ: Best for centralizing influencer marketing data via API
  * **Data management:** CreatorIQ has an API that lets you send influencer marketing data straight to your business intelligence tool so it can be centralized alongside your other marketing data (_paid media etc_). Modash doesn’t offer this (_although you can do a bunch of other stuff with our_[ _influencer marketing API_](https://www.modash.io/influencer-marketing-api)).


  * **Creator search:** CreatorIQ isn’t a great choice for finding brand-fit influencers at scale. It only contains ~20 million influencer profiles, compared to Modash’s database of 350+ million profiles. Users also say its search functionality [often returns irrelevant results](https://www.g2.com/products/creatoriq/reviews?utf8=%E2%9C%93&order=g2_default&filters%5Bsentiment_snippet%5D=1759984).


  * **Platform coverage:** While CreatorIQ’s search tools aren’t the best, you can use them to find influencers across a wide range of platforms (_Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, Twitch, X/Twitter, and YouTube_). Modash only covers the three main platforms of Insta, TikTok, and YouTube.


  * **Transparent pricing:** CreatorIQ doesn’t publish pricing information, although I’ve seen reports ranging from ~$2,300 per month to [$36,000 per year](https://www.capterra.com/p/169151/CreatorIQ/pricing/) for an entry-level plan 💸 That puts it out of range for smaller businesses. Modash, on the other hand, offers plans starting at just $299 per month.


  * **Competitor benchmarking:** With BenchmarkIQ from CreatorIQ, you can discover how your influencer program is performing vs your competitors across key metrics like share of voice, engagement, and exposure. Modash doesn’t offer any benchmarking capabilities.


  * **Self-serve purchase options:** CreatorIQ doesn’t offer a free plan or free trial, so the only way to learn more is to request a demo. If you’re like me, you’d rather do your own due diligence before speaking to a sales rep.

### Aspire: Best for low-touch influencer marketing
  * **Influencer database:** Aspire has a database of [170+ million influencers](https://www.aspire.io/find-influencers), which is roughly half the size of Modash’s database.


  * **Transparent pricing:** Aspire doesn’t publish pricing information, although estimates suggest a starting price of $2,000+ per month. Whereas Modash shares pricing details for all our standard plans, which start at just $299 per month.


  * **Low-touch solutions:** With Aspire’s creator marketplace, you can attract pitches from 1+ million influencers for a low-touch approach to influencer recruitment (_although it’s not exactly scalable_). Aspire also offers agency services for brands that want to completely outsource [influencer marketing](https://www.modash.io/blog/influencer-marketing). Modash doesn’t do either.


  * **Lookalike search:** [Aspire doesn’t support “lookalike” searches on Instagram](https://help.aspireiq.com/en/articles/6651099-social-search-changes), making it harder to find influencers who match your current best performers. Whereas Modash shows you lookalikes for each of an influencer’s profiles.


  * **Content tracking:** Aspire only tracks live content if an influencer tags the brand or submits it manually, which is a pain. Modash supports tagging too, of course. But we also automatically pull in all influencer content during a set time period – so you can be sure you’ll never miss a post.


  * **Platform coverage:** Both tools cover Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube, but Aspire _also_ lets you search for influencers on Pinterest.


  * **Influencer payments:** With Aspire, payments are sent via PayPal in US dollars – so if your influencer partners use a different currency, they’ll be charged a conversion fee on their agreed rates. By contrast, Modash supports 36 currencies across 180 countries.

Instead, you’ll have to ask your influencer partners to share it themselves – which can be pretty time-consuming at scale. Modash does all this automatically.


  * **Influencer database:** Both platforms have _huge_ influencer databases, but Modash has the edge here with 350+ million profiles vs 300+ million.


  * **Social media management:** Unlike Modash, Influencity offers built-in social media management – allowing you to schedule posts, track performance, and manage messages from a single platform. Of course, if you only want an influencer marketing solution, you could end up paying for something you don’t need.

### Shopify Collabs: Best for basic Shopify influencer marketing
  * **Influencer database:** Unlike Modash with our database of 350+ million influencers, Shopify Collabs doesn’t say how many influencer profiles it offers – just that you can search “millions” of creators. At time of writing, Collabs wasn’t accepting any new influencer signups, so the database isn’t growing any time soon.


  * **Scalability:** Shopify Collabs only lets you invite up to 100 influencers in any seven-day period. If you’re recruiting at scale, you could easily bump up against this. Modash scales as you need it to; if you hit our limits on team members, opened profiles, unlocked emails, or tracked creators, you can simply upgrade to a higher plan.


  * **Free functionality:** It’s totally free to get started with Shopify Collabs, making it a popular choice for smaller brands with lower budgets. While Modash offers a free trial, it doesn’t have a free-for-life plan.


  * **Ongoing fees:** That said, Shopify Collabs isn’t as “free” as it initially appears, charging an additional 2.9% payment processing fee on influencer-affiliate commission payments. If you’ve got hundreds of influencers driving thousands of sales, these fees can really add up – in which case you’re better off with Modash’s fixed monthly plans.


  * **Platform support:** Shopify Collabs supports more social platforms than Modash (_Insta, TikTok, and YouTube, plus three extras – Facebook, Twitch, and X/Twitter_). On the downside, it’s only for Shopify stores, whereas any brand or business can use Modash.

### Traackr: Best for competitor benchmarking 
  * **Influencer database:** Traackr’s database only features [7+ million influencer profiles](https://www.traackr.com/blog/how-your-agency-can-build-an-influencer-program-your-client-will-love). That’s about 50 times smaller than Modash 🤏


  * **Platform coverage:** Although its database is comparatively small, Traackr lets you search for influencers across 13 social platforms (_it doesn’t list them all, though_). Modash only covers Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.


  * **Benchmarking:** Traackr shares data and insights about top competitors for brands in five industries – beauty, personal care, fashion, spirits, and consumer electronics. Modash doesn’t offer any comparable data or insights.


  * **Opt-in requirement:** Influencers have to opt in to campaigns on Traackr. If they don’t, you won’t be able to brief them, track their content, or pay them. Modash doesn’t require opt-ins, making for a smoother journey for your influencer partners.

Modash pricing starts at just $299 per month (_or $199 per month on an annual contract_).
