### **Science-Backed & Educational Fitness YouTubers**
The nerds are winning, and thank goodness for that. These creators prove that fitness content doesn’t have to choose between being smart and being popular.
####  **Jeff Cavaliere (ATHLEAN-X)**
**14.1M subscribers | Joined 2006**
Jeff turned his physical therapy background into YouTube gold by **making complex anatomy accessible to regular gym-goers**. His [“face pulls for rear delts” videos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ljgqer1ZpXg) have probably prevented more shoulder injuries than a lifetime of gym safety posters!
What trainers can learn: **Credibility sells itself when you can explain the**** _why_****behind each exercise**. Jeff’s success shows that people crave education, not just entertainment.

#### **Jeremy Ethier**
**7.21M subscribers | Joined 2016**
Jeremy’s biomechanics content proves you can be incredibly smart without being incredibly boring. He takes complex movement science and packages it into **actionable workout tips that actually work**.
His rapid growth shows there’s huge demand for **evidence-based content**. Trainers who can bridge the gap between research and practical application have a massive competitive advantage. 
#### **Greg Doucette**
**2.34M subscribers | Joined 2006**
Greg’s no-nonsense approach to **calling out fitness BS** has earned him a cult following. His blunt style might not be for everyone, but his **commitment to honesty** resonates with people tired of fitness industry snake oil.
The lesson? **Authenticity beats polish every time**. Greg’s success proves that being genuinely helpful matters more than having perfect production quality.
#### **Natacha Océane**
**1.66M subscribers | Joined 2015**
This biophysicist-turned-fitness creator **destroys fitness myths with hard research**. She’s built a devoted following by treating her audience like intelligent humans who can handle nuanced discussions about training and nutrition.
And [although she’s taking a hiatus from YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5ez0eKAuCOk&t=1s), her library of fitness content proves you don’t need to dumb things down to build an audience. **Smart people want smart content** , and they’ll pay premium prices for trainers who can deliver it.
#### **Abby Pollock**
**1.09M subscribers | Joined 2013**
Abby applies her engineering background to fitness, creating incredibly practical content about how to train efficiently. She’s the queen of **“work smarter, not harder”** advice that busy people actually want to hear.
Her approach shows that **unique perspectives create unique value**. Think about it: _what background or expertise do_**you** _bring that could set your content apart?_

### **Strength & Weight Training Fitness YouTubers**
Strength training doesn’t have to be boring. These creators prove it. 
#### **Tibo InShape**
**26.9M subscribers | Joined 2008**
This French bodybuilder has cracked the code on **making strength training genuinely fun to watch**. His challenge videos and high-energy personality have built one of the largest fitness audiences on the platform.
The takeaway for trainers: **Personality matters as much as programming** , and **energy is contagious**. Tibo shows that people follow _people_ , not just workout routines. 
#### **Chris Heria**
**5.29M subscribers | Joined 2013**
Chris made calisthenics cool again by focusing on **bodyweight skills that make you genuinely athletic** , not just muscle for show. His content brings together street workout culture with legit training principles—a true reflection of his Miami roots. 
His success highlights **the power of niching down**. Chris owns the calisthenics space and has built a community around it.
#### **Lee Weiland**
**243K subscribers | Joined 2017**
Lee’s content feels more like **watching a Netflix documentary than a workout video**. His production quality and clear instruction style represent the emerging premium tier of fitness content.
This shows where the industry is heading—clients expect more than clear workout instructions. They want **higher production values and more thoughtful content creation**. Trainers who can**teach and entertain simultaneously** will dominate their markets.

### **Women’s Fitness & Empowerment Fitness YouTubers**
These creators have built empires by understanding that women want more than just “bikini body” workouts.
#### **Chloe Ting**
**25.8M subscribers | Joined 2011**
Chloe’s home workout challenges have created a **global fitness movement**. Her “2 Week Shred” programs prove that you don’t need fancy equipment to build an engaged community.
The genius here is **accessibility**. Chloe removes every barrier to getting started—**no gym, no equipment, no excuses**. That’s a masterclass in meeting your audience where they’re at.
#### **Sydney Cummings Houdyshell**
**2.02M subscribers | Joined 2016**
Sydney’s journey from Division I track athlete to YouTube sensation shows the power of **purpose-driven content**. After losing her younger brother in 2017, she decided to create a fitness legacy that removes barriers to training.
The lesson for trainers: **Personal stories create deeper connections than perfect abs ever will**. Sydney’s transparency about loss, career changes, and chasing passion resonates because it’s real. Clients don’t just want your expertise—they want to know _why_ you do what you do.
#### **Heidi Somers (BuffBunny)**
**811K subscribers | Joined 2008**
Heidi turned her YouTube success into a **multi-million dollar fitness apparel brand**. Her content mixes workout tips with lifestyle and business insights.
She’s proof that **YouTube can be a launching pad for bigger business ventures**. Smart trainers are already thinking about how their content can support [multiple revenue streams](https://www.trainerize.com/blog/secondary-revenue-stream/).
### **Fun & Entertaining Fitness YouTubers**
Sometimes the best workout is the one that doesn’t feel like work. These creators have mastered the art of making fitness genuinely enjoyable—and oftentimes, hilarious! 
#### **Maddie Lymburner (MadFit)**
**10.6M subscribers | Joined 2018**
Maddie has taken dance workouts up a notch by making them **accessible anytime, anywhere**. Her videos feel more like dance parties than exercise— _perfect_ for people who dread traditional workouts (AKA me). 
The business lesson: Instead of just creating dance workouts, **she reinvented how they should look and feel on YouTube**. Her example just goes to show you don’t have to invent a completely new category to succeed online. Sometimes you just need to make it look and feel different than how others are doing it. 
#### **Browney**
**10M subscribers | Joined 2013**
For this German creator, **humor and fitness are a mad combo**. His playful approach hooks younger audiences who typically scroll past traditional fitness content.
The lesson: **Serious results don’t require serious content.** So don’t be afraid to be playful! Browney shows that fitness content can be educational without being so serious all the time. 
#### **Vladimir Shmondenko (ANATOLY)**
**8.56M subscribers | Joined 2022**
This powerlifter’s **prank videos** —where he pretends to be a cleaner before deadlifting enormous weights—have gone massively viral. His content proves that surprise and skill create **viral moments**.
**Surprise and entertainment value still rule YouTube**. Vladimir’s genius lies in capturing attention first, _then_ demonstrating credibility to sell training programs. Smart trainers can apply this by leading with personality before diving into technique.
#### **The Fitness Marshall**
**6.93M subscribers | Joined 2014**
Caleb’s dance cardio workouts are **pure joy in video form**! His infectious energy and body-positive messaging have created a community [where everyone feels welcome to move their body](https://www.trainerize.com/blog/marketing-to-the-lgbtq-community-guide-for-gyms-and-fitness-studios/), just as they are. 
It’s true: **inclusivity and fun can be super effective business strategies**. People want to feel good about fitness, not intimidated by it.

**13.3M subscribers | Joined 2012**
Next, Adriene has basically become the internet’s yoga teacher, making yoga feel like hanging out with your most zen friend. Her “30 Days of Yoga” series quickly took off because she wanted **more people to benefit from the power of yoga** —whether or not they could attend [in-person classes](https://www.trainerize.com/blog/unique-ways-to-sell-online-personal-training/). 
The business gold here? **She treats viewers like friends, not students.** That personal connection has built incredible loyalty and trust with her massive audience. When people trust you, they’ll follow you anywhere.
#### **Cassey Ho (Blogilates)**
**10.8M subscribers | Joined 2009**
Cassey has turned Pilates into a **full-on lifestyle brand** , mixing workouts with fashion, food, and personal development. She’s one of the OG fitness YouTubers who’s stayed relevant for over a decade, which is no small feat! 
Her secret sauce?**Constant evolution**. She’s continuously expanding her content and business model to build entire ecosystems around her expertise. The lesson for trainers: **think bigger than just workouts.**
#### **SarahBethYoga**
**1.79M subscribers | Joined 2010**
Next, Sarah has built her channel around the idea that yoga isn’t just about getting bendy—**it’s about feeling better in every aspect of your life**. Her flows combine physical practice with real talk about mental health and self-care.
What’s brilliant about her approach? She’s tapped into what people actually want: **complete wellness, not just another workout**. Sarah shows that the future belongs to trainers who understand [fitness is just one piece of the puzzle](https://www.trainerize.com/blog/longevity-coaching/).
