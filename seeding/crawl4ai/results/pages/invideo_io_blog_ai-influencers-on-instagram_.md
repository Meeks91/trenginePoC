## **What is an AI Influencer?**
AI influencers are digital humans created using artificial intelligence technology to be positioned and promoted as real human influencers. They carry a specific personality, create content around certain topics, and if they garner good engagement and following, they might as well attract brand collaborations. 
Just like real human influencers, they engage with the audience on social media through their content. They create a sense of belongingness, which is necessary to create and build a sense of trust and relatability among the audience.

## **What Makes a Great AI Influencer in Marketing?**
While the idea of AI influencers sounds great, and that's true to a good extent, it’s important to understand what makes an “ideal” one. This section explores what really makes an AI influencer work in a way that feels right, builds trust, and gets the desired results!
### **Consistent Persona**
Every influencer you may know carries a certain vibe. The same should go for AI influencers, too. If they don’t have a clear personality or keep changing their tone and message, it becomes confusing and untrustworthy. This can be their tone of voice, values, which has to stay consistent across platforms, so that people recognize them and build a strong connection.
### **Adaptability**
Just as adaptability is important for human influencers, the same goes for AI influencers as well. We get to see new memes, topics, and formats always popping up, so if an AI influencer doesn’t evolve with these changes, they’ll quickly feel outdated. Staying static is not an option here because people lose interest in things that just feel stuck in time.
### **Transparency**
Here’s something people often get wrong: trying to make the AI influencer seem completely human. But in reality, most people can tell when someone isn’t real, and trying to hide it just feels dishonest. What actually builds trust is being transparent. It doesn’t need to be over-explained, but it should be clear in some way that the influencer is digital. 
In fact, many followers find the idea of AI influencers fascinating, as long as it’s presented the right way. Think to yourself: when you know you’re following a virtual character, you’d engage with it differently. Trying to act too “real” breaks that experience. But owning the fact that they’re virtual, even casually, makes them easier to accept.
### **Brand Integration**
No matter how cool an AI influencer looks or sounds, if they can’t bring value to a brand, they’re not doing their job. Brands look for influencers who can connect with their audience, share messages in a way that fits the brand tone, and help drive awareness or sales. A good AI influencer can do all of that, but only if they’re designed to be easy to work with. 
Shudu, for example, recently [collaborated with “The Digitals”](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DGV3eLiqkdg/?igsh=cmRmYjFienczd3c5) for an innovative spring-themed campaign. This shows how AI influencers are actually now an active part of brand endorsements and collaborations, which can be a good inspiration to take from.
Source: [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DGV3eLiqkdg/?igsh=cmRmYjFienczd3c5)
She's customizable to brand needs, which shows how AI influencers can actually bring genuine value to a brand’s marketing efforts. They should be part of the strategy, and that's exactly why it's important to emphasize the brand value you can leverage with the help of an AI influencer.
