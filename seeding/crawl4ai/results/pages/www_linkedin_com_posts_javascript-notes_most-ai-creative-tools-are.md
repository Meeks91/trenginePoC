Most “AI creative tools” are just prompt boxes with better branding. They generate outputs. They don’t support real creative work. If your workflow looks like this: idea → prompt → regenerate → tweak → still off → abandon That’s not creativity. That’s roulette. I came across Anora today, and it actually made me pause. Not because it’s “another AI model” — but because it treats creativity like a system, not a one-off prompt. Anora brings text, image, and video models into a single, intuitive workspace where everything connects. The real shift is this 👇 You’re not just asking AI for results. You’re codifying your creative process into something repeatable. Ideate. Iterate. Scale. However you want. Instead of losing great ideas after one good session, you can turn them into workflows that faithfully execute your vision. This feels like the move from: ❌ one-off outputs to ✅ durable creative systems Tools built for real creative work, not AI slop, tend to age very well.
