User-generated content (UGC) creators have become one of the fastest-growing segments in influencer marketing. In fact, [Collabstr’s 2025 Influencer Marketing Report ](https://collabstr.com/2025-influencer-marketing-report)shows a 93% year-over-year surge in [UGC creators](https://collabstr.com/blog/ugc-creator), proving that brands are increasingly shifting budgets toward authentic, relatable content produced by everyday creators.
To help you get started with UGC for your own brand, we’re sharing quick stats on UGC trends and the best options for influencer marketing tools to hire creators and manage campaigns more effectively.
  * UGC creators have surged 93% year-over-year, dropping pricing
  * Brands are favouring authentic, relatable voices
  * Who's the best influencer marketing tool for UGC creators 


This year we analyzed data from 40,000 advertisers and 100,000 creators. What the data shows makes it clear: the UGC creator economy is expanding rapidly. For brands, that means favorable pricing, shifts in top-performing niches, and more opportunities to test content across platforms.
  1. **UGC Costs Are Dropping** The average price for UGC content decreased by 44.37% year-over-year. This dramatic shift is largely driven by an increase in creator supply, as more individuals enter the UGC space.


For brands, the impact is significant: UGC is now one of the most cost-effective formats in influencer marketing. Compared to paying thousands for a celebrity post, brands can commission multiple pieces of authentic UGC content for the same budget—content that can be repurposed into ads, emails, and social campaigns.
  1. **Stable Pricing Compared to Other Niches** UGC also shows the smallest discrepancy between asking and actual price (10.29%) compared to other influencer categories. In practice, this means brands are paying close to what creators ask, leading to more predictable budgeting.


The average spend per UGC creator is $177.68, which gives small businesses and agencies more flexibility to experiment with creators across different platforms. Instead of negotiating unpredictable rates, marketers can plan campaigns with confidence.

  1. **Shift in Top-Paid Niches**


In 2023, the highest-paid influencer niches were Acting, Celebrity & Public Figures, and Entrepreneurship. By 2024, however, there was a major shift: LGBTQ+, Comedy & Entertainment, and Vegan content rose to the top.
This reflects a broader consumer demand for diverse, authentic voices—creators who can connect with audiences through humor, lifestyle content, or values-driven storytelling. For brands, investing in these niches signals a shift toward relatable, community-driven campaigns rather than aspirational celebrity marketing.
  1. **UGC Market Share is Rising**


UGC campaigns now account for 15% of all influencer collaborations on Collabstr. That’s a significant increase compared to previous years, and it underscores the mainstream adoption of UGC as a reliable marketing channel.
Agencies in particular are embracing UGC because it scales: one campaign can involve dozens of creators, generating hundreds of content assets that fuel paid ads, organic content, and product launches.
The rise of UGC has created demand for platforms that make it easier to discover, hire, and manage creators. Instead of spending hours on manual outreach, influencer marketing tools allow brands to streamline everything from searching creator profiles to contracting and payment.
A UGC influencer platform is a marketplace or software tool that helps brands connect with creators, commission authentic content, and manage collaborations at scale. Instead of manually searching Instagram, TikTok, or LinkedIn for creators, these platforms act as central hubs where advertisers can:
  * Search and filter creators by niche, location, audience size, or content style.
  * Review creator profiles and pricing to understand deliverables up front.
  * Contract and pay creators securely, without messy back-and-forth over email or DM.
  * Receive and organize UGC content, often with built-in tools for approval and feedback.
  * Track campaign performance, like engagement, reach, or conversions.


For small businesses especially, UGC platforms make influencer marketing far more accessible. Rather than needing an agency or in-house influencer manager, a self-serve UGC platform allows a founder or small marketing team to quickly hire creators, test content, and scale what works.
