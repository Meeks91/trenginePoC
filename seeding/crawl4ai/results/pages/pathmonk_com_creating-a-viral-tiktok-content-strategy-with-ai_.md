## How does the TikTok algorithm work?
Let’s start with the basics. **TikTok’s algorithm** plays a key role in determining who sees your content and how far it spreads, which can make or break a content strategy. 
Unlike traditional social media algorithms that prioritize followers, TikTok’s focuses on content relevance and engagement, meaning **even small accounts have the chance to go viral** if their videos meet certain criteria. Here are the main factors TikTok considers when pushing content:

### 1. Watch time
**TikTok rewards videos that keep users engaged.** If your video’s watch time is high, it’s a signal to the algorithm that the content is interesting or entertaining. Videos with high completion rates (i.e., viewers who watch until the end) are more likely to be promoted to a wider audience. So, your content needs to hook viewers from the very beginning.
Also, when a viewer watches a video multiple times, it tells TikTok that the content is valuable enough to re-watch. This can help boost the video’s visibility, as repeated views show higher viewer interest.
### 2. Engagement rate
Engagement metrics are key indicators that a video resonates with the audience. **Every like, comment, or share reinforces the value of the content** , prompting TikTok to share it with a larger group. Each type of engagement is weighted slightly differently, with comments and shares having more influence than likes alone.
TikTok also pays attention to the quality of interactions though. For instance, thoughtful comments or meaningful conversations within the comment section suggest strong viewer interest, and TikTok is more likely to boost these videos.
### 3. Video information and relevancy
Some technical bits that are taken into account by the algorithm include:
  * **Captions and hashtags:** TikTok’s algorithm reads captions and hashtags to understand the topic of a video. Choosing the right keywords and relevant hashtags (especially trending ones) helps TikTok categorize your content accurately, so it reaches the right audience.
  * **Sounds and effects:** Popular sounds and effects increase a video’s chances of being shared, as TikTok tends to promote content that aligns with current platform trends. Using trending audio clips or visual effects helps your videos stay relevant within TikTok’s ecosystem.
  * **Editing quality:** TikTok prioritizes high-quality videos with seamless editing, clear visuals, and good sound. While there’s flexibility in style, polished videos with high visual standards tend to hold viewers’ attention longer, boosting watch time and engagement.


### 4. User activity and personalization
TikTok’s algorithm learns about**user preferences based on their past interactions**. It customizes each user’s ‘For You’ feed by matching content to their viewing and engagement history. For example, if someone frequently interacts with fitness content, TikTok will prioritize similar content in their feed, giving fitness brands a natural way to connect with relevant audiences.
There are other factors influencing the algorithm, such as language preference, location, and even device, but these are the main ones explaining why you see what you see.
## How to use AI for video content creation
### 1. Discover trending topics and hashtags
Staying relevant on TikTok requires**tapping into current trends and popular hashtags**. AI-powered tools can identify these trends as they emerge, ensuring you’re not missing out on opportunities to join conversations that matter to your audience.
Tools like [**BuzzSumo**](https://buzzsumo.com/) , [**VidIQ**](http://vidiq) , and [**TrendTok**](https://www.forusapps.com/trendtokapp) track social media trends across different platforms, including TikTok. BuzzSumo, for example, uses AI to analyze massive amounts of content and detect rising topics. With it, you can see which keywords, hashtags, and themes are growing popular, so you can quickly align your content with audience interests.
### 2. Generate content ideas and scripts
Coming up with **unique content ideas and creating scripts** is time-consuming. AI-driven tools like [**ChatGPT**](https://chat.openai.com/) , [**Copy.ai**](http://copy.ai) , and [**Jasper**](https://www.jasper.ai/) can generate fresh content ideas when you input keywords or topics. For instance, by entering “DIY skincare tips,” these tools might suggest video ideas like “5 Surprising Ingredients You Can Use in Face Masks” or “Why Your Skin Type Matters in DIY Skincare.” Each suggestion is crafted to grab attention and match TikTok’s short-form content style.
They can also help with creating quick and engaging scripts for your videos. With Copy.ai, you can input key points you want to cover, and the AI generates an outline or full script. For example, if you want to discuss eco-friendly travel, the tool can create a script flow that keeps the message concise and engaging—perfect for TikTok’s fast-paced format.
### 3. Streamline video editing 
Editing can be a real time sink, especially if you’re not a pro. Thankfully, **AI-powered video editors** make it a whole lot easier to create polished, professional videos without needing deep editing skills.
Tools like [**InVideo**](https://invideo.io/) , [**Pictory**](https://pictory.ai/) , and [**Magisto**](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.magisto&hl=es&pli=1) help simplify the editing process, automatically removing filler words, adjusting pacing, and adding transitions. InVideo, for instance, lets you just drag and drop clips, while it handles cuts and effects, so your video flows better and keeps viewers engaged. 
These tools are especially handy if you’re trying to keep a consistent, high-quality look across all your videos without getting bogged down in manual editing.
**Adding subtitles or a voiceover can really elevate your content** , and tools like [**Descript**](https://www.descript.com/) and [**Zubtitle**](https://zubtitle.com/) make it simple. Descript, for example, even lets you edit your video by just editing the transcript, so you can make fast, on-the-go changes. Plus, automatic subtitles boost accessibility and engagement, which is key for reaching viewers who watch with the sound off.
### 4. Analyze performance and refine content strategy
**Knowing what works and what doesn’t** is essential for keeping your content fresh and effective. So, here too, AI can help you massively giving you in-depth insights into your video’s performance so you can keep fine-tuning your approach and boosting engagement.
Tools like [**Sprout Social**](https://sproutsocial.com/es/) provide detailed data on important metrics like watch time, engagement rates, and click-throughs. These insights show you which videos are capturing attention and which might need adjustments. 
Predictive AI tools like [**Emplifi.io**](http://emplifi.io) and [**Crimson Hexagon**](https://www.brandwatch.com/p/crimson-hexagon/) (now part of Brandwatch) can even suggest specific tweaks. Emplifi, for example, might reveal that videos with an early call-to-action perform better, helping you make small adjustments that could lead to better conversions.
### 5. Automate posting and scheduling
Timing is everything on TikTok. AI-powered scheduling tools analyze when your audience is most active and help you post at peak times, ensuring your videos get maximum visibility.
[**Hootsuite**](https://www.hootsuite.com/es?srsltid=AfmBOop5_Vz3GXFB6ro5pVu_IOGq0j9UZqCH6GnYMDOtCPvkH6FZ_hv5) , and [**Buffer**](https://buffer.com/) look at audience activity patterns to recommend the best times for posting. Later even lets you batch-schedule your content, so it automatically posts when engagement is likely to be highest, helping you keep a consistent schedule without having to be online all the time.
If you’re repurposing TikTok videos for other platforms, tools like [**Adobe Express Content Scheduler**](https://www.adobe.com/express/feature/content-scheduler) (formerly ContentCal) are great for managing cross-platform schedules. ContentCal’s AI can even suggest the best times and channels for each video, making it simple to extend your reach without creating extra work.

## Creating content that engages and converts: 20 AI-powered prompts
Staying relevant on TikTok can be a challenge with trends shifting rapidly, and coming up with fresh, engaging ideas is a constant demand. ChatGPT can be a powerful tool in your content creation toolkit, acting as a creative partner to generate ideas, build engaging scripts, and even help refine final touches—all with the goal of creating content that stands out and drives conversions.
Let’s get our hands dirty 👇
### 1. How to brainstorm unique and trend-ready ideas
**Start with a broad prompt** , like “Suggest some unique TikTok video ideas for promoting [product],” and ChatGPT can provide a range of suggestions—whether it’s fun challenges, educational content, or product highlights—that fit with your brand’s tone and audience preferences.
Here are some examples of prompts you could use with ChatGPT to spark creative ideas:
  * “How can I jump on TikTok trends to build engagement for [goal, like raising awareness or increasing followers]?”
  * “Suggest fresh, creative ways to promote [product/service] on TikTok that will stand out.”
  * “How can I create a viral TikTok video about [topic] that’s engaging and unique?”
  * “What are some ideas for TikTok videos that educate about [topic] while keeping the content entertaining?”
  * “Come up with a TikTok content plan for the next month to boost awareness around [topic/product]”
  * “What are some TikTok video ideas that will help build trust in [brand/product] and engage viewers?”
  * “What are some visually interesting ways to showcase product features of [product] on TikTok?”
  * “How can I create an interactive TikTok series around [topic] that keeps viewers coming back for more?”


These prompts can serve as the basis for trend-aware ideas that make it easier to consistently create content that resonates with TikTok’s audience.
### 2. How to develop strong storytelling concepts
**Let’s hook your viewers**. By feeding ChatGPT with some background information—like your brand’s style, key messaging, or a bit about your target audience—you can get ideas that are more tailored and impactful.
To guide storytelling, try prompts like:
  * “I want to tell an engaging story about [product/service] on TikTok. Suggest 3 concepts, 3 simple scripts, and some relevant hashtags”
  * “Help me create a TikTok challenge around [topic] that will engage users and encourage participation. What’s a good name, concept, and some hashtag ideas?”
  * “Suggest 5 ways to share customer success stories on TikTok that are fun, engaging, and highlight [product/service benefits].”
  * I want to create a TikTok video that tells a nostalgic story related to [topic]. Outline 3 concepts and the main points to hit”
  * “Suggest 3 ways to share the origin story of [brand/product] on TikTok in a way that feels personal and connects with viewers


### 3. How to write scripts that capture attention
Once you have a solid concept, ChatGPT can help turn it into a well-structured TikTok script. From educational videos to product promotions, **a strong script is essential for engaging viewers and prompting them to take action.**
Examples of script prompts include:
  * “Write a short script for a TikTok video that promotes [product] in a fun and informative way.”
  * “I want to create a viral TikTok about [topic]. Write a script that’s engaging and easy to follow”
  * “Help me write a TikTok script that uses humor to promote [brand/product/service] and is relatable to viewers.”


Scripts generated by ChatGPT can serve as a draft, allowing you to add a unique twist, adjust language to fit your brand’s voice, and ensure the final product is just right.
### 4. How to refine your content for a polished, professional outcome 
Once you’ve nailed down a script, ChatGPT can help with the **final details that make a video stand out**. From scene suggestions to organizing the content flow, ChatGPT’s capabilities make it easy to polish the entire video concept, ensuring it’s clear, compelling, and ready to engage.
Some ways ChatGPT can help with refinement include:
  * **Scene suggestions** : “Give me a few different ideas for scenes in this TikTok script to make it visually engaging.”
  * **Combining script and scene Ideas** : “Organize the script with these scene ideas to create a storyboard.”
  * **Formatting the final video outline** : “Put this script into a table format with columns for scene, setting, voiceover, and any suggested text animations.”
  * **Getting detailed descriptions** : “Provide a detailed description of each scene, focusing on what the viewer should see and hear.”


By combining script elements with detailed scene and formatting suggestions, ChatGPT helps you build a clear and cohesive content plan that’s ready for production.
## Step-by-step: the art of creating a TikTok script with ChatGPT
Now that you have the fundamental knowledge and general concept for crafting a script, let’s put everything together and:
  * Polish a script outline.
  * Generate scene suggestions.
  * Define a breakdown of the scene and script information.


### 1. Create your script
Develop the core content of your TikTok—the script itself. Think of this as the heart of your video, where you’ll nail down the main message, essential product features, and any must-have details.
‘Write a 30-second script for a TikTok video to promote a [product description] that contains [product specification]. Include the following [value propositions]. Make sure to include CTA.’
This prompt will generate a concise, engaging script that brings your product’s best qualities forward, wrapping up with a clear call to action, like “Tap the link to learn more” or “Follow us for more tips.”
Once you have this basic script, experiment by asking ChatGPT for variations. You can request a more humorous tone, an inspirational spin, or even a version that’s a bit more direct, depending on what feels right for your brand.
### 2. Request 5 different scene ideas
This is the stage where you’ll bring the video to life through dynamic scenes that match the message and mood of your script.
‘Provide 5 scene ideas for this script.’
These scene ideas might include suggestions for different settings, like showcasing your product in a cozy, at-home environment if it’s a lifestyle item, or a sleek, tech-inspired backdrop if it’s a high-tech gadget. 
ChatGPT can also suggest transitions that give the video a natural flow—maybe switching between a wide shot of the product in use to a close-up that highlights a key detail. 
You can even experiment by asking for mood variations, like one idea with a humorous angle and another with an inspirational touch, so you can decide which best captures the vibe you’re going for.
### 3. Combine the script ideas and the scenes together
With your script and scene ideas in place, the next step is to merge them into a cohesive narrative. This is where your TikTok’s storyline takes shape, as each scene seamlessly transitions to the next, guiding viewers through your message from start to finish.
‘Combine the script ideas and the scenes.’
During this phase, ChatGPT will match parts of your script to specific scenes, ensuring each scene complements what’s being said. This process creates a natural rhythm where visuals add depth to the voiceover, enhancing the story you’re telling. 
As the scenes unfold, viewers get drawn deeper into the narrative, making each second of your video count. The aim here is to craft a beginning, middle, and end, keeping the pace quick but meaningful, so your audience remains engaged throughout.
### 4. Make it easy: get some format
With the script and scenes now aligned, the next step is to bring structure to your video by organizing everything in a clear format.
‘Format this into a table including scene – setting – voiceover – description – text animation’
This step will allow you to see how each piece fits together, making it easy to visualize the final product. Having your video planned out in a table also lets you quickly spot any adjustments you might want to make, like adding or shortening certain elements to keep things on track.
### 5. Feel like you need more detailed information?
If your video still feels like it could use a bit more depth, ask ChatGPT to expand a bit more. With this, you’ll get extra detail for each scene, making it easier to direct any on-screen actions, define specific camera angles, or set a consistent tone.
‘Can you provide a more detailed description of each scene in the table?’
By now, your TikTok script is fully fleshed out, with each part adding to the story you want to tell.
## Brands succeeding with TikTok: Gymshark
, the popular fitness apparel brand, has always focused on building a strong social media community. Their target audience includes Gen Z and millennials, so embracing TikTok felt like a natural move to extend their reach. 
To make the most of TikTok’s unique platform, **Gymshark launched the #gymshark66 challenge** , asking followers to commit to 66 days of working toward their fitness goals. This challenge created massive engagement, with people posting updates to track their progress, which amplified Gymshark’s brand visibility.
The campaign also included **authentic partnerships with fitness influencers** who embodied Gymshark’s community-oriented values. The influencers created motivational content that felt more like personal encouragement than sponsored posts, helping the challenge grow even further. 
As a result, Gymshark saw a significant surge in sales, with many followers purchasing apparel to join in. The #gymshark66 hashtag ultimately brought millions of views, solidifying Gymshark’s presence on TikTok and setting the stage for continued long-term growth. Their success highlights how creative, community-driven campaigns on TikTok can resonate deeply, leading to both engagement and real sales.

was **among the first beauty brands to see TikTok’s potential,** leveraging the platform to connect with a younger, socially engaged audience and drive impressive sales.

Elf saw their e-commerce sales rise, especially from younger demographics, proving that a mix of unique music, influencer support, and interactive content can build viral momentum and measurable sales growth.
