Created by the LA-based tech studio Brud, she has become one of the most recognizable virtual faces on the internet. With millions of Instagram followers and partnerships with global powerhouses such as Prada and Calvin Klein, Miquela is redefining what it means to be an influencer in the era of AI.
But here’s the twist—she’s not real, at least, not in the traditional sense. Despite being computer-generated, Miquela lives a life that feels all too familiar. She posts selfies, shares fashion inspiration, attends music events, and even speaks out on social causes. Her feed is a mix of glam shoots, chill hangs with friends, and heartfelt messages about things that matter.
Fashion and music are her main playgrounds. She’s graced magazine covers, starred in ad campaigns, and released her songs, showing the world that a virtual persona can be just as stylish, creative, and culturally relevant as any human influencer.
What makes Miquela truly stand out is her voice. She doesn’t shy away from activism, championing movements like Black Lives Matter, LGBTQ+ rights, and climate action. In doing so, she bridges the digital and human worlds with empathy, relevance, and flair.
Lil Miquela isn’t just a CGI character. She’s a symbol of where tech meets culture, and a glimpse into the future of influence, one that’s pixel-perfect and purpose-driven.

What makes Shudu truly fascinating is how she opens up conversations about inclusivity, authenticity, and how technology is reshaping our visual culture. She’s not just modeling clothes—she’s modeling the future.
Through Shudu, Wilson didn’t just create a virtual icon—he ignited a movement, blending tech and style to inspire a new era of digital fashion.

Liam isn’t just a digital avatar—he’s a personality. A vibe. Proof that virtual influencers can have just as much flavor, fandom, and flair as their human counterparts.
