# Micro Influencer
A **micro-influencer** refers to a social media influencer with a follower count typically ranging from 10,000 to 50,000. These influencers have a strong connection with their audience and are seen as experts or enthusiasts in specific niches, making their endorsements highly credible and influential.
Micro-influencers offer brands the opportunity to reach engaged, targeted audiences. They strike a balance between high engagement rates and broader reach compared to __, making their endorsements both impactful and authentic.
The success of collaborations with micro-influencers is measured through key metrics such as:
  * **Engagement rates** (likes, comments, shares)
  * **Reach** and **impressions**
  * **Conversion rates**


These metrics help brands gauge how the influencer’s content impacts awareness, audience engagement, and sales.
For example, a fitness brand may collaborate with a **micro-influencer** who has 25,000 followers on [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/adparlor/) and frequently posts workout tips and healthy lifestyle content. The influencer shares a series of Instagram reels featuring the brand’s new line of workout gear, driving high engagement and interest among their fitness-focused audience. The brand can further amplify its reach by promoting the reel through branded content.
