## **2026 Creator Trends Brands Need to Watch**
Creators are shaping culture faster than ever, and 2026 is already showing clear shifts in how influencers grow, connect, and collaborate. Here are the trends driving the creator landscape this year — and the ones your brand should pay attention to.
### **1. Niche > Numbers (Again)**
Follower count keeps mattering less. Hyper-specific creators with tight communities — from micro skin-care reviewers to niche travel storytellers — are outperforming broad lifestyle influencers. Brands want relevance, not reach.
**What it means for collabs:** Expect more campaigns built around micro influencers who can move their audience with trust, not scale.
### **2. Short-Form Doesn’t Slow Down**
Reels, [TikTok](https://ecommercefastlane.com/recommends/tiktok/ "Tiktok"), and Shorts continue to dominate discovery. Creators who can deliver tight, engaging storytelling in under 15 seconds will stay in demand.
**Why it matters:** Campaigns need snackable assets, not long scripts.
### **3. More Creators Are Becoming Small Businesses**
Influencers aren’t just creating content. They’re launching podcasts, newsletters, digital products, communities, and subscription hubs. Audiences want “more” from the creators they trust.
**Brand takeaway:** Partner with creators who have multi-channel influence, not just a single social profile.
### **4. Authenticity Is the New Algorithm**
Highly polished content is losing ground to real, personal, and unfiltered moments. Creators who share lived experiences, behind-the-scenes struggles, or values-based storytelling see sharper engagement.
**For brands:** Campaign briefs are trending looser — influencers need room to be themselves.
### **5. AI Becomes a Normal Part of Creator Workflows**
Creators now use AI for script ideas, editing support, content planning, and even color grading. The smart ones use tools that save time so they can spend more time engaging their audience.
**Brand takeaway:** Expect faster turnaround times and more sophisticated content from solo creators.
### **6. Affiliate + Commerce Collabs Grow Fast**
Platforms are doubling down on built-in commerce tools. TikTok Shop and Instagram’s affiliate features are pushing creators toward revenue-sharing deals.
**Why it matters:** Creators want partnerships that pay them for real outcomes, not just a flat fee.
### **7. Audience Trust Is Becoming Non-Negotiable**
Followers are paying closer attention to paid disclosure, repeated sponsorships, and creator values. Audiences reward influencers who choose collaborations selectively.
**Brand takeaway:** Choose creators who already talk about your niche naturally — it makes the partnership feel organic.
### **8. Travel, Wellness, and Everyday Expertise Lead the Way**
Wellness creators sharing realistic routines. Travel creators focused on underrated destinations. Everyday experts sharing hobbies, hacks, and skills. These categories continue to surge because they feel useful, not just aspirational.
**For campaigns:** Educational content + personality is the winning formula.
### **9. “Quiet Creators” Are Trending Up**
Not every creator is high-energy or entertainment-driven. Calm, slow-living, cozy, informational, and soft-spoken creators are rising fast — especially with overwhelmed audiences wanting content that feels peaceful.
**Brand takeaway:** Match your brand tone to the creator’s storytelling style.
### **10. Real-Time Content Beats Scheduled Content**
Creators are posting more live reactions, day-in-the-life updates, and quick takes. Audiences want immediacy and relatability.
**For brands:** Expect creators to pitch more “real-time” campaign executions (live unboxings, live try-ons, quick reaction Reels, etc.)

The Shopify App Store has 8,000+ apps, and each category has multiple ones offering some form of AI. It’s incredibly easy to download various apps and lose sight of what’s important, so here’s how to pick the best apps:
  * **Approach the Shopify App Store with goals:** Find your current bottlenecks, such as growing your email list, increasing average order values, or winning more subscription customers. Knowing what you need an AI app to do will help you create a shortlist fast.
  * **Prioritize tools with tight Shopify integration:** The bare minimum is that your apps should say “works with the latest themes” on their app pages. Ideally, they should have one-click integration. It doesn’t matter if they provide a third-party dashboard, since these typically have layouts better-suited to their tasks anyway.
  * **Compare each app’s pricing:** Consider what you need now and what you’ll need in three, six, and 12 months. Will a free plan grow with you? All app pages on the Shopify App Store have a Pricing section, and some apps also have their own websites for validation.
  * **Consider your current tech stack:** You probably already have Shopify apps, in which case, any new AI apps shouldn’t overlap or conflict with them. For instance, Omnisend is a high-quality, complete [replacement for Shopify Email](https://www.omnisend.com/blog/omnisend-vs-shopify-email/).
