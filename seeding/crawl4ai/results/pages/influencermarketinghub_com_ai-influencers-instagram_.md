## **How Are AI Influencers Created?**
AI influencers, also known as digital or virtual influencers, are computer-generated characters. Such AI-generated personas are a result of computer-generated imagery (CGI) combined with AI. Most of these virtual characters have come about through the work of two leading AI-generating tools, Midjourney and Stable Diffusion.
These tools usually implement text-to-image generation. This means the creator enters a text prompt, and the trained system breaks it down and generates images based on that input. These initial results are then refined according to the creator’s liking. Once a virtual persona has been generated, the creator sets up a social media account and customizes the AI character according to their target audience and preferred personality.
Due to AI influencers’ customizability, brands prefer to use them as endorsers. In fact, [around 48.7% of marketers](https://influencermarketinghub.com/ai-in-influencer-marketing/) integrate AI into their marketing efforts regularly. AI influencers’ success in the marketing realm is achieved through effective engagement and personalized content. Creators tailor these AI characters’ behaviors and characteristics to match a brand's voice and target market.
## **Why Are AI Influencers Gaining Popularity?**
Aside from the fact that they’re not human, AI influencers do almost everything social media influencers do. They share posts, upload photos, and post their life updates. Interestingly, in the online world, they live like we all do, posting images of them getting their hair and nails done, taking pictures with celebrities, and attending concerts.
Now, you might be wondering: if they’re almost similar to existing influencers, why are they becoming so popular these days? What’s their edge compared to human influencers in terms of marketing? Below are five benefits of using digital influencers for marketing:
### **1. They’re highly customizable**
The main advantage of AI personas is that they can be generated and tweaked however a brand pleases. This is because they’re designed to target specific demographics and marketing trends. Let’s say a particular company’s target audience is teenagers. With this information sourced via [AI-driven tools](https://influencermarketinghub.com/ai-optimization-tools/), the creator will train an AI model to act and behave in a way that’s suitable and interesting for teenagers.
### **2. They ensure consistent branding**
In addition to the previous point, digital influencers can be more consistent in aligning their online presence with their partnered brand. One significant difference between these AI stars and human influencers is the consistency of their beliefs, opinions, and advocacies. While humans tend to make mistakes and do things that contradict the brand they support, AI influencers are programmed to consistently stick with a brand’s narrative and guidelines.
### **3. They are limitless**
Because AI influencers are entirely created by computers, they do not have restrictions, such as schedule conflicts, physical limitations like fatigue or illness, language issues, or geographical barriers. For example, some [influencers on Instagram](https://influencermarketinghub.com/top-instagram-influencers/) may decline brand collaborations because of lack of time or because they are from different countries. Meanwhile, AI influencers are always available and thus can adapt to any opportunity.
### **4. They don’t engage in controversies**
As [Instagram personalities](https://influencermarketinghub.com/fast-rising-instagram-creators/) gain fame, they also attract criticism. And most of the time, this leads to them being tangled with online controversies. Meanwhile, unlike humans, who tend to be emotionally driven, virtual influencers are a better option for brands who aim to avoid negative attention as much as possible.
### **5. They are less expensive than human influencers**
Finally, although it may involve huge money to build an AI model, it may prove to be cost-effective over time. Traditional social media influencers usually require regular compensation for every promotional content or appearance throughout the length of their contract. An AI won’t require additional expenses—like travel and logistics—for future promotional activities.

## **The Impact of AI Influencers on Marketing**
According to IMH’s AI Influencer Marketing Benchmark Report, nearly half of the respondents who collaborated with AI influencers for their brand campaign reported a “very positive” experience. In addition, 52.8% of respondents believe that the versatility of AI influencers will have a major effect on the future of marketing and entertainment.
### **Brand Collaborations and Engagement**
With AI influencer marketing's versatility, it’s not surprising that brands use it to reach a wider audience and increase sales. Digital influencers offer advantages, such as ease of control, consistent branding, and better availability and flexibility than conventional online influencers, making them a better option for promotional campaigns. So, to back this up, let’s take a look at some success stories of brands collaborating with AI personas.
  * #### **Lil Miquela for Samsung Galaxy**


[Samsung Galaxy’s 2019 global campaign with We Are Social](https://www.instagram.com/p/Bz_0ntPnsyt/) put together Millie Bobby Brown, Steve Aoki, and gamer Ninja with AI influencer Lil Miquela. Since the brand’s campaign highlights the philosophy of Do What You Can’t through technology, using a computer-generated avatar was a good move. This campaign generated 126M organic views and 24M engagements, leading to a 12% increase in Instagram mentions for Samsung.
  * #### **Imma for IKEA**


[Imma Gram’s partnership with the retail giant IKEA](https://www.instagram.com/p/CESbxoVjFj7/) in 2020 is another example of a successful campaign with an AI influencer. IKEA Japan gave the virtual model a home featuring a showroom apartment, which people could enjoy for three days. Around 110,000 daily commuters at the Harajuku Station could enjoy watching this ad from outside. Plus, people could watch Imma’s appearance on IKEA online during the 72-hour campaign period. This blend of reality and digital landscape received positive feedback from the audience.
### **Audience Perception and Interaction**
Public opinion on AI influencers is varied. Some are amazed by the idea of how realistic these CGI models seem online, while [others are still skeptical](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3387/13/8/178) about their credibility and find them less reliable. Our AI Marketing Benchmark Report reveals that 35.6% are worried about AI threatening their job roles. Interestingly, marketers believe that, despite AI handling many routine tasks, humans will continue to lead in areas like high-level strategy and decision-making (42.2%) and in tasks requiring creativity and emotional engagement (22.6%).
Moreover, to address people’s skepticism, brands are advised to prioritize transparency for AI models’ audiences. Based on [Statista September 2023 data](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1455150/disclosure-ai-ml-influencer-marketing/), only 67.1% of influencers worldwide indicated that they disclosed using artificial intelligence in their content.

### **Challenges and Opportunities**
Just as human influencers face struggles and backlash online, AI influencers are no exception. Public skepticism is the main challenge that AI creators have to overcome. No matter how realistic the CGI models seem to be, netizens still show distrust toward their authenticity as influencers. In connection to this, one opportunity they could leverage from this issue is to provide authentic and relatable content for their audience through better audience analysis.
### **Ethical Considerations**
A lot of online users are quite puzzled whether some online influencers are digitally created or actual people. In a study, [participants found it challenging to distinguish AI influencers](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0160791X24002537) from human ones and even found digital avatars’ accounts to be more appealing. That’s why it’s a must for companies behind AI influencers to clearly disclose this information. For example, a simple note on the influencer’s Instagram bio will clearly help people identify whether they’re CGI personas or not. 
Mentioning simple terms like “robot” or “digital”, as shown in Miquela’s and Shudu’s Instagram profiles would suffice. This kind of transparency not only builds trust but also prevents potential backlash, creating a more authentic and open digital space.
As AI technology continues to see advancements, there’s also the increasing possibility of AI influencers being used for manipulation and deception. They can be programmed to spread misinformation or biased views. They can also be used to collect vast amounts of data from followers, which can then be used for hyper-targeted ads and manipulating consumers’ purchasing decisions. 
**To mitigate these concerns:**
  * Transparency is key. AI influencers must be disclosed as such, so their followers are aware they’re interacting with AI.
  * Privacy protections and regulations around AI should be strengthened. 


## Frequently Asked Questions
### What are AI influencers?
AI influencers, also called digital or virtual influencers, are characters made by computers using CGI and AI. These virtual personalities are designed to act as humanly as possible and are used by brands for promotions.
### How many AI influencers are there?
On Instagram, there are around 150 AI-generated influencers. However, the total count of AI influencers across all platforms is yet to be identified.
### What benefits do AI influencers offer to brands?
AI influencers provide brands with consistent messaging, control over their appearance and behavior, and the ability to create unique and engaging content without the unpredictability of human influencers.
### Are AI influencers allowed on Instagram?
Digital influencers are allowed on Instagram, but not without requirements. For instance, while they’re free to create content based on their persona, they still must follow copyright laws. Plus, since they also engage with real audiences by collecting their data, they must follow data privacy laws.
### What are the future implications of AI influencers in digital marketing?
It’s expected that more and more businesses will use AI digital influencers for their digital marketing campaigns, especially as AI tech advances. Brands will leverage these virtual influencers for scaling content creation and facilitating 24/7 personalized engagement with audiences. 
### Are there any legal regulations regarding AI influencers?
While regulations can differ by location, generally, the creators behind CGI models are legally responsible if laws and guidelines, such as those set by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), are not followed.
