##  **How much do** UK influencers **make**?
The amount Instagram influencers earn varies significantly depending on factors such as their follower count, engagement rate, and content type. Many earn only small sums by posting to small audiences. While others who have large followings and high engagement rates can earn millions per year. 
However, compensation mainly comes from brand sponsorships, collaborations, and affiliate marketing. 
Micro-influencers (10,000 to 100,000 followers) might earn between £100 and £500 per post, while macro-influencers (100,000 to 1 million followers) can earn between £1,000 and £10,000 per post. 
## How do Instagram influencers make money?
Influencers typically earn money through the platform and brand collaborations. The most common methods of payment are:
### Affiliate marketing
Affiliate marketing involves influencers promoting an [ecommerce brand's](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/influencer-marketing-for-e-commerce) products through unique tracking links. Influencers earn a commission for every sale generated through their links.
This model can lead to [great influencer marketing campaigns](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/real-and-creative-examples-of-influencer-marketing-campaigns) because it benefits both parties. Brands only pay for actual sales, and influencers can earn substantial income by driving traffic and conversions.
[Learn more about affiliate influencer marketing here](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/affiliate-marketing-meets-influence-why-brands-must-develop-a-holistic-influence-strategy).
### Long-term partnerships or brand ambassadorships
Long-term partnerships or[ brand ambassadorships](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/three-successful-campaigns-with-brand-ambassadors) involve a more extended commitment between the influencer and the brand. These arrangements often come with higher compensation packages that can include a mix of flat fees and performance-based payments. Such collaborations typically require the influencer to create consistent content over a specified period, maintaining brand alignment and continuous audience engagement.
### One-off collaborations
One-off collaborations are single campaigns or posts where an influencer promotes a brand's product or service. These are typically straightforward agreements involving a one-time payment for a specific piece of content. While these can be less lucrative than long-term partnerships, they allow influencers to work with a variety of brands and maintain diverse content.
