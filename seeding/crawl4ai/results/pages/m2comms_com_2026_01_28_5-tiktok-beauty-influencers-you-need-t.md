Filipino beauty content creators have turned TikTok into more than just an entertainment app. They’ve made it a place where Filipinas discover new products, learn techniques suited for their skin tones, and make actual purchasing decisions. A well-placed product recommendation from a trusted creator can sell out items in hours, and brands have taken notice.
Filipino beauty consumers are getting smarter. They’re tired of pretty faces just showing off products. They want creators who understand their morena skin tones, their humid climate struggles, and their tight budgets. They want content that speaks to their realities, not just recycled Western beauty standards with a Filipino accent.
Here are five TikTok beauty influencers who’ll be all over your feed throughout 2026.

Spend any time on Filipino beauty TikTok and you’ll encounter [“The Lierge Effect”](https://metro.style/metro-style/beauty/2025/8/26/how-to-cop-the-lierge-effect-lierge-perey-tiktok-beauty-1525), that signature combination of dramatic eye makeup artistry paired with perfectly blown-out hair that’s become synonymous with Lierge Perey’s name. Her signature blowout has [become a BeautyTok blueprint](https://mega-asia.com/beauty/beauty-confessions/lierge-perey-hair/), with tutorials breaking down everything from the right barrel size to the “cool shot” technique for that iconic volume. Perey has also secured brand partnerships, including [Vice Cosmetics’ Perfect Kizz Velvet Collection](https://www.tiktok.com/@pereylierge/video/7532722619548830997?lang=en), and becoming the face of [Colourette’s Face Base](https://www.facebook.com/ColouretteBeIT/posts/heres-why-lierge-perey-loves-the-all-bases-covered-routine-her-face-base-line-up/1142674227893827/) line.

Paula Pelaez, better known as “Chikana,” has [8.8 million TikTok followers](https://www.tiktok.com/@paupelaez_?lang=en) and counting. Lenie Aycardo has a solid track record for creating viral beauty moments. Her content mixes cultural celebration with beauty innovation in ways that connect with Filipino audiences.
Beyond participating in trends, Aycardo creates them. Her take on the Asoka Makeup Challenge became a phenomenon in 2024, with [her first video garnering 94 million views and her second version reaching 135 million views](https://www.pep.ph/lifestyle/beauty/180437/celebrities-content-creators-asoka-makeup-trend-a6894-20240501). The challenge involved transforming into elaborate Indian bridal makeup looks while grooving to the song “San Sanana” from the Bollywood film “Asoka.” She revealed spending around [₱100,000 on production](https://interaksyon.philstar.com/videos/2024/05/09/275265/tiktok-creator-lenie-aycardo-doubles-down-on-piliin-mo-ang-pilipinas-trend/) for the challenge.
She also created the “[Piliin Mo Ang Pilipinas](https://interaksyon.philstar.com/videos/2024/05/09/275265/tiktok-creator-lenie-aycardo-doubles-down-on-piliin-mo-ang-pilipinas-trend/)” trend, tapping into national pride through beauty content and generating mass participation.

## **FAQ: TikTok Beauty Influencers in the Philippines 2026**
### **Why are engagement rates more important than follower counts?**
Engagement rates show how actively audiences interact with content through likes, comments, saves, and shares. A creator with a smaller but highly engaged following can generate more meaningful interaction than one with millions of passive followers.
### **What makes Filipino beauty TikTok different from Western beauty content?**
Filipino beauty creators address specific concerns like humidity-proof makeup, products suited for morena skin tones, and budget-conscious recommendations that reflect local economic realities.
### **How do these influencers make money beyond sponsored posts?**
Beauty creators can diversify through TikTok Shop commissions, brand ambassador roles, product line development, and exclusive content.
### **Why are “honest reviews” becoming more popular?**
Filipino consumers increasingly research products before purchasing, especially in the beauty category. Creators who give straightforward assessments of both affordable and premium products build credibility that actually influences what people buy.
### **How can I find more Filipino beauty creators to follow?**
Start by exploring TikTok’s Filipino beauty hashtags and checking which creators appear consistently. Pay attention to the comments sections of creators you already follow, as audiences often recommend similar accounts.
