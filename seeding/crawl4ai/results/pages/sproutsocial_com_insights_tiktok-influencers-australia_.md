## How to find the right Australian TikTok influencers for your brand
Choosing the right TikTok influencers can make or break your campaign. Here are key factors to consider:
### 1. Audience alignment
Ensure the influencer’s followers match your target market in age, location, interests and values. With [70% of Australians aged 16-64 using social media daily](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-trends-australia/), understanding your audience’s social media habits and preferences is crucial for effective targeting.
### 2. Engagement over follower count
High follower numbers don’t always mean high engagement. Look for influencers whose content gets real interaction—likes, comments and shares. These show an active and interested audience. Meaningful engagement is becoming more critical than sheer reach. 58% of [Australian marketers use engagement rates as the top metric](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/influencer-marketing-stats-australia/) to measure influencer success, highlighting why engagement matters more than follower count.
### 3. Content style and authenticity
Review their videos to ensure their tone and style fit your brand personality. Authentic creators who connect with their audience drive better trust and campaign results. Users crave genuine, relatable content over polished ads.
### 4. Previous brand collaborations
Check their past sponsored posts to see how they handle partnerships. Transparent, well-executed collaborations usually indicate a professional influencer.
### 5. Brand safety and reputation
Assess any past controversies or content that might clash with your brand values. Protecting your brand’s reputation is critical. [Sprout Social’s Influencer Marketing tool](https://sproutsocial.com/influencer-marketing/) allows users to easily check and compare influencer brand safety scores.
### 6. Platform and campaign fit
Consider if TikTok’s short-form video format suits your campaign goals and whether the influencer regularly produces content that performs well on the platform. Short-form video continues to grow rapidly, with TikTok and Instagram Reels leading engagement trends in Australia.

## TikTok influencers in Australia FAQs
How do Australian influencers disclose paid content? 
In Australia, transparency is key when it comes to influencer marketing. The [Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC)](https://www.accc.gov.au/media-release/scrutiny-of-influencers-and-businesses-for-misleading-advertising-and-online-reviews-continues) requires influencers to disclose any paid partnerships or sponsored content. This means creators must use clear labels like #ad, #sponsored or #paidpartnership in their posts. The goal is to keep audiences informed and maintain trust—something Aussie audiences value highly. Partnering with influencers who follow these guidelines will protect your reputation and ensure compliance with advertising laws.
Can micro-influencers generate strong engagement in Australia? 
Absolutely. While mega-influencers grab headlines with huge follower counts, micro-influencers can deliver better engagement and authenticity within niche communities. [Micro-influencers](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/microinfluencer-marketing/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) (10K to 100K followers) can boast higher engagement rates than their larger counterparts. They account for 91% of the influencer market. In Australia, many brands successfully leverage macro- and micro-influencers to drive meaningful conversations and conversions on TikTok.
