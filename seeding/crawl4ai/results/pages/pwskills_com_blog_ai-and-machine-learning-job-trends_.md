## **What is AI and Machine Learning?**
**[Artificial intelligence](https://pwskills.com/blog/artificial-intelligence/) **refers to algorithms or computer systems that can imitate human intelligence and replicate cognitive functions, such as problem-solving. In simple terms, artificial intelligence is a human-like ability to think and understand. 
This implies that specific systems and algorithms can learn or understand beyond what they have been clearly told as long as they are provided with data and instructions. Well-known examples of this technology include recommendation engines like Spotify and virtual assistants like Apple’s Siri.
**Machine learning** is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables a computer system to make predictions and decisions by extracting information from structured and semi-structured data. It utilizes data to build models that can perform specific tasks, like predicting sales. Everyday instances of machine learning can be seen in image recognition, Google translation, and the auto-friend tagging suggestions on Facebook.
### **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**
NLP is an AI technology that simplifies language-based processes by automatically interpreting and manipulating human language using algorithms. Businesses across various sectors leverage NLP applications for tasks such as language translation, text extraction, and sentiment analysis. 
Examples include using NLP in customer engagement through chatbots in banking and finance, clinical documentation and speech recognition in healthcare, and automation and quality control in manufacturing.
### **Computer Vision**
Computer vision enables computer systems to derive insights from visual data and images, similar to how the human eye perceives information. Industries such as transportation (for pedestrian detection and self-driving cars), healthcare (for cancer detection and radiology), and retail (for inventory management and self-checkout) are actively utilizing computer vision technologies to enhance their operations.
### **Edge Computing**
Edge computing involves processing data at or near its source, reducing latency and bandwidth requirements. It enables real-time processing and data analysis locally without transmitting it to a centralized location. 
Industries like manufacturing (for data analysis and asset monitoring) and remote workspaces (to increase efficiency and bandwidth) are adopting edge computing to improve operational efficiency.
### **Deep Learning**
, a machine learning subset, uses artificial neural networks with multiple processing layers to extract accurate features from complex data. It has applications in autonomous driving (for self-driving vehicle models), e-commerce (for personalised recommendations), and entertainment (for content analysis and user preferences).
### **Explainable AI**
Explainable AI focuses on providing reasoning behind the outputs generated by machine learning algorithms. It aims to make AI models transparent, trustworthy, and interpretable for users. In sectors such as healthcare (for explaining diagnoses and treatment plans), autonomous vehicles (for explaining decisions and predictions), and HR (for job application status), explainable AI enhance transparency, fairness, and accountability.
