## **1. HiFiMAN HE1000 WiFi & Arya WiFi**
HiFiMAN HE1000 WiFi and Arya WiFi (From: HiFiMan)
HiFiMAN took two of its most respected planar magnetic headphones and made them wireless, without the usual audio quality trade-off.
The key here is [**Wi-Fi**](https://www.headphonesty.com/2024/06/wifi-headphones-still-dream/). Most wireless headphones use Bluetooth, which [compresses audio in ways that matter to critical listeners](https://www.headphonesty.com/2025/01/wireless-headphones-never-sound-better-wired/). These two skip that entirely, sending full-resolution lossless audio, including high-res PCM and native DSD, over a direct Wi-Fi connection.
Each model has a custom R2R DAC and amplifier built into the earcups, so the headphones handle their own conversion instead of relying on your phone, 
On the show floor, the [HE1000 WiFi](https://hifiman.com/products/detail/361) stood out for soundstage and treble clarity, two areas where wireless headphones typically fall short. Meanwhile, the [Arya WiFi](https://hifiman.com/products/detail/362) drew praise for natural-sounding timbre.
**Where to buy:** Both models are listed on HiFiMAN’s [official website](https://hifiman.com/products/class/2#:~:text=Headphones-,HE1000%20WiFi,%241449,-HE1000%20UNVEILED), but are not yet shipping. Units are expected to ship in **April 2026**.
## **2. Grell OAE2**
Grell OAE2 (From: Grell Audio)
The standout trick with the **OAE2** is how they place the soundstage in front of you instead of inside your head.
Axel Grell, who led headphone development [at Sennheiser](https://www.reddit.com/r/headphones/comments/mdq1zo/axel_grell_legendary_former_sennheiser_chief/) for decades, built the OAE2 around a technique called [**Front-sided Sound Field Modulation**](https://stereoguide.com/guides/news/grell-oae2-presented/) (FSFM).
Rather than firing directly into the ear canal, the drivers interact with the **outer ear first**. That creates spatial cues that more closely resemble listening to speakers in a room. The result is imaging that feels **stable and forward-facing** , not centered between the ears.
Attendees at CanJam confirmed the effect is real. Voices stayed locked in place without sounding artificially wide, and the overall presentation leaned neutral, with fast, controlled bass rather than boosted low end.
At **$599** , they were one of the most interesting designs of the weekend.
**Where to buy:** The OAE2 launches internationally on March 31, 2026 via [Grell Audio’s official website](https://en.grellaudio.com/) and selected specialist retailers. 
## **3. ZMF Tessidera**
ZMF Tessidera (From: ZMF)
The **Tessidera** drew one of the longest lines at CanJam NYC 2026, with early listeners consistently describing them as the most resolving headphones ZMF have made.
A big part of that comes from **ZMF Acoustic Matrix (ZAM)** , a new internal acoustic structure debuting in the Tessidera.
ZMF developed this system after experimenting with different shapes, densities, and 3D-printed materials inside the headphone. Each variation changed how sound moved and decayed within the enclosure.
The final structure is designed to produce more **natural decay** and a soundstage that behaves more like a **real acoustic space**.
That’s why the overall tuning leans **vocal-forward and intimate** , placing midrange presence ahead of air and sparkle. And compared with the [Caldera](https://www.headphonesty.com/2023/05/review-zmf-caldera/), the presentation feels more centered on voices and instruments rather than analytical detail.
**Where to buy:** To be released on April 3, 2026, at $2,499.99 retail. An introductory launch price of $2,199.99 has been confirmed by ZMF.
## **4. Audeze CRBN2**
Audeze CRBN2 (From: Audeze)
Electrostats are prized for transparency, speed, and detail, but they have rarely delivered the weight and physical presence listeners expect in the low end. But the **CRBN2** are going straight for that.
Audeze’s [**SLAM technology**](https://www.audeze.com/blogs/technology-and-innovation/introducing-slam-breakthrough-technology-for-more-lifelike-bass-and-detail?srsltid=AfmBOor2vfs2JNAeSBwSTBlIAJhBStuTvldUZZVEBk7VDo4ne-FxV1B4) addresses this directly by adding a passive acoustic structure designed to increase output in the **10-50 Hz range** compared with the original CRBN. The carbon nanotube diaphragm remains unchanged, with conductive elements embedded inside the membrane rather than coated onto the surface.
At CanJam NYC, listeners reported real sub-bass weight alongside the speed and openness electrostats are known for. Just as important, the added low-end presence did not seem to come at the expense of texture or detail.
Their price puts them firmly in summit-fi territory, but they also sounded like a serious attempt to solve a long-standing category problem, so they’re worth a try.
**Where to buy:** Available at Audeze’s [official website](https://www.audeze.com/products/crbn2?srsltid=AfmBOorbB_gXfao_rCP0vmJzy0ycuJDO0jIb11bom6KLMOTcBme2kHRi). They require a dedicated [**580VDC Pro Bias electrostatic amplifier**](https://staxaudio.com/driver/stax-srm-d10), which adds significant cost. 
## **5. Audio-Technica ATH-ADX7000**
Audio-Technica ATH-ADX7000 (From: Audio-Technica)
The [**ATH-ADX7000**](https://www.audio-technica.com/en-us/ath-adx7000) had been building momentum since their late-2025 launch, but CanJam NYC 2026 was many listeners’ first real chance to hear them. The response suggested the hype was justified.
Audio-Technica built them around **HXDT driver technology** , which is a precision manufacturing process that tightly aligns the 58mm diaphragm, baffle plate, magnet, and voice coil.
This is less a story about exotic driver materials than about mechanical precision. Audio-Technica’s argument is that a dynamic driver built to extremely tight tolerances can compete with planar and electrostatic flagships on technical performance.
Floor impressions were positive but split.
Listeners praised the bass dynamics as unusually strong for an open-back flagship. On the other hand, treble response drew more mixed reactions, with some finding it slightly sharp. And, their **490-ohm impedance** also made amplification a major factor.
**Where to buy:** Available now at Audio-Technica’s [official website](https://www.audio-technica.com/en-us/ath-adx7000) and authorized retailers.
## **6. Meze Strada**
Meze Strada (From: Meze Audio)
At **$799** , the [**Strada**](https://mezeaudio.com/products/strada?srsltid=AfmBOorjr4wxS-KKYBA-laKkAZMuIA4QANAoovLF5mem1M30lolK6Y8W) were not expected to become a major talking point at a show full of far more expensive gear. They did anyway.
Listeners familiar with [Meze’s Empyrean](https://mezeaudio.com/products/empyrean2?srsltid=AfmBOoq8so4ZxI93vyc3qXYHeFXahKfFkYgG_wI_byIq2fE-hbmd2nSe) and [Elite](https://mezeaudio.com/products/elite?srsltid=AfmBOop5CVR9ZE1scRxsXq4HehPiS_woKdfvMS3NueKXX7WLHfAqAA6C) came away surprised by how composed and natural they sounded. The tuning was described as clear and slightly warm, with enough body to stay engaging without turning soft or overly relaxed.
But what stood out most was how little they sounded like a typical closed-back. Instead of feeling boxed in, they presented music with an openness that gave them an advantage in quick show-floor comparisons.
That mattered because crowded audio shows are unforgiving to closed-backs. If a headphone feels congested or overly sealed-off, people notice fast.
The Strada avoided that problem cleanly and ended up feeling like one of the weekend’s strongest value plays.
**Where to buy:** Available at Meze Audio’s [official website](https://mezeaudio.com/products/strada?srsltid=AfmBOopeALe9c9x0pQSOiA8BT6ExVdObYUahlleyNGPp1ARgew64nfwz).
## **7. MA Audio INFINITUM**
MA Audio INFINITUM (From: Head-Fi)
The **INFINITUM** made one of the strongest technical impressions at CanJam NYC 2026, not because of the brand’s profile, but because of what MA Audio put inside them.
First off, they are built around **a pure beryllium diaphragm in a planar magnetic driver**.
Beryllium is already prized in top-tier dynamic drivers for its stiffness-to-weight ratio and fast transient response. Applying it to a planar magnetic design is a much harder problem, since the diaphragm has to move uniformly across a larger surface.
That is what made the INFINITUM such a curiosity on the show floor.
Early reactions were strong. At least one experienced attendee reportedly said they were still thinking about them the day after the show, which is notable for a headphone from a brand many listeners had not encountered before.
Whether the beryllium diaphragm proves itself beyond first impressions remains to be seen, but as a debut statement, they were difficult to ignore.
**Where to buy:** Not available as of this writing. Release is expected around **April 2026** at approximately **$6,000** , based on informal communications at the show. No official product page exists yet. 
## **8. ThieAudio Cypher**
ThieAudio Cypher (From: Linsoul)
ThieAudio built its reputation largely on in-ear monitors, so the **Cypher** drew immediate attention as a higher-profile move deeper into over-ear headphones.
They use a **50mm dynamic driver** supported by a **20-core N45 magnetic array** inside CNC-milled aluminum earcups. Despite the premium materials, they weigh just **405 grams** and are priced aggressively at **$399** , with early Kickstarter units starting at **$329**.
The booth stayed busy throughout the weekend, and early listeners described the sound as far exceeding expectations for the price. That mattered because the Cypher were entering a crowded part of the market where strong first impressions matter more than brand familiarity.
**Where to buy:** Currently available through ThieAudio’s [Kickstarter campaign](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/linsoul/thieaudio-cypher-flagship-dynamic-driver-headphones?ref=3u5h3g), with units expected to ship in **April 2026**. A product page is also live at [Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/thieaudio-cypher?srsltid=AfmBOopRtBRauWBlJvtsgqLKgZKUwgvd4kuNOArElqnakNnr8FpJSVI6), though the Cypher have not yet shipped to buyers. 
## **9. FiiO EH13**
FiiO EH13 (From: FiiO)
The **EH13** only cost **$50** , which made them one of the easiest headphones at CanJam NYC to underestimate.
Yet people were caught people off guard at how much FiiO packed in at that price:
  * **Full parametric EQ** through the FiiO Control app
  * **LDAC** hi-res Bluetooth

  * Up to **75 hours of battery life**.


More important, the feature set did not feel like filler. The EH13 came across as a genuinely usable entry-level wireless option with real room for adjustment, rather than a budget model padded out with spec-sheet distractions.
They were never going to challenge a flagship on raw performance, though. What they did do was make a strong case that entry-level buyers no longer have to settle for stripped-down features or limited control over tuning.
**Where to buy:** Available now through FiiO’s [official AliExpress store](https://www.aliexpress.com/item/1005010803943199.html?gatewayAdapt=kor2glo) and [authorized resellers](https://www.voptronics.com/fiio-eh13.html). 
## 10. Meze Audio ASTRU
Meze Audio ASTRU (From: Meze Audio)
The **ASTRU** are Meze’s answer to a market that keeps piling on more drivers: use one, and make it exceptional. Where competitors stack balanced armatures, electrostatics, and hybrid configurations, the ASTRU rely on a **single 10mm dynamic driver** with no crossover.
The diaphragm dome uses more than 80 ultra-thin layers of gold applied during a 48-hour vacuum sputtering process, bonded to titanium, and mounted on a PEEK base. The goal is fast transients, extended treble, and the natural warmth dynamic drivers are known for.
Plus, the housing is CNC-machined from a single block of titanium, with each matched pair reportedly requiring up to seven days of machining and finishing. This construction is meant to minimize internal resonance while maintaining tight acoustic control around the driver.
At a show where many IEM designs focused on ever-larger driver counts, the ASTRU stood out by emphasizing **materials engineering and acoustic tuning** instead.
**Where to buy:** Available starting March 20, 2026, through Meze Audio’s [official website](https://mezeaudio.com) and selected retailers worldwide.
