## What are the benefits for brands that use AI models?
Firstly, brands find AI models useful as they are able to significantly reduce their expenses, ensure the consistent delivery of their brand messages, and generate an infinite number of content variations.
Thus, provide marketing departments with the opportunity to supervise every single detail, whether it's the outfit or the location, without the need for arranging conventional photo shoots.
The market around virtual influencers on a broad scale is already quite large.
[According to one industry source](https://www.territory-influence.com/virtual-influencers-and-their-social-media-appeal-to-brands-in-the-meteverse/), the virtual influencer market was valued at approximately $4.6 billion, with a strong upward trend anticipated until 2025.
**Key benefits for brands include:**
  * **Cost-effectiveness.** The use of AI models eliminates the need for traveling, studio rentals, and other recurring production costs associated with each photoshoot. After the initial creation, the model can be incorporated into various campaigns with a relatively small additional cost.
  * **Consistent messaging.** The use of machine learning makes sure that images, captions, and stories are always compliant with brand guidelines. The character does not share personal opinions that are off-brand or unapproved content.
  * **High content volume.** The staff can come up with numerous sets of images for different seasons, locations, and product lines without having to reschedule the talents or account for weather conditions.
  * **Global reach.** AI models have the ability to communicate in different languages and can even be adjusted to the local style while still being one central brand asset.
  * **Creative experimentation.** Companies may want to consider this option if they would like to test a futuristic or fantasy theme for their visuals, which would be hard or costly to make with human models.


These benefits attract not only large corporations but also smaller brands that seek influencer-style content while maintaining better control over their assets and schedules.
## What are the downsides for brands that use AI models?
Artificial intelligence models carry risk factors related to authenticity, ethics, regulation, and technical reliability.
Brands, therefore, should be very cautious in handling these risks to avoid negative reactions, court cases, or loss of trust from their audience.
**Key downsides for brands include:**
  * **Lack of authenticity and believability.** Some people may view AI models as being less authentic than human influencers.
  * **Technical issues.** Errors, strange or missing body parts, and other inconsistent details can erode the trustworthiness of the brand. Suddenly changing the style of AI may also disorient the followers.
  * **Unclear regulation.** There are ongoing developments regarding the rules on AI disclosure, deepfakes, and advertising transparency. Complying with the new standards may become the responsibility of brands in different regions.
  * **Ethical concerns.** AI models can reinforce unrealistic beauty standards, replace human models in certain roles, or misappropriate identities and cultures.
  * **Risk of reputation.** A poorly targeted ad campaign can trigger a strong backlash, particularly when AI is used as a substitute for genuine diversity or representation.


Real-life situations are, in fact, examples of this struggle already.
For example, [Levi's received backlash](https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/fashion/levis-ai-models-diversity-backlash-b2310280.html) after announcing the use of AI-generated models to increase diversity.
Critics claimed that companies should hire real people with different bodies and backgrounds instead of creating them.
[A Calvin Klein campaign](https://www.thecut.com/2019/05/bella-hadid-lil-miquela-calvin-klein-apology.html), featuring Bella Hadid and Lil Miquela, was met with criticism, and the brand had to apologize for the way it presented the kiss between a human and a virtual character.
The point these instances highlight is that AI models can be used to support a marketing plan; however, they can also spark debates about authenticity, diversity, and the future of creative work.
