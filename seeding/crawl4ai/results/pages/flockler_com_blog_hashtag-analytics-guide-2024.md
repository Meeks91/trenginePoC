## **What is hashtag analytics?**
Hashtag analytics tracks and monitors a collection of metrics that provide information about the performance of branded or other hashtags on each social media platform. Hashtag analytics is a part of social media analytics that helps understand the used hashtags' effectiveness for social media marketing.There are many permutations and combinations of hashtag usage as each social platform demands a different strategy. For example, on some platforms, 2-3 hashtags are sufficient (LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter), but on others (Instagram & TikTok), you need many more hashtags per post.
Hashtag analysis clears all the air around these possibilities, and helps you discover the following.
  * What is the potential to increase brand awareness via hashtags 
  * What type of hashtags to use to amplify product reach 
  * What hashtags to use for generating traction for UGC campaigns

### **Important hashtags to track**
Now that we’ve established the importance of hashtag analytics and how your brand hashtags can be repurposed, let's look at which ones work best. Today, hashtags are important for a brand’s discoverability and giving the right context to your content. The key is using the right hashtags that match your tone, brand voice, and messaging. But how do you go about finding a balance between what’s relevant to your brand and what works?Let’s look at 6 categories of hashtags that help with engagement.
**1. Location Hashtags** Location hashtags tag content to a specific place, such as a city, neighborhood, or landmark. These hashtags help users discover posts related to a particular area, making them ideal for businesses and influencers aiming to attract local audiences.

For instance, #GoldenGateBridge is used by tourists, locals, or businesses targeting those in the vicinity. Location hashtags are effective in enhancing the visibility of posts to users searching for content or services within a specific geographic region, thereby increasing local engagement and potentially driving foot traffic to physical locations. 
**2. Event Hashtags**
Event hashtags are used to boost content related to events like conferences, concerts, sports games, or festivals. These hashtags enable participants and attendees to connect, share experiences, and follow updates in real-time.
Events hashtags often form a community and create an active buzz for the event. For example, #Coachella2024 brings out all the relevant posts of the Coachella event. This hashtag catches feeds from celebrities, influencers, and other participants leading to strong marketing for the event.
You’ll also notice that events go over and above to boost engagement by creating social walls that relay all attendee content that uses the event’s hashtag. Take a look at the example here.
Social media walls are a credible choice for making your events more engaging. In events, you can add social media walls on multiple locations in the venue, such as – hallways, the main screen or jumbotrons, the lobby, the stage area, live streaming, etc. You can use social walls to display every single critical update, run contests, and display real-time reactions and results.
**3. Branded Hashtags** Branded hashtags are unique to a company or product and are used to promote and track brand-specific content. These hashtags are often created by brands to encourage [user-generated content](https://flockler.com/blog/what-is-ugc-meaning), brand recognition, and engagement.

Content hashtags describe the subject or theme of the post, helping users discover content based on their interests. These hashtags are specific to the content’s nature, such as #TravelPhotography, #HealthyRecipes, or #FitnessTips.
