## What is UGC on TikTok?
User-generated content (UGC) is any content—videos, images, reviews, testimonials—created by users, not brands. This is what makes TikTok different from traditional marketing. Big brands like [Pepsi](https://www.tiktok.com/@pepsiglobal), [Disney](https://www.tiktok.com/@disney?lang=en), and[ Apple](https://www.tiktok.com/@apple?lang=en) use it. Why does it work so well?
  * **Authenticity sells** – People trust other users more than brands.
  * **It's cost-effective** – UGC campaigns can go viral with little to no ad spend.
  * **Higher engagement** – TikTok's algorithm prioritizes real, relatable content.


There are**two types of TikTok UGC** :
  1. Organic UGC – Created by real customers without brand involvement (reviews, testimonials, hashtag challenges).


  1. Paid UGC – Made by UGC creators, paid to produce brand-friendly content without sounding like an ad.


### Why is UGC on TikTok Important?
TikTok rewards engagement, not ad spending, helping user-generated content (UGC) to gain visibility without a massive budget. Additionally, unlike traditional ads, UGC feels real and relatable, and it's some kind of social proof.
This trust translates into action - the hashtag [#TikTokMadeMeBuyIt](https://www.tiktok.com/tag/tiktokmademebuyit?lang=en) has 77.8 billion views!
The platform's features make it easy for users to create and share brand-related content. Tools like Duet and Stitch encourage interaction, while trends and challenges help brands reach new audiences organically. 
Therefore, even smaller brands can compete by leveraging authentic content from micro and nano creators, who often have more engaged audiences.
## A Success Story
[Hyundai](https://www.tiktok.com/@hyundai) proved that TikTok UGC isn't just for small brands—it's also a game-changer for global companies. When launching their fully electric IONIQ 6, Hyundai tapped into TikTok's creator-led content strategy to boost engagement during one of the year's biggest North American sporting events.
Instead of relying solely on traditional TV ads, Hyundai mixed TikTok-native content, creator collaborations, and premium video production to make the campaign stand out. Their strategy combined:
  * Celebrity-driven content – TV ads featuring [Kevin Bacon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevin_Bacon) and [Sosie Bacon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sosie_Bacon).
  * TikTok UGC storytelling – Partnering with TikTok creators like [@oldgays](https://www.tiktok.com/@oldgays), [@jetbentlee](https://www.tiktok.com/@jetbentlee), and [@chefreactions](https://www.tiktok.com/@chefreactions).
  * Community-based ad placement – Using [TikTok Pulse ads](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/tiktok-pulse?lang=en) to reach auto and sports fans.


The results? According to [Hyundai’s case study](https://ads.tiktok.com/business/en-US/inspiration/hyundai-1118), they achieved:
  * 242M+ impressions


  * 13% increase in ad recall
  * 3.5% lift in brand awareness
  * Thousands of TikTok users engage through Duets, Stitches, and remixes.


## 8 Ways to Get UGC on TikTok
Getting UGC on TikTok isn't about luck—it's about giving people a reason to talk about your brand. Since [Gen Z users have made purchases based on social media](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1455391/shoppers-social-media-discovery-and-purchase-worldwide/), brands should prioritize UGC and learn how to turn engagement into sales. Here's how to make that happen.
### 1. Know Your Audience
UGC isn't just about letting random users post about your brand—it's about attracting the right creators who naturally fit with your audience.
For instance, influencer reviews will work if you target beauty lovers or your audience is into fitness. In that case, think about tutorial-style videos; they'll perform better.
That's how you make smarter content decisions! So, start with:
  * _Age, gender, location, and language all play a role._
  * _What kind of videos do they watch, like, and share?_


How to do that? It's easy with TikTok's Audience Insights tool that helps brands analyze real user data from paid and organic activity. It reveals:
  * **Demographics:** Age, gender, location, and device type.
  * **Interests:** What topics do they engage with, top hashtags and ad interest categories?
  * **Engagement patterns:** How often they open the app, how much time they spend watching videos, and how frequently they interact with content.
  * **Hashtag interactions:** What trending topics does your audience follow?


### 2. Find and Reach Out to UGC Creators
Audience alignment, engagement, and authenticity matter more than raw numbers. A creator's content must feel natural for your brand while speaking directly to the people you want to reach. 
But you don't have to wait for the right creator to come to you! There are several ways to find and recruit quality UGC creators actively:
  * **Influencer Marketing Platforms** – These platforms offer tools for filtering creators based on their niche, past performance, and alignment with your brand's identity.


  * **Social Media Monitoring** – TikTok is among the best tools for finding UGC creators. Search hashtags related to your industry, check your brand mentions and look at tagged posts. If someone already talks about your product, they will likely create authentic content.


  * **Competitor Research** – See who's creating UGC for brands similar to yours. Check their tagged posts, explore collaborations, and observe follower interactions. This can give insight into which types of creators work best for your industry.


  * Brand Ambassador Programs **– Big brands like Nike, GoPro, and Gymshark use** structured ambassador programs to turn loyal customers into UGC creators. These creators produce content in exchange for perks like exclusive access, discounts, or features on brand pages.


  * **Contests and Challenges** – Instead of searching for creators, let them come to you. Launch a branded challenge or UGC campaign, encouraging users to submit their best videos for a chance to win a reward.


Before reaching out, use TikTok Audience Insights to analyze demographics, interests, and behaviors to ensure their audience matches your target market. Don't be lazy—check comments, shares, and likes to gauge real audience interaction. Always look for their content style—does it feel natural or overly polished? UGC should be relatable and unscripted.
Once you've identified potential creators, your outreach should feel personal, not transactional. Instead of sending a generic message, show that you've watched their content and appreciate their style.
