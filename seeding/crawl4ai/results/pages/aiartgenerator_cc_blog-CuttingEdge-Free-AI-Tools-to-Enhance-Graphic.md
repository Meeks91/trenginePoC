## Text-to-Image for Precise 8-Bit Graphic Assets
I bet most of you don't even know this next tool exists. Microsoft Bing has stepped into the AI game with an impressive text-to-image generator built right into their search engine. And I've found it often creates more precise imagery and assets compared to DALL-E or Midjourney.
The credits in the top right of the search bar are your free tokens that get used up with each generation. When they run out, it just means the render times will be a bit slower. But the big advantage is that, unlike DALL-E or Midjourney, you don't need to use special text commands. You simply type out a natural language description of whatever you want to generate.
### Bing Delivers Retro Gaming Graphics
Let's say I'm working on a retro gaming design project and I need some 8-bit graphic style visuals of a hut. Bing actually does a surprisingly good job here of creating something usable for my design concept. And it's generated pretty quickly too. It's a shame there's no way to tweak the aspect ratio or do much fine-tuning. But for a completely free tool, being able to get decent results simply by typing a text description is wild.
### Royalty-Free High Quality Alternatives

This saves tons of time hunting down usable images while preventing legal issues. Definitely check Immerse AI out next time you need an image but can't find a solid royalty-free option.
## Crafting Logos Based on Audience Personas
I'm going to demonstrate an unusual way of leveraging ChatGPT that graphic designers can find super useful. Let's say I'm working on a logo project for a new tech startup client. The first step is having ChatGPT generate detailed audience personas that encapsulate the target users. You'll want to include things like demographics, psychographics, goals, desires, pain points, and more in your prompt to get rich personas.
ChatGPT does a great job profiling fictional personas based on the client description. Now here's where things get really interesting...
### ChatGPT Outlines Logo Design Directions
Using the rendered audience personas, I then ask ChatGPT to list specific ways we could tailor the logo design to best resonate with those user profiles. And it delivers! The AI suggests things like using tech symbolism in the mark itself since this is a software company. Or that a modern, minimal aesthetic would appeal most to the persona 'Sarah'. It even explains the psychology behind certain shapes and colors that would make the audience feel like the brand is innovative or trustworthy. ChatGPT is essentially handing creative direction to me on a silver platter tailored to my client's target users. This helps inform smarter design choices rooted in data vs assumptions.
### Psychology of Shape and Color Choices
Of course as the designer, I don't need to do exactly as ChatGPT suggests. But having these personalized recommendations based on audience personas provides an awesome starting point or foundation for the logo concepting phase. I can take the helpful creative direction from ChatGPT and blend it with my own visual ideas and branding knowledge. This approach helps remove guesswork from the branding process. And being able to point to concrete persona data as the framework for design decisions can really impress clients and set you apart from other agencies as well. Give it a try on your next logo project for kickstarting concepting strategically!
