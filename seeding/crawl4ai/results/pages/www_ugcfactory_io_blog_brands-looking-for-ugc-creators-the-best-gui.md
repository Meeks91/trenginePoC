[User-generated content (UGC)](https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbesbusinesscouncil/2023/03/13/why-user-generated-content-is-winning/) refers to any type of content related to a brand’s products and services and not by the brand itself. UGC content includes photos, reviews, or videos created by a brand’s clients or UGC creators and shared on different platforms. UGC can also be shared through testimonials, Q&A forums, or photos of a brand’s products. 
According to [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbesbusinesscouncil/2023/03/13/why-user-generated-content-is-winning/), UGC is inexpensive, authentic, and reaches a wider audience making it attractive to brands and creators. This means UGC can boost conversions, drive sales, and turn creators into marketing machines. 
Are you a UGC creator looking for brand partnerships or a brand looking for UGC creators? This article explores the benefits of working with UGC creators and brands working with UGC creators. Let’s dive into the article. 
## **Why are Brands Looking for UGC Creators?**
UGC is a deal-changer in digital marketing given it’s all about authenticity and creativity. Brands understand that UGC is a critical component in a customer’s journey, especially the sales funnel stage. Research shows that [79% of online users](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/survey-reveals-that-ugc-can-drive-improved-trust-and-loyalty-for-ecommerce/606801/) admit that UGC impacts their purchasing decisions. Therefore, there is no doubt brands are in a tough supremacy battle in the online space. 
The reasons why brands are looking for UGC creators are outlined below:
### **1. UGC Leads to Brand Authenticity and Trustworthiness**
A recent survey by [Tagbox](https://taggbox.com/blog/how-to-find-ugc-creators/) showed that 90% of consumers consider authenticity when deciding whether to purchase from a brand. UGC creators are actual users who reflect authentic experiences, interactions, and opinions with a brand’s products. Therefore, brands looking for UGC creators create a sense of authenticity and trustworthiness. 
### **2. UGC Enhances Engagement and Relatability**
Ads created by UGC creators have [4 times higher CTR](https://www.shopify.com/blog/user-generated-content) (clickthrough rate) and are 50% cheaper compared to average ads. Consequently, brands are looking for UGC creators to create relatable and engaging content that consumers can connect with. This optimizes engagement levels and the brand’s market share. 
### **3. UGC Content is Cost-Effective**
Brands that work with UGC creators understand it’s an effective marketing strategy. Leveraging UGC is cost-effective compared to producing branded content. Therefore, UGC brands can tap into the endless stream of content generated by creators in their niche. 
### **4. UGC Provides Social Proof and Influence**
A recent study by [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2023/05/05/when-it-comes-to-building-trust-with-consumers-brands-need-to-put-customer-satisfaction-first/) showed that 92% of consumers trust recommendations from other users over branded content. This means that UGC serves as social proof that a brand’s products are good. 
### **5. UGC Impacts Purchase Decisions**
A recent survey also found that [79% of consumers](https://taggbox.com/blog/user-generated-content-facts-and-stats/)’ purchasing decisions are impacted by UGC. Consumers are more likely to trust UGC brands when purchasing. This means that UGC creators influencer consumer perceptions and their purchasing decisions. 
### **6. UGC Encourages Diversity**
UGC enables brands to reach out to prospects with everyone being invited to be an ambassador for their products or services. The UGC creators are from a broad spectrum and cut through religious, racial, sexual, and ethnic lines. 
## **Types of Brands Looking for UGC Creators**
While the idea of brands working with UGC creators is relatively new, its benefits are felt by brands in all niches. This includes e-commerce brands, fitness and wellness brands, food and drink brands, travel and hospitality brands, fashion brands, and tech brands. 
### **a). UGC Creators and Fashion Brands**
Fashion brands like Nike, Adidas, and Gymshark regularly request their clients to post themselves wearing their products on social media. This is meant to create social proof that the products are good and drive more sales and engagement. Brands working with different UGC get honest opinions of their products. 
### **b). UGC Creators and Tech Brands**
Another industry with brands looking for UGC creators is the technology sector. Firms like GoPro collaborate with shoppers to post their products like gimbals and cameras. Apple also uses UGC creators during product launches to showcase their new devices and capabilities. 
### **c). UGC Creators and Fitness and Wellness Brands**
Fitness and wellness firms are another example of brands that work with UGC creators. Brands like MyFitnessPal use UGC creators to showcase their products and services. Others like Peloton use UGC creators to tell success stories after trying the brand. Brands in the fitness space demonstrate their value by working with successful UGC creators. 
### **d). UGC Creators and E-Commerce Brands**
Firms in the e-commerce sector are other examples of brands looking for UGC creators. Brands in this sector use UGC creators for reviews or to market their products. Firms like Esty and Amazon are examples of brands that work with UGC creators.
