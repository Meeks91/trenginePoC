# 10 Top LinkedIn Learning AI Courses to Build Skills in 2025
By [Surbhi](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/users/surbhimalik)
  * Published: September 19, 2025
  * 11 min read


Whether you’re a working professional looking to advance your career or a student preparing for tomorrow’s opportunities, learning AI skills can expand your professional prospects. DigitalOcean’s [2025 Currents research report](https://doimages.nyc3.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/Currents_2025/DigitalOcean%20Currents%20February%202025.pdf) found that 38% of businesses surveyed struggle to justify AI spending because they lack in-house expertise to leverage AI technologies. This skills gap creates an opening for motivated learners to gain the AI skills that companies are struggling to find internally.
Fortunately—from [AI certifications](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-certifications) to [AI courses](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-courses)—plenty of online resources are available to build these skills. For instance, [LinkedIn Learning](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/) AI courses can help you add new skills to your portfolio and provide an understanding of the latest trends and developments in artificial intelligence and machine learning. Keep reading to discover LinkedIn Learning courses that blend AI fundamentals theory with practical experience in machine learning algorithms and data analysis.
**Key takeaways:**
  * LinkedIn Learning provides AI courses explicitly designed for busy working professionals. Most programs require minimal time commitment and no extensive technical background.
  * Course certificates come from established technology leaders, including Microsoft, KNIME, and NASBA. They add credentials to your professional profile that employers actively recognize and value.
  * Most courses can be completed in under two hours, so these programs fit easily into busy schedules. They allow evening and weekend learning without overwhelming time commitments.


##  [Pros and cons of LinkedIn learning for AI upskilling](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#pros-and-cons-of-linkedin-learning-for-ai-upskilling)
Before enrolling in a LinkedIn Learning course (or two), understanding how the platform stacks up against the numerous other [AI training resources](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-research-tools) and educational programs available online is worth understanding. Below are some pros and cons to help you decide whether LinkedIn learning is right for you:
###  [Pros of LinkedIn Learning](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#pros-of-linkedin-learning)
  * Most courses clock in under two hours, so you can realistically complete them without sacrificing your weekends or pulling all-nighters.
  * Established organizations like Microsoft, KNIME, NASBA, and Wolfram Research develop LinkedIn Learning courses. The certifications carry real weight with employers who value these credentials.
  * Your certificates automatically appear on your profile, complete with verification badges. No need to upload PDFs or explain what you learned—it’s all right there for recruiters and hiring managers on LinkedIn to see.


###  [Cons of LinkedIn Learning](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#cons-of-linkedin-learning)
  * Most LinkedIn Learning courses give you the 30,000-foot view of a topic, not a technical deep dive. If you plan to become an [AI researcher](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/ai-research-tools) or build complex models from scratch, you’ll likely need to supplement with more intensive programs.
  * AI is moving so quickly that a course created six months ago might already feel dated. New tools launch weekly, and best practices shift constantly. Treat LinkedIn Learning as ongoing education, not a one-and-done certification.
  * Unlike bootcamps or university courses, you won’t get personalized feedback or career-specific guidance. Most courses are on-demand and asynchronous, so you’re mainly on your own to figure out how to apply what you’ve learned.


##  [Top 10 LinkedIn Learning AI courses in 2025](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#top-10-linkedin-learning-ai-courses-in-2025)
These LinkedIn Learning courses span the essentials—from understanding machine learning algorithms and mastering prompt techniques to building ethical AI practices and strategic implementation:
###  [1. Artificial Intelligence Foundations: Machine Learning](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#1-artificial-intelligence-foundations-machine-learning)
“[Artificial Intelligence Foundations: Machine Learning](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/artificial-intelligence-foundations-machine-learning-22345868/introduction-to-ai-foundations-machine-learning-course?u=81506066)” is taught by [Kesha Williams](https://www.linkedin.com/in/keshaewilliams/), a tech leader with nearly three decades of experience and a Senior Director, Enterprise Architecture & Engineering at Slalom. She’s authored over 25 courses for more than one million learners worldwide. This course covers machine learning concepts through a structured approach, starting with a foundational understanding and progressing through hands-on training of custom models. Students learn about different learning algorithms for classification and regression, explore model performance evaluation, and get practical experience with real machine learning implementations.
Williams breaks down complex topics into digestible segments, making [machine learning](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/tutorials/an-introduction-to-machine-learning) accessible whether you want to understand the fundamentals or apply these techniques in your work. The course includes [exercise files on GitHub](https://github.com/LinkedInLearning/artificial-intelligence-foundations-machine-learning-3067770) that can be practiced with actual code and datasets rather than just watching videos.
**Instructor:** Kesha Williams
**Duration:** 1h 50m
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Key skills taught:** Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence
**Certificate availability:** Professional Certificate from Wolfram Research
**Who should take it:** Students, Executives, and Product Managers
###  [2. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#2-introduction-to-artificial-intelligence)
[Doug Rose](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dougrose/)’s “[Introduction to Artificial Intelligence](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/introduction-to-artificial-intelligence-2023/why-you-need-to-know-about-artificial-intelligence?u=81506066)” offers a business-focused overview perfect for non-technical professionals looking to understand AI’s role in modern organizations. With over three decades of experience transforming companies through technology, advanced degrees from Syracuse University, and eight years of teaching experience at the University of Chicago’s Graham School, Rose breaks down complex AI concepts into manageable pieces for managers, directors, and executives.
The course covers lessons like “Strong vs. weak AI,” and “Machine learning algorithms” alongside practical applications in “Robotics,” and “The Internet of Things.” Rose specifically targets people who need to make informed decisions about AI implementation rather than build systems themselves.
**Instructor:** Dough Rose
**Duration:** 1h 34m
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Key skills taught:** AI for business

##  [Looking ahead: The future of AI learning over the next five years](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#looking-ahead-the-future-of-ai-learning-over-the-next-five-years)
Learning about AI matters, but AI will also change how we learn everything. AI tutors will teach at your own speed, and intelligent assistants will help you practice skills with instant feedback. Here are a few predictions for what’s coming:
  * **AI agents as learning companions** : One of the most exciting developments will be AI-powered tutoring systems that act as personalized learning assistants. These [AI agents](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/types-of-ai-agents) will adapt to individual learning styles, provide instant feedback, answer questions in real-time, and create customized learning paths based on your career goals and existing knowledge gaps. For instance, OpenAI recently released “[study mode](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-study-mode/)” in ChatGPT.
  * **University program transformation** : We’ll see colleges and universities rapidly restructure their offerings, moving beyond computer science departments to create interdisciplinary AI programs. Expect new degrees combining AI with law, medicine, business, and liberal arts. Universities will also partner more closely with the industry to ensure graduates have immediately applicable skills; we’ve already seen Anthropic introduce [Claude for Education](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-claude-for-education), partnering with post-secondary institutions like Northeastern University, the London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE), and Champlain College.
  * **Democratized learning pathways** : AI education will become more accessible to non-technical professionals through visual programming interfaces, [no-code AI tools](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/best-ai-app-builder), and business-focused curricula. The emphasis will shift from requiring deep programming knowledge to understanding AI applications, ethics, and strategic implementation. As NVIDIA’s Jensen Huang said, “Now, all of a sudden… there’s a new programming language. This new programming language is called 'human.’”


##  [LinkedIn Learning AI Courses FAQs](https://www.digitalocean.com/resources/articles/linkedin-learning-ai-courses#linkedin-learning-ai-courses-faqs)
**What are the best LinkedIn Learning AI courses in 2025?**
The top courses include “Artificial Intelligence Foundations: Machine Learning,” “Introduction to Prompt Engineering for Generative AI,” and “Building AI Literacy.” Choose based on your role—executives should focus on strategy courses, while individual contributors benefit more from technical and prompt engineering courses.
**Are LinkedIn Learning AI certificates worth it?**
Yes, especially certificates from Microsoft, KNIME, and NASBA partners. They add credibility to your LinkedIn profile and demonstrate current AI knowledge to employers. However, they should be combined with practical applications for maximum impact.
**How long does it take to complete an AI course on LinkedIn Learning?**
Most courses range from 30 minutes to 2 hours, with the comprehensive “Building AI Literacy” taking about 8 hours. You can complete most individual courses on the weekend or spread them over a week.
**Do LinkedIn AI courses help in career advancement?**
Yes. AI skills are increasingly valuable across all industries. These courses help you speak the language of AI, understand its business applications, and position yourself as forward-thinking in your field.
**Which LinkedIn AI courses are best for beginners?**
Start with “Introduction to Artificial Intelligence” or “Introduction to Prompt Engineering for Generative AI.” Both provide solid foundations without overwhelming technical jargon. For comprehensive coverage, follow up with “Building AI Literacy.”
**Are LinkedIn Learning AI courses recognized by employers?**
Yes, particularly those offering certificates from Microsoft, KNIME, and NASBA. These appear directly on your LinkedIn profile, which most recruiters and hiring managers review during candidate evaluation.
**How does LinkedIn Learning compare to Coursera or Udemy for AI courses?**
LinkedIn Learning excels in short, business-focused courses with strong industry partnerships. [Coursera](https://www.coursera.org/) offers more academic depth, while [Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/?srsltid=AfmBOopLkBoSruDDBcXi0BBzvjQb0nU2ZjLEbMVRHB0mHz9JMFUYz8SY) provides more hands-on coding. LinkedIn is best for busy professionals who want to gain practical AI knowledge quickly.
