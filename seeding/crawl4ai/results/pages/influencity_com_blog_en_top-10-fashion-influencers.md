Fashion influencers are more than people sharing outfits online. They shape how trends emerge, how brands are perceived, and how audiences decide what feels relevant right now. [Their platforms have become spaces where inspiration](https://influencity.com/blog/en/find-the-best-social-media-platform-for-your-influencer-marketing-strategy), identity, and commerce naturally intersect.
Not all fashion influencers operate in the same way. Some build strong visual worlds around a specific aesthetic, others stand out through bold styling choices or highly wearable everyday looks. The key is understanding which type of [creator aligns with your brand](https://influencity.com/blog/en/build-a-marketing-business-plan) positioning and audience.
Below, you’ll find a curated selection of fashion influencers who are making an impact through distinct personal style and consistent visual identity, along with insight into how [brands can collaborate with them in a meaningful way](https://influencity.com/blog/en/how-influencers-build-brand-loyalty-that-lasts-from-lego-to-target).
## How Fashion Influencers Shape Trends and Impact Consumer Purchasing
Fashion influencers are no longer just style icons—they are trendsetters who actively reshape consumer behavior, shifting how audiences discover, purchase, and engage with fashion. One of the key trends they have pioneered is the move toward micro-trends, where styles and aesthetics change rapidly, often lasting just a few months or even weeks. Influencers on [platforms like TikTok](https://influencity.com/blog/en/tiktoks-product-preview) and Instagram contribute to this fast-paced environment by spotlighting new aesthetics.
Another significant shift is the rise of sustainable and conscious fashion. Influencers advocating for sustainability encourage their followers to make mindful purchasing decisions, favor second-hand shopping, or invest in quality pieces that last. This shift in consumer behavior is evident as shoppers are increasingly opting for eco-friendly alternatives or [participating in trends](https://influencity.com/blog/en/turning-data-into-substance-influencer-marketing-trends-for-2026) like the “capsule wardrobe,” which prioritizes minimalism and longevity over fast fashion. As a result, [brands collaborating](https://influencity.com/blog/en/platform-playbook-for-influencer-collabs-tiktok-instagram-youtube) with these influencers not only enhance their image but also appeal to ethically-minded consumers.
_Influencer[Annie Em](https://www.youtube.com/@hiannieem) highlights must-have pieces from her capsule wardrobe_
Additionally, influencers impact how consumers shop by [integrating social commerce](https://influencity.com/blog/en/best-tech-influencers) into their content. Through live shopping events, product recommendations, and direct links to purchases, fashion [influencers make the buying](https://influencity.com/blog/en/influencer-fees-in-the-food-industry-what-brands-should-pay-for) process seamless, encouraging impulse purchases.
The rise of TikTok Shop has accelerated this shift. Instead of sending followers to external websites, creators can showcase products directly within their videos and livestreams, allowing users to discover, evaluate, and purchase without leaving the app. This significantly shortens the decision-making process. A product can move from trend to checkout in minutes, especially when supported by social proof in the comments and [real-time](https://influencity.com/blog/en/lifecycle-management-how-to-retain-rebook-reprice-influencers) interaction during live sessions.
For fashion brands, this means[influencers](https://influencity.com/blog/en/wellness-influencers-shaping-2026-new-years-health-fitness-resolutions) are no longer limited to [generating awareness](https://influencity.com/blog/en/from-awareness-to-order-mapping-the-buyers-journey-in-influencer-campaigns-the-taco-bell-way) or traffic. They are becoming integrated sales channels within the platforms where their audiences already spend time.
By blending fashion with lifestyle content—like beauty, wellness, and travel—these influencers create a sense of aspirational yet attainable living. Consumers are drawn to the [authenticity](https://influencity.com/blog/en/how-influencity-safeguards-brands-in-tiktok) and relatability of influencer-generated [content](https://influencity.com/blog/en/negotiating-influencer-content-rights), making them feel more connected to the products and brands being featured.

Traditional advertising, in contrast, often lacks this personal touch, which is why [influencer partnerships](https://influencity.com/blog/en/influencer-partnerships-what-mcdonalds-coca-cola-can-teach-you-about-ugc) yield [higher engagement](https://influencity.com/blog/en/instagram-engagement-rate-calculator-how-to-measure-your-influencer-campaign-performance) and more meaningful connections with audiences. ## Fashion Influencers You Should Know
The following list highlights 10 leading fashion influencers that brands [should consider collaborating with](https://influencity.com/blog/en/brand-safe-influencer-partnerships-data-protection). Each one brings a distinct aesthetic, from bold, statement-led styling to modern casual chic and color-driven looks.
Strong visual identities, consistent styling choices, and clear points of view make them valuable partners for brands looking to connect with specific audiences in an authentic way.
### Nicki Han
Niche: Soft, polished fashion with a romantic edge
If you’re looking for an influencer who blends softness with confidence, [Nicki Han](https://www.instagram.com/_nickihan/) is a strong fit. Her style revolves around clean silhouettes, delicate textures, and carefully curated outfits that feel intentional without looking forced.
Her content sits comfortably between elevated and approachable. She creates looks that feel refined and cohesive, but still wearable. The overall aesthetic is subtle, modern, and visually consistent, which makes her feed instantly recognizable.
For [brands](https://influencity.com/blog/en/social-media-talent-management-for-brands-and-agencies) positioning themselves in the soft luxury, contemporary chic, or aesthetic-driven space, she brings a presence that feels aspirational while remaining relatable.
### Leila Kashanipour
Niche: Bold, expressive fashion
If your brand leans toward strong silhouettes, confident styling, and looks that refuse to blend in, [Leila Kashanipour](https://www.instagram.com/leivankash/) brings exactly that energy. On [Instagram](https://influencity.com/blog/en/en/blog/en/the-instagram-algorithm-2025), her outfits feel intentional and high-impact, often built around sharp structures, striking contrasts, and pieces that naturally command attention.
Her content carries a subtle editorial feel, but it never feels distant. There’s personality in the way she layers, mixes textures, and plays with proportion. The result is fashion that feels self-assured and modern, without looking try-hard.
For brands that want to communicate individuality and a bold point of view, her presence turns a [simple collaboration](https://influencity.com/blog/en/ugc-price-how-to-price-ugc-collaborations) into something with attitude.
### Hadley Greene
Niche: Effortless style with playful pops of color
If your brand lives in that space between relaxed and elevated, [Hadley Greene](https://www.instagram.com/hadleyhgreene/) brings a really natural fit. Her outfits feel easy and wearable, but she often breaks up neutrals with unexpected touches of color that keep things interesting.
She might start with clean basics, structured denim, tailored pieces, and then add a bright knit, a bold accessory, or a saturated tone that shifts the whole mood. It never feels random. The color [works](https://influencity.com/blog/en/how-influencer-payment-automation-saves-agencies-hour) as a focal point rather than noise.
Her content feels approachable and modern, which makes it easy for [audiences](https://influencity.com/blog/en/defining-influencer-target-audiences-for-maximum-impact-how-nike-targets-the-right-influencer-audience) to picture themselves in the looks. For brands that want to stay polished but not predictable, that subtle play with color adds personality without losing versatility.
