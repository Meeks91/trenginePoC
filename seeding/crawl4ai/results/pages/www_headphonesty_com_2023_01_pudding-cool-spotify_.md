## What Is Pudding.cool’s ‘Judge My Music’ All About?
An example of Pudding.cool’s AI’s judgment on a user’s music taste.
**‘Judge My Music** ’ or ‘**How Bad Is Your Streaming Music?** ’ is an artificial intelligence (AI) bot that analyzes music data. Specifically, this bot looks at your artist, album, genre, and track data from streaming apps, and, based on insights from your data, roasts your taste in music.
The ‘Judge My Music’ AI bot was developed and trained by Mike Lacher and Matt Daniels of [The Pudding](https://pudding.cool) – a digital publication that creates visual essays backed by data. This AI was officially [launched in 2020 for Spotify](https://twitter.com/puddingviz/status/1341792142088417280?lang=en) but [updated in 2021 to include Apple Music](https://twitter.com/puddingviz/status/1466459071603691528?lang=en).
After giving the bot access to your account, you’ll be able to read its impressions regarding your music data in real time. You’ll find snide remarks on your music choice and condescending prompts, like, “Did you really listen to this song, like, ironically?” Or even “Are you okay?” after enumerating your top artists and albums.
Once the bot finishes analyzing your music data, it summarizes your music taste in one hyphenated phrase – for instance, “tay-tay-fangirl-cabincore-trendy-middle-part bad.” The bot also offers extra descriptors of your music taste using other hyphenated terms, such as “k-pop-for-breakfast bad” or “mason-jar-candle-from-target bad.”

## How Does ‘Judge My Music’ Work?
A list of the different music genres available on Spotify. (From: Unsplash)
Now, you might be wondering how the Pudding.cool AI calculates its results. How does it analyze your music data, and how does it know what’s good or bad?
Well, this AI bot doesn’t actually use real artificial intelligence to “judge” your music taste. Instead, its algorithm matches jokes from The Pudding’s database with insights from your music track data.
The AI’s verdict on your music taste comes from “over two million indicators of objectively good music” from which the bot is trained. And these include [Pitchfork reviews](https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/), [music discovery websites](https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/), [music news, reviews and commentaries](https://www.stereogum.com), obscure subreddits, and record store recommendations.
However, note that [the app can take forever to load](https://www.reddit.com/r/popheads/comments/kiheb4/how_bad_is_your_spotify_the_ai_reads_are_wonderful/), especially when multiple people use it simultaneously. Spotify also limits the number of users who can utilize the bot, which may add to your waiting time.
Also, because of how the app analyzes your data, the prompts are not entirely personalized from user to user. Indeed, some users have noticed [similar roasts and questions](https://www.reddit.com/r/popheads/comments/kiheb4/comment/ggqvctk/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=web2x&context=3), despite listening to different songs and artists.
## Is the Pudding’s ‘Judge My Music’ Safe?
A locked iMac that requires a password for access. (From: Unsplash)
Using The Pudding’s Judge My Music requires you to log in via Spotify or Apple, so it’s normal to fret about your data’s safety and privacy.
Luckily, The Pudding has taken many steps to ensure the safety and security of your data, as detailed on their [Privacy Policy page](https://pudding.cool/privacy/). Similarly, you can check their FAQs on data privacy at the lower-right side of the [_How Bad Is Your Streaming Music?_](https://pudding.cool/2021/10/judge-my-music/) page.
The Pudding assures users that their Spotify or Apple Music data are not saved. After logging in with your streaming account, the website creates a unique, secure one-time code to check your top songs, tracks, artists, and recent playlists.
This access is temporary, which means they won’t be able to access your data until you come back and log in again. Additionally, this also means no information regarding your username or password is shared with The Pudding.
### How to revoke app access from ‘Judge My Music’
However, if you’re still worried about your data safety and privacy, you can remove The Pudding’s access to your streaming accounts entirely.
On Spotify, you can revoke the bot’s access via [Spotify’s 3rd Party app page](https://www.spotify.com/us/account/apps/). Here’s how:
  1. Go to your Spotify account’s Third Party app page. Third Party app page.
  2. Look for **bad-music** , then click on the **Remove Access** button to its right. Remove Access for bad-music.
  3. You have successfully revoked The Pudding’s access to your account. Successfully revoked access for application.


For Apple Music users, logging out of your account is as easy as clicking [this link](https://pudding.cool/2021/10/judge-my-music/#:~:text=data%20questions%3F%20answers%20and%20privacy%20policy%20here.).
