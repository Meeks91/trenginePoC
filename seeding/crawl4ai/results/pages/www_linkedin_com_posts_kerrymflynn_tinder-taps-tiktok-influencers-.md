#  Kerry Flynn’s Post
NEW from me, featured in today's [#AxiosAM](https://www.linkedin.com/signup/cold-join?session_redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Ffeed%2Fhashtag%2Faxiosam&trk=public_post-text): Young people are finding dating apps more appealing after watching paid influencers on [TikTok](https://www.linkedin.com/company/tiktok?trk=public_post-text) share their experiences, per [Tinder](https://www.linkedin.com/company/tinder-incorporated?trk=public_post-text)'s recent brand sentiment study. 💡 The marketing strategy comes after reports that Gen Z and Millennials are not interested in swiping and are instead seeking in-person alternatives, negatively impacting Tinder parent [Match Group](https://www.linkedin.com/company/matchgroup?trk=public_post-text)'s business. 🗣️ Tinder CMO [Melissa Hobley](https://www.linkedin.com/in/melissahobley?trk=public_post-text) told me, "We know that you want an IRL meet cute. That's great. It's really hard for it to happen. Finding love on an app is so possible. It's really fun. It can lead to you finding someone great." 💕 [Madi B W.](https://www.linkedin.com/in/madibwebb?trk=public_post-text), who has 2.6 million followers on TikTok, told me she was excited to be a part of the campaign because when Tinder reached out she had recently gotten out of a relationship and saw it as a fun next step. One benefit she found in using dating apps is being able to publicly identify as queer. She met a woman through Tinder during the campaign. They are still dating.
