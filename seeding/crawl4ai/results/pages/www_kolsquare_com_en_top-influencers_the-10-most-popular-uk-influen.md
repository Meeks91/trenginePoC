## Identifying the most famous social media influencers: Our methodology
The Kolsquare database stores insights about influencers with 5,000 or more followers on at least one of the popular platforms, such as Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.We created our top 10 list by filtering the entire database for UK influencers and sorting by popularity. We then filtered the creators to show only those with at least 30% of their audience in the UK and good audience credibility.For each influencer, we have shared the most important social media metrics, including:
  * **Credibility score /100** : Kolsquare's[ credibility score](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/fake-influencers-how-to-identify-them) is rated out of 100. It tells you whether real people--not bots or fake followers--make up their follower count. We only included influencers with scores higher than 60 in our list. 
  * **Engagement rate** : This is one of the most well-known influencer marketing metrics. It tells you how often followers interact with the creator's content. The [engagement rate is](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/instagram-engagement-rate-definition-calculation-and-tips-to-boost-it) calculated by analysing how many of an influencer's followers interact with their posts through comments, shares, likes, etc.
  * **Earned media value (EMV):** EMV is the monetary value of the exposure gained by collaborating with a key opinion leader (KOL) on a platform in each format. 


We also listed their interests and follower counts on Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.

## What is the meaning of "influencer marketing"?
Influencer marketing is a strategy where brands collaborate with social media creators to promote products or services. The idea is simple: influencers use their credibility and reach to drive awareness, engagement, or sales for a brand. It’s a modern spin on celebrity partnerships. 
There are many different ways to work with influencers. Some brands, especially small businesses, prefer to collaborate with micro-influencers. These social media bloggers have fewer followers but deeper connections and operate in a niche.

##  **How** do influencers **work** with **brands**?
Influencers work with brands through partnerships that range from sponsored posts and product reviews to long-term ambassadorships. Typically, the brand provides the influencer with a product or service, which the influencer posts about in a way that feels authentic to their audience. 
Let's explore these collaborations with third parties in depth:
  * **Sponsored Content** : The most common type of influencer campaign where brands pay influencers to create posts or videos featuring their product or service. The goal can be to increase awareness, engagement, or sales through authentic content tailored to the influencer’s audience.
  * [**Affiliate Marketing**](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/kolsquare-guide-to-affiliate-marketing-with-influencers): Influencers earn a commission for every sale or action driven by their unique affiliate link or code. Affiliate campaigns allow brands to track conversions and ROI directly while leveraging influencers’ trust with their followers.
  * **Gifting Campaigns** : Brands provide influencers with free products or services in exchange for reviews. Here, brands aim to generate organic buzz and reach without formal payment agreements. [_Discover how gifting combines with affiliate campaigns for maximum impact here_](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/the-power-duo-gifting-and-affiliate-marketing-in-influencer-campaigns). 
  * **Brand Ambassadorships** : Long-term partnerships where influencers become “faces” of a brand, consistently promoting it over time. These campaigns build credibility and sustained visibility through repeated, authentic endorsements.
  * **User-Generated Content (**[**UGC**](https://www.kolsquare.com/en/blog/understanding-the-mechanisms-of-user-generated-content)**) & Events**: Influencers create or share content for brand-owned platforms (e.g., Instagram ads) or participate in events like product launches or live streams. It allows brands to increase community engagement as people attend the event and share blog posts and social media content.
