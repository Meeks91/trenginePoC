### Customize your Midjourney profile and follow other creators!
Welcome to your Profile page—your own creative corner of midjourney.com! This is what other members see when they click your name on the Explore page or open your shareable profile link.
You can customize it with a unique username, display name, a profile and banner image or video, a short bio, links to your socials, and spotlight your favorite creations.
Profiles are visible only to logged-in Midjourney members.
## Customize Your Profile 
To view your Profile page, log in to midjourney.com and click your display name in the bottom left corner of the website. You can also use the 3 dots icon next to your display name and select Profile Page.
Your profile has three main sections:
  * **Spotlight** – Showcase your favorite creations.
  * **Archive** – Browse all of your public creations.
  * **Following** – See the people you follow. This list is private and visible only to you.


Click the pencil icon in your banner, next to your display name to edit and customize your profile.
**Unique Username** Set a unique, public username that will be used in your profile URL.
**Display Name** Your display name will be used on the Explore Page, in the file names of your downloaded images and videos, and does not have to be unique.
**Bio** Use this space to tell people about yourself. You can use up to 1,000 characters.
**Social Profiles** Add URLs to your Twitter, Instagram, Pinterest, and YouTube accounts.
Use the "Update" button to save your changes.

  * [ Pick Your Banner Image ](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/41117938447629-Profiles#zp-1-1)


Click the pencil icon on your profile picture to select or generate a new one.
Profile pictures can be images or videos, but they must be square (1:1 aspect ratio) and use a [Personalization profile](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32433330574221-Personalization), a [moodboard](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/39193335040013-Moodboards), or a [Style Reference](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32180011136653). To use a video, animate an image from your gallery that meets these requirements. Currently only V7 and Niji 6 creations can be used.
By default, all eligible creations from your gallery appear as options. You can filter these by liked images, Personalization profiles, or moodboards using the menu on the right. Click an image or video to enlarge it, then select "Choose Image" to set it as your profile picture.
To create something new, click "Generate New Image." This will automatically run the prompt `My favorite character ever, named [your display name] --s 1000`. Use the menu on the right to select which Personalization profile or moodboard to apply. If you don't have any Personalization profiles or moodboards set up, the prompt will use `--sref random`.
Note: Generating new images for your profile will use your [GPU time](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32016412137741-GPU-Speed-Fast-Relax-Turbo).
You can also right-click a qualifying image or video from your Create or Organize page and use the More menu to set it as your profile image.
Click the pencil icon on your banner to select or generate a new one.
Banners can be images or videos, but they must be 11:3 aspect ratio and use a [Personalization profile](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32433330574221-Personalization), a [moodboard](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/39193335040013-Moodboards), or a [Style Reference](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32180011136653). To use a video, animate an image from your gallery that meets these requirements. Currently only V7 and Niji 6 creations can be used.
By default, all eligible creations from your gallery appear as options. You can filter these by liked images, Personalization profiles, or moodboards using the menu on the right. Click an image or video to enlarge it, then select "Choose Image" to set it as your banner.
To create something new, click "Generate New Image." This will automatically run the prompt `My favorite place ever --s 1000`. Use the menu on the right to select which Personalization profile or moodboard to apply. If you don't have any Personalization profiles or moodboards set up, the prompt will use `--sref random`.
Note: Generating new images for your profile will use your [GPU time](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32016412137741-GPU-Speed-Fast-Relax-Turbo).
You can also right-click a qualifying image or video from your Create or Organize page and use the More menu to set it as your banner.
Choosing a [private](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32019750070669-Stealth-Mode) image or video for your profile, banner, or to spotlight will automatically make it public, along with the other images/videos from the same grid. To avoid making the other images public, use an upscaled image instead.
## Spotlight Your Favorite Creations 
The Spotlight tab on your profile is your place to curate and showcase your favorite Midjourney creations.
**To spotlight a creation:**
  * Open it fullscreen and use the spotlight icon above the prompt text
  * Use the More Options icon and select the Spotlight Image button
  * Right-click and select the Spotlight Image button
  * Hover your mouse over a creation on your Profile page and use the spotlight icon 


## Your Archive 
The Archive tab on your profile displays all of your public Midjourney creations. [Private](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/32019750070669) creations and [trashed](https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/33329462451469-Organizing-Your-Creations#h_01JHP66YYRKF988ABQBHXMFJ3K) creations are not visible here.
## Follow Other Midjourney Creators 
When you follow other people, their profiles are saved in your "Following" tab on your profile for quick access. The list of people you follow is private and visible only to you.
To follow someone, go to their Profile page and click the Follow button under their banner. If you find them on the Explore page, click the follow icon next to their display name. 
Midjourney keeps follower lists and counts hidden, helping everyone focus on sharing and exploring art.
