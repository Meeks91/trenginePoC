##  Did you hear about Google’s Latest AI Creative Push?
Early this week, [Google announced new AI-powered creative](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=https%3A%2F%2Fblog%2Egoogle%2Fproducts%2Fads-commerce%2Fnew-creative-updates-advertisers-generate-lifestyle%2F&urlhash=x_RO&trk=article-ssr-frontend-pulse_little-text-block) capabilities in Google Ads, including AI-generated images with human characters, asset-audience recommendations, and automated testing for Performance Max campaigns. The AI system includes safeguards, preventing the generation of politicians, celebrities, minors, or sensitive content, and tagging AI-generated images with SynthID.
This got me thinking about the broader AI shift across major ad platforms we use in the digital advertising industry. Every major player is embedding AI into creative workflows, but they are doing it differently. This article breaks down how Google, Meta, TikTok, and LinkedIn are leveraging generative AI and what it means for marketers navigating this evolving space.
##  Google Ads: The Automation Powerhouse
Google’s AI strategy integrates campaign management across Search, Display, YouTube, Shopping, and App campaigns. The focus? Scalability and automation helping advertisers generate assets that perform, even if they lack originality.
What It Offers:
  * Imagen 3 AI-powered image generation, now with human characters.
  * Performance Max creative automation, tailoring assets across Google’s ecosystem.
  * Asset-audience recommendations, revealing which visuals connect best with key audiences.
  * Automated A/B testing for feed-only Performance Max campaigns.


Where It Wins:
  * Full automation across multiple Google platforms.
  * Data-backed creative suggestions that refine performance over time.
  * Deep integration within Google’s advertising stack.


Where It Falls Short:
  * Creativity takes a back seat, AI optimises for engagement, not differentiation.
  * Google-first mindset, with no cross-platform alignment.


##  Meta: The Engagement Machine
Meta’s AI fine-tunes visuals and copy to maximise engagement across Facebook, Instagram, and Messenger. Unlike Google, it doesn’t fully automate campaigns, but it helps brands make content more interactive and effective.
What It Offers:
  * AI-powered image resizing and background tweaks.
  * Video expansion that adjusts length dynamically for Stories and Reels.
  * Image animation that brings static assets to life.
  * AI-driven ad copy that mirrors brand tone and voice.


Where It Wins:
  * Smart, engagement-driven creative tweaks.
  * Strong visual and video manipulation tools.
  * AI personalisation that aligns with user behaviour.
  * Advantage+ shopping campaigns saw 70% YoY growth in Q4,  annual revenue run rate.


Where It Falls Short:
  * No full campaign automation, human oversight required.
  * Locked into Meta’s ecosystem, with no broader platform connectivity.


##  TikTok: The AI-Generated Video Factory
TikTok’s AI is designed for one thing: turning ideas into video content at scale. Instead of focusing on asset optimisation like Google or Meta, TikTok’s AI acts like a full content production studio.
What It Offers:
  * AI-generated video ads based on text prompts.
  * Automated script writing that aligns with TikTok’s fast-paced format.
  * AI-created characters for brands that want to experiment with virtual personas.
  * A partnership with Getty Images for licensed, high-quality content.
  * Smart+ launched in October 2024 as TikTok’s answer to Google’s Performance Max and Meta’s Advantage+.
  * Symphony Creative Studio enables video generation from product information or URLs.


Where It Wins:
  * Best-in-class AI for video production.
  * Reduces dependency on traditional video teams.
  * Gives advertisers flexibility on AI involvement.


Where It Falls Short:
  * TikTok-only focus, AI-generated content isn’t optimised for other platforms.
  * No cross-platform creative automation.


##  LinkedIn: AI for B2B Precision, Not Production
LinkedIn isn’t using AI to create flashy ads. It’s focused on getting the right B2B message to the right audience at the right time. While it lacks creative execution tools, it’s using AI to make targeting and personalisation sharper.
What It Offers:
  * AI-powered audience segmentation that adjusts in real-time.
  * Interactive ad formats, including polls and event-based promotions.
  * AI-driven sales messaging for LinkedIn’s outreach tools.
  * "Accelerate" AI tool, launched in October 2023, reached 50% global rollout by mid-2024, reducing campaign creation time from 15 hours to 5 minutes.
  * Microsoft Designer integration for AI-driven creative customisation.
  * The tool can analyse websites and company LinkedIn pages to build campaigns.


Where It Wins:
  * Best AI targeting for B2B advertisers.
  * Strong personalisation that helps with lead conversion.
  * Deep integration with LinkedIn’s professional network.


Where It Falls Short:
  * No advanced AI creative tools, ads remain mostly static.
  * Focused only on LinkedIn, making multi-platform strategies difficult.


##  Platform Comparisons
Each platform is embedding AI into advertising, but their priorities differ. Google is optimising for full-scale automation, allowing AI to manage creativity across its entire ecosystem. Meta focuses on engagement-driven AI, refining visuals and personalisation within its walled garden. TikTok is pushing AI-powered video creation, streamlining content production but limiting cross-platform flexibility. LinkedIn is leveraging AI for precision targeting and enhancing B2B campaigns but lacks advanced creative tools. Understanding these differences is crucial for brands managing campaigns across multiple platforms.
##  Where Is "Embedded AI Creative" Heading?
AI is becoming deeply embedded in advertising, but each platform takes a different approach. Google is driving full campaign automation, Meta is enhancing engagement-focused creative, TikTok is refining AI-driven video production, and LinkedIn is advancing B2B targeting and personalisation.
Despite these advancements, AI is not a replacement for a strong, unified creative strategy. The most effective campaigns require consistent, well-executed creative across multiple platforms. AI can scale production, optimise performance, and speed up testing, but brand identity, messaging, and execution still need human oversight. The best advertisers will use AI as a tool to enhance creativity, not replace it, ensuring that campaigns remain impactful and strategically aligned.
