## What 9,000 Listeners Told Deezer About AI
AI music is increasingly hard to distinguish from the real deal. (From: Deezer)
Ipsos [surveyed 9,000 people across eight countries](https://newsroom-deezer.com/2025/11/deezer-ipsos-survey-ai-music/) between October 6 and October 10, 2025. Participants listened to three tracks, one of them fully AI-generated, and were asked to identify the synthetic song. Only 3% succeeded.
Most listeners were caught off guard:
  * 71% said the results surprised them.
  * 52% felt uncomfortable realizing they couldn’t distinguish AI from human-made music.
  * 55% said their first reaction toward AI was curiosity, while only 19% listed trust among their initial feelings.


Those emotions spill over into how people imagine AI’s role in creativity.
For instance, half of the respondents (51%) think AI will play a major role in music creation over the next decade. An equal number shares worries that it will flood streaming platforms with low-quality, generic tracks.
Nearly 64% also fear a broader loss of creativity if synthetic music continues to scale unchecked.
At the same time, listeners want clear guardrails and transparency:
  * 80% believe fully that AI-generated music should be clearly labeled.
  * 73% want to know if AI tracks are recommended by their streaming platform.
  * 52% think synthetic songs shouldn’t appear in the same charts as human-created music.


Under all this is concern for working artists. Deezer now receives 50,000 AI-generated submissions per day, making up 34% of all daily uploads.
Even so, Deezer says fully AI-generated tracks still account for only about 0.5% of total streams. Most of those plays are flagged as fraudulent and removed from recommendations and editorial playlists.”
To date, Deezer remains the only major streaming platform to detect [and explicitly tag fully synthetic content](https://support.deezer.com/hc/en-gb/articles/28222522835101-AI-Content-Tagging-on-Deezer).
## How Synthetic Tracks Perform in the Charts
More than 1,000 UK artists banded together for a silent protest album. (From: Is This What We Want?)
Even with confusion, skepticism, and low overall engagement, AI-generated songs are still [breaking into mainstream rankings](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/nov/13/ai-music-spotify-billboard-charts).
A huge example of that is how the two songs by Breaking Rust, “[ _Walk My Walk_](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OU71XDWYeIk)” and “[ _Livin’ on Borrowed Time_](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bUnZyUuCK-Q),” topped Spotify’s U.S. Viral 50 chart. _Walk My Walk_ ” also led Billboard’s Country Digital Song Sales chart for three weeks.
At the same time, the Dutch song “ _We Say No, No, No to an Asylum Center_ ” reached No. 1 on Spotify’s global Viral 50 before abruptly disappearing from both Spotify and YouTube.
The artist, who goes by Broken Veteran, defended AI as a tool that gives “just another tool for expression, particularly valuable for people like me who have something to say but lack traditional musical training.”
### What is driving their success?
Platforms like [DistroKid](https://distrokid.com/product/distrokid/plans-and-pricing-2?), [Amuse](https://www.amuse.io/en/), [Landr](https://www.landr.com/ai-music-tools-and-music-distribution), and [CDBaby](https://cdbaby.com/) allow anyone to upload AI-generated tracks to music streaming services. This distribution ecosystem is built to scale synthetic music at unprecedented speed.
Also, within AI creator communities, influencers openly promote these tools as ways to build “streams of passive income,” according to The Guardian.
## The Industry’s Response to the Rise of AI
Reactions from the music business are increasingly divided between working with AI and pushing back against it.
On one side, Universal Music Group recently settled its lawsuit against AI startup Udio. Simultaneously, it announced plans to co-launch a [commercial platform for licensed AI music in 2026](https://www.techradar.com/ai-platforms-assistants/chatgpt/ai-music-platform-udio-and-universal-music-strike-a-deal-is-this-ai-musics-spotify-moment). It’s a clear sign that major labels see opportunity despite earlier backlash.
On the other side, artists are staging symbolic protests. Paul McCartney is releasing a nearly silent track as part of [the anti-AI album ](https://www.isthiswhatwewant.com/)[_Is This What We Want?_](https://www.isthiswhatwewant.com/) The record also features contributions from Kate Bush, Hans Zimmer, and Pet Shop Boys.
The tension is clear. While AI promises innovation and accessibility, it raises concerns about exploitation, fairness, and the very meaning of authorship.
